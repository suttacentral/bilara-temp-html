Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pāṭidesanīyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing acknowledgment

2. Dutiyapāṭidesanīyasikkhāpada 2. The second training rule on offenses entailing acknowledgment

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. At one time the Buddha was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrel sanctuary. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū kulesu nimantitā bhuñjanti. At that time families were inviting monks to meals, Chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vosāsantiyo ṭhitā honti— and the nuns from the group of six were directing people toward the monks from the group of six, saying, “idha sūpaṃ detha, idha odanaṃ dethā”ti. “Give curries here; give rice here.” Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yāvadatthaṃ bhuñjanti. The monks from the group of six ate as much as they wanted, Aññe bhikkhū na cittarūpaṃ bhuñjanti. but other monks did not eat as much as they had intended.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo vosāsantiyo na nivāressantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six not restrain the nuns from giving directions?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo vosāsantiyo na nivārethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you didn’t do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhuniyo vosāsantiyo na nivāressatha. Foolish men, how can you not restrain the nuns from giving directions? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Bhikkhū paneva kulesu nimantitā bhuñjanti, tatra ce sā bhikkhunī vosāsamānarūpā ṭhitā hoti—‘idha sūpaṃ detha, idha odanaṃ dethā’ti, tehi bhikkhūhi sā bhikkhunī apasādetabbā— ‘apasakka tāva, bhagini, yāva bhikkhū bhuñjantī’ti. Ekassa cepi bhikkhuno na paṭibhāseyya taṃ bhikkhuniṃ apasādetuṃ— ‘apasakka tāva, bhagini, yāva bhikkhū bhuñjantī’ti paṭidesetabbaṃ tehi bhikkhūhi— ‘When monks eat at invitations to families, if a nun is there giving directions, saying, “Give bean curry here; give rice here,” then those monks should stop her: “Stop, Sister, while the monks are eating.” If not even a single monk addresses that nun in this way to stop her, they must acknowledge it: ‘gārayhaṃ, āvuso, dhammaṃ āpajjimhā asappāyaṃ pāṭidesanīyaṃ, taṃ paṭidesemā’”ti. “We have done a blameworthy and unsuitable thing which is to be acknowledged. We acknowledge it.”’”

Definitions

Bhikkhū paneva kulesu nimantitā bhuñjantīti When monks eat at invitations to families:
kulaṃ nāma cattāri kulāni— khattiyakulaṃ, brāhmaṇakulaṃ, vessakulaṃ, suddakulaṃ. there are four kinds of families called families: aristocratic families, brahmin families, merchant families, worker families.
Nimantitā bhuñjantīti Eat at invitations:
pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññatarena bhojanena nimantitā bhuñjanti. eat any of the five staple foods at invitations.
Bhikkhunī nāma A nun:
ubhatosaṃghe upasampannā. she has been given the full ordination by both Orders.
Vosāsantī nāma Giving directions:
yathāmittatā yathāsandiṭṭhatā yathāsambhattatā yathāsamānupajjhāyakatā yathāsamānācariyakatā— “idha sūpaṃ detha, idha odanaṃ dethā”ti. Esā vosāsantī nāma. saying, “Give bean curry here; give rice here,” according to friendship, according to companionship, according to who one is devoted to, according to having the same preceptor, according to having the same teacher— this is called “giving directions.”
Tehi bhikkhūhīti Those monks:
bhuñjamānehi bhikkhūhi. the monks who are eating.
Sā bhikkhunīti Her:
yā sā vosāsantī bhikkhunī. the nun who is giving directions.
Tehi bhikkhūhi sā bhikkhunī apasādetabbā— “apasakka tāva, bhagini, yāva bhikkhū bhuñjantī”ti. Those monks should stop her: ‘Stop, Sister, while the monks are eating’:
Ekassa cepi bhikkhuno anapasādito— “khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī”ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. if she is not stopped by even one monk, and a monk then receives food with the intention of eating it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. For every mouthful, he commits an offense entailing acknowledgment.

Permutations

Upasampannāya upasampannasaññī vosāsantiyā na nivāreti, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is fully ordained, and he perceives her as fully ordained, and he does not restrain her from giving directions, he commits an offense entailing acknowledgment. Upasampannāya vematiko vosāsantiyā na nivāreti, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is fully ordained, but he is unsure if she is, and he does not restrain her from giving directions, he commits an offense entailing acknowledgment. Upasampannāya anupasampannasaññī vosāsantiyā na nivāreti, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is fully ordained, but he does not perceive her as fully ordained, and he does not restrain her from giving directions, he commits an offense entailing acknowledgment.

Ekatoupasampannāya vosāsantiyā na nivāreti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he does not restrain a nun who is fully ordained only on one side from giving directions, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not fully ordained, but he perceives her as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not fully ordained, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya anupasampannasaññī, anāpatti. If she is not fully ordained, and he does not perceive her as fully ordained, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: attano bhattaṃ dāpeti na deti, if a nun gets someone else to give her own food, and she does not give it herself; aññesaṃ bhattaṃ deti na dāpeti, if a nun gives someone else’s food, and she does not get anyone to give it; yaṃ na dinnaṃ taṃ dāpeti, if a nun gets someone to give what has not been given; yattha na dinnaṃ tattha dāpeti, if a nun gets someone to give to someone who has not received anything; sabbesaṃ samakaṃ dāpeti, if a nun gets someone to give equally to all; sikkhamānā vosāsati, if a trainee nun is giving directions; sāmaṇerī vosāsati, if a novice nun is giving directions; pañca bhojanāni ṭhapetvā sabbattha, anāpatti, if it is anything apart from the five kinds of staple food; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Dutiyapāṭidesanīyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second training rule entailing acknowledgment is finished.
