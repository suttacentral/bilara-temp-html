Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Ratanavagga The sub-chapter on precious things

89. Nisīdanasikkhāpada 89. The training rule on sitting-mats

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ nisīdanaṃ anuññātaṃ hoti. At that time the Master had allowed the sitting-mat for the monks. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū— “bhagavatā nisīdanaṃ anuññātan”ti appamāṇikāni nisīdanāni dhārenti. Knowing this, the monks from the group of six used over-sized sitting-mats. Mañcassapi pīṭhassapi puratopi pacchatopi olambenti. They let them hang down from beds and benches, both in front and behind.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū appamāṇikāni nisīdanāni dhāressantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six use over-sized sitting-mats?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, appamāṇikāni nisīdanāni dhārethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, appamāṇikāni nisīdanāni dhāressatha. Foolish men, how can you use over-sized sitting-mats? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

“Nisīdanaṃ pana bhikkhunā kārayamānena pamāṇikaṃ kāretabbaṃ. ‘If a monk is getting a sitting-mat made, it is to be made to the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ—dīghaso dve vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ diyaḍḍhaṃ. This is the right size: 50 centimeters long and 37.5 centimeters wide. Taṃ atikkāmayato chedanakaṃ pācittiyan”ti. If it exceeds that, it is to be cut down, and he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī mahākāyo hoti. At that time Venerable Udāyī was big. So bhagavato purato nisīdanaṃ paññapetvā samantato samañchamāno nisīdati. After putting out his sitting-mat in front of the Master, he stretched it on all sides before sitting down. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca— The Master asked him, “kissa tvaṃ, udāyi, nisīdanaṃ samantato samañchasi; “Udāyī, why are you stretching the sitting-mat on all sides, seyyathāpi purāṇāsikoṭṭho”ti? as if it was an old hide?”

“Tathā hi pana, bhante, bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ atikhuddakaṃ nisīdanaṃ anuññātan”ti. “It is, Venerable Sir, because the sitting-mat you’ve allowed for the monks is too small.”

Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards the Master gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “anujānāmi, bhikkhave, nisīdanassa dasaṃ vidatthiṃ. “Monks, I allow a border of 25 centimeters for the sitting-mat.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Nisīdanaṃ pana bhikkhunā kārayamānena pamāṇikaṃ kāretabbaṃ. ‘If a monk is getting a sitting-mat made, it should be made to the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ—dīghaso dve vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ diyaḍḍhaṃ. Dasā vidatthi. This is the right size: 50 centimeters long and 37.5 centimeters wide, and a border of 25 centimeters. Taṃ atikkāmayato chedanakaṃ pācittiyan”ti. If it exceeds that, it is to be cut down, and he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Nisīdanaṃ nāma A sitting-mat:
sadasaṃ vuccati. one with a border is what is meant.
Kārayamānenāti Is getting […] made:
karonto vā kārāpento vā pamāṇikaṃ kāretabbaṃ. making it himself or getting someone else to make it, it should be made to the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ— dīghaso dve vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ diyaḍḍhaṃ. Dasā vidatthi. This is the right size: 50 centimeters long and 37.5 centimeters wide, and a border of 25 centimeters. Taṃ atikkāmetvā karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If he makes one or gets one made that exceeds that, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena chinditvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ. When he obtains it, it is to be cut down, and he is then to confess an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he makes one or gets one made for the benefit of someone else, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he obtains one that was made by someone else and then uses it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pamāṇikaṃ karoti, if he makes it the right size; ūnakaṃ karoti, if he makes it less than the right size; aññena kataṃ pamāṇātikkantaṃ paṭilabhitvā chinditvā paribhuñjati, if he obtains one made by another that exceeds the right size and he cuts it down before using it; vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, if he makes a canopy, a floor cover, a cloth screen, a mattress, or a pillow; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Nisīdanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ. The seventh rule, the training rule on sitting-mats, is finished.
