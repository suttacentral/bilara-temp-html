Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

82. Pariṇāmanasikkhāpada 82. The training rule on diverting

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññatarassa pūgassa saṃghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ hoti— At that time an association had prepared a meal together with robe-cloth for the Order. They said, “bhojetvā cīvarena acchādessāmā”ti. “After giving the food, we’ll offer the robe-cloth.”

Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena so pūgo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; But the monks from the group of six went to that association upasaṅkamitvā taṃ pūgaṃ etadavocuṃ— and said, “dethāvuso, imāni cīvarāni imesaṃ bhikkhūnan”ti. “Sirs, give this robe-cloth to these monks.”

“Na mayaṃ, bhante, dassāma. “Venerables, we can’t do that. Amhākaṃ saṃghassa anuvassaṃ sacīvarabhikkhā paññattā”ti. We’ve prepared our annual alms-offering together with robe-cloth for the Order.”

“Bahū, āvuso, saṃghassa dāyakā, bahū saṃghassa bhattā. “The Order has many donors and supporters. Ime tumhe nissāya tumhe sampassantā idha viharanti. But since these monks are staying here, they depend on you and look to you for support. Tumhe ce imesaṃ na dassatha, atha ko carahi imesaṃ dassati? If you don’t give to them, who will? Dethāvuso, imāni cīvarāni imesaṃ bhikkhūnan”ti. So give them this robe-cloth.” Atha kho so pūgo chabbaggiyehi bhikkhūhi nippīḷiyamāno yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ datvā saṃghaṃ bhattena parivisi. Being pressured by the monks from the group of six, that association gave the prepared robe-cloth to those other monks from the group of six and served the food to the Order.

Ye te bhikkhū jānanti saṃghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ “na ca jānanti chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ dinnan”ti te evamāhaṃsu— The monks who knew that a meal together with robe-cloth had been prepared for the Order, but not that the robe-cloth had been given to those monks from the group six, said, “oṇojethāvuso, saṃghassa cīvaran”ti. “You may offer the robe-cloth.”

“Natthi, bhante. “Venerables, there isn’t any. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ ayyā chabbaggiyā ayyānaṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ pariṇāmesun”ti. The robe-cloth we had prepared were diverted by the monks from the group of six to other monks from the group of six.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmessantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six divert to an individual things they know were intended for the Order?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmessatha. Foolish men, how can you divert to an individual things that you know were intended for the Order? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk diverts to an individual something that he knows was intended for the Order, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Jānāti nāma He knows:
sāmaṃ vā jānāti, aññe vā tassa ārocenti, so vā āroceti. he knows by himself or others have told him or the donor has told him.
Saṃghikaṃ nāma For the Order:
saṃghassa dinnaṃ hoti pariccattaṃ. given to the Order, given up to the Order.
Lābho nāma Something:
cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā, antamaso cuṇṇapiṇḍopi, dantakaṭṭhampi, dasikasuttampi. robe-cloth, almsfood, a dwelling, and medicine; even just bath powder, a toothbrush, or a piece of string.
Pariṇataṃ nāma Intended:
“dassāma karissāmā”ti vācā bhinnā hoti, taṃ puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if they have said, “We will give/we will prepare,” and he diverts it to an individual, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Pariṇate pariṇatasaññī puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is intended for the Order and he perceives it as such, and he diverts it to an individual, he commits an offense entailing confession. Pariṇate vematiko puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for the Order, but he is unsure if it is, and he diverts it to an individual, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Pariṇate apariṇatasaññī puggalassa pariṇāmeti, anāpatti. If it is intended for the Order, but he does not perceives it as such, and he diverts it to an individual, there is no offense.

Saṃghassa pariṇataṃ aññasaṃghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for one Order and he diverts it to another Order or to a shrine, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Cetiyassa pariṇataṃ aññacetiyassa vā saṃghassa vā puggalassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for one shrine and he diverts it to another shrine or to a Order or to an individual, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Puggalassa pariṇataṃ aññapuggalassa vā saṃghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for an individual and he diverts it to another individual or to a Order or to a shrine, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Apariṇate pariṇatasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not intended for the Order, but he perceives it as intended for the Order, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apariṇate vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not intended for the Order, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apariṇate apariṇatasaññī, anāpatti. If it is not intended for the Order and he does not perceive it as such, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: “kattha demā”ti pucchīyamāno— if being asked, “yattha tumhākaṃ deyyadhammo paribhogaṃ vā labheyya paṭisaṅkhāraṃ vā labheyya ciraṭṭhitiko vā assa yattha vā pana tumhākaṃ cittaṃ pasīdati tattha dethā”ti bhaṇati, “Where may we give?” he says, “Give where your gift goes toward equipment/goes toward repairs/where it will last for a long time/where you feel inspired;” ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Pariṇāmanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dvādasamaṃ. The twelfth rule, the training rule on diverting, is finished.

Sahadhammikavaggo aṭṭhamo. The eighth sub-chapter on legitimately is finished.

Tassuddānaṃ This is the summary:

Sahadhamma vivaṇṇañca, Legitimately, and disparaging,
mohāpanaṃ pahārakaṃ; Deception, striking;
Talasatti amūlañca, Threatening with the palm of the hand, and groundlessly,
sañcicca ca upassuti; And intentionally, eavesdropping;
Paṭibāhana chandañca, Blocking, and consent,
dabbañca pariṇāmananti. And Dabba, diverting.
