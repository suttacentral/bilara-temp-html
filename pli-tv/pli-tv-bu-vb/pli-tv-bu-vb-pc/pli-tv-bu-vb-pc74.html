Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

74. Pahārasikkhāpada 74. The training rule on striking

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā sattarasavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pahāraṃ denti. At that time the monks from the group of six struck monks from the group of seventeen in anger. Te rodanti. They cried. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu— Other monks asked them why, “kissa tumhe, āvuso, rodathā”ti? “Ime, āvuso, chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā amhākaṃ pahāraṃ dentī”ti. and they told them what had happened.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā bhikkhūnaṃ pahāraṃ dassantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six strike other monks in anger?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, kupitā anattamanā bhikkhūnaṃ pahāraṃ dethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, kupitā anattamanā bhikkhūnaṃ pahāraṃ dassatha. Foolish men, how can you strike other monks in anger? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa kupito anattamano pahāraṃ dadeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk strikes another monk in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Bhikkhussāti Another monk:
aññassa bhikkhussa. a different monk.
Kupito anattamanoti In anger:
anabhiraddho āhatacitto khilajāto. discontent, having hatred, hostile.
Pahāraṃ dadeyyāti Strikes:
kāyena vā kāyapaṭibaddhena vā nissaggiyena vā antamaso uppalapattenapi pahāraṃ deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he strikes with his body, with something connected to his body, or with something released, even if only with a lotus leaf, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampanne upasampannasaññī kupito anattamano pahāraṃ deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, and he perceives him as fully ordained, and he strikes him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne vematiko kupito anattamano pahāraṃ deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he is unsure if he is, and he strikes him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī kupito anattamano pahāraṃ deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he does not perceive him as fully ordained, and he strikes him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannassa kupito anattamano pahāraṃ deti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he strikes one who is not fully ordained in anger, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he perceives him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, and he does not perceive him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: kenaci viheṭhīyamāno mokkhādhippāyo pahāraṃ deti, if he strikes in self-defence; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Pahārasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ. The fourth rule, the training rule on striking, is finished.
