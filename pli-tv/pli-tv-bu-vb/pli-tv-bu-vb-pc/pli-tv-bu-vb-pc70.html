70. Kaṇṭakasikkhāpada 70. The training rule on Kaṇṭaka

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena kaṇṭakassa nāma samaṇuddesassa evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ hoti— At that time the novice monk Kaṇṭaka had taken the following bad and erroneous view: “tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā”ti. “As I understand the teachings of the Master, the things he calls obstructive are not able to obstruct one who indulges in them.”

Assosuṃ kho sambahulā bhikkhū kaṇṭakassa nāma kira samaṇuddesassa evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ— A number of monks heard that the novice monk Kaṇṭaka had taken that view. “tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā”ti. Atha kho te bhikkhū yena kaṇṭako samaṇuddeso tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ etadavocuṃ— They went to him and asked, “saccaṃ kira te, āvuso kaṇṭaka, evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ— “Is it true, Kaṇṭaka, that you have taken such a view?” ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’”ti?

“Evaṃ byā kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi— ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’”ti. “Yes, indeed. As I understand the Master’s Teaching, the things he calls obstructive are not able to obstruct one who indulges in them.”

“Mā, āvuso kaṇṭaka, evaṃ avaca. Mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi. Na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ. Na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya. “No, Kaṇṭaka, don’t misrepresent the Master, for it’s not good to misrepresent him. The Master would never say such a thing. Anekapariyāyenāvuso kaṇṭaka, antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāya. The Master has given many discourses about the obstructive things being obstructive and how they obstruct one who indulges in them. Appassādā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo … pe … The Master has said that there’s little enjoyment in sensual objects, but much suffering and much trouble: the danger in them is greater. … ” evampi kho kaṇṭako samaṇuddeso tehi bhikkhūhi vuccamāno tatheva taṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ thāmasā parāmāsā abhinivissa voharati— But even though the monks spoke to Kaṇṭaka in this way, he stubbornly held on to that bad and erroneous view, and he continued to insist on it. “evaṃ byā kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi— ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’”ti.

Yato ca kho te bhikkhū nāsakkhiṃsu kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ etasmā pāpakā diṭṭhigatā vivecetuṃ, atha kho te bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Since those monks were not able to make him give up that view, they approached the Master and told him what had happened. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṃghaṃ sannipātāpetvā kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ paṭipucchi— The Master then had the Order of monks assembled and questioned the novice monk Kaṇṭaka, “saccaṃ kira te, kaṇṭaka, evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ— “Is it true, Kaṇṭaka, that you have taken a bad and erroneous view such as this: ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’”ti? ‘As I understand the teachings of the Master, the things he calls obstructive are not able to obstruct one who indulges in them’?”

“Evaṃ byā kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi— ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’”ti. “Yes indeed, Venerable Sir.”

“Kassa nu kho nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mayā evaṃ dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāsi? “Foolish man, who do you think I have taught in this way? Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā, alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāya? Haven’t I given many discourses about the obstructive things being obstructive and how they obstruct one who indulges in them? Appassādā kāmā vuttā mayā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. I have said that there is little enjoyment in sensual objects, but much suffering and much trouble, and that the danger in them is greater. Aṭṭhikaṅkalūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā … pe … I have said that sensual objects are similar to a skeleton … sappasirūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. similar to a snake’s head; they are much suffering and much trouble, and the danger in them is greater. Atha ca pana tvaṃ, moghapurisa, attanā duggahitena amhe ceva abbhācikkhasi attānañca khaṇasi bahuñca apuññaṃ pasavasi. And yet, foolish man, by misunderstanding you have misrepresented me, hurt yourself, and made much demerit. Tañhi te, moghapurisa, bhavissati dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāya. This will be for your long-lasting harm and suffering.

Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … pasannānañca ekaccānaṃ aññathattāyā”ti. and it will cause some with confidence to change their minds.”

Vigarahitvā … pe … Having criticized him … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— the Master gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “tena hi, bhikkhave, saṃgho kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ nāsetu. “Well then, monks, let the Order expel the novice monk Kaṇṭaka. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nāsetabbo— And this is how he should be expelled: ‘ajjatagge te, āvuso kaṇṭaka, na ceva so bhagavā satthā apadisitabbo. ‘From today, Kaṇṭaka, you may not refer to the Master as your teacher. Yampi caññe samaṇuddesā labhanti bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ dirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ sāpi te natthi. And you can no longer share a sleeping place with the monks for two or three nights, as can other novice monks. Cara pire vinassā’”ti. Go; away with you!’” Atha kho saṃgho kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ nāsesi. The Order then expelled Kaṇṭaka.

Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ tathānāsitaṃ kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ upalāpentipi upaṭṭhāpentipi sambhuñjantipi sahāpi seyyaṃ kappenti. Soon afterwards the monks from the group of six supported Kaṇṭaka, and they were attended on by him, lived with him, and shared a sleeping place with him. Yet they knew that he had been expelled. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ tathānāsitaṃ kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ upalāpessantipi upaṭṭhāpessantipi sambhuñjissantipi sahāpi seyyaṃ kappessantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six support the novice monk Kaṇṭaka, and be attended on by him, live with him, and share a sleeping place with him, even though they know that he has been expelled?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ tathānāsitaṃ kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ upalāpethāpi upaṭṭhāpethāpi sambhuñjathāpi sahāpi seyyaṃ kappethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ tathānāsitaṃ kaṇṭakaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ upalāpessathāpi upaṭṭhāpessathāpi sambhuñjissathāpi sahāpi seyyaṃ kappessatha. Foolish men, how can you support the novice monk Kaṇṭaka, and be attended on by him, live with him, and share a sleeping place with him, even though you know that he has been expelled? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Samaṇuddesopi ce evaṃ vadeyya— ‘Also if a novice monk says, ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’ti, “As I understand the Master’s Teaching, the things he calls obstructive are not able to obstruct one who indulges in them,” so samaṇuddeso bhikkhūhi evamassa vacanīyo— then the monks should correct him in this way: ‘māvuso samaṇuddesa, evaṃ avaca, mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi, na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ, na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya. “No, don’t misrepresent the Master, for it’s not good to misrepresent the Master. The Master would never say such a thing. Anekapariyāyenāvuso samaṇuddesa, antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāyā’ti. In many discourses the Master has declared the obstructive things to be obstructive and how they obstruct one who indulges in them.” Evañca so samaṇuddeso bhikkhūhi vuccamāno tatheva paggaṇheyya, so samaṇuddeso bhikkhūhi evamassa vacanīyo— If that novice monk continues as before, he should be corrected in this way: ‘ajjatagge te, āvuso samaṇuddesa, na ceva so bhagavā satthā apadisitabbo. “From today on you may not refer to the Master as your teacher. Yampi caññe samaṇuddesā labhanti bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ dirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ sāpi te natthi. And you can no longer share a sleeping place with the monks for two or three nights, as can other novice monks. Cara pire vinassā’ti. Go; away with you!” Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ tathānāsitaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ upalāpeyya vā upaṭṭhāpeyya vā sambhuñjeyya vā saha vā seyyaṃ kappeyya, pācittiyan”ti. If a monk supports that novice monk, or he is attended on by him, lives with him, or shares a sleeping place with him, even though he knows that he has been expelled, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Samaṇuddeso nāma A novice monk:
sāmaṇero vuccati. a novice monk is what is meant.
Evaṃ vadeyyāti— Says:
“tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā”ti. “As I understand the Master’s Teaching, the things he calls obstructive are not able to obstruct one who indulges in them.”
So samaṇuddesoti Him:
yo so evaṃvādī samaṇuddeso. the novice monk who speaks in that way.
Bhikkhūhīti The monks:

aññehi bhikkhūhi, ye passanti ye suṇanti tehi vattabbo— “mā, āvuso samaṇuddesa, evaṃ avaca. Mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi. Na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ. Na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya. other monks, those who see it or hear about it. They should say, No, don’t misrepresent the Master, for it’s not good to misrepresent the Master. The Master would never say such a thing. Anekapariyāyenāvuso samaṇuddesa, antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāyā”ti. In many discourses the Master has declared the obstructive things to be obstructive and how they obstruct one who indulges in them.”

Dutiyampi vattabbo … And they should say this a second time … tatiyampi vattabbo … And they should say this a third time …

sace paṭinissajjati iccetaṃ kusalaṃ, no ce paṭinissajjati so samaṇuddeso bhikkhūhi evamassa vacanīyo— If he gives up that view, all is well. If he does not, he should be corrected in this way: “ajjatagge te, āvuso samaṇuddesa, na ceva so bhagavā satthā apadisitabbo. “From today on you may not refer to the Master as your teacher. Yampi caññe samaṇuddesā labhanti bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ dirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ sāpi te natthi. And you can no longer share a sleeping place with the monks for two or three nights, as can other novice monks. Cara pire vinassā”ti. Go; away with you!”

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Jānāti nāma Knows:
sāmaṃ vā jānāti, aññe vā tassa ārocenti, so vā āroceti. he knows by himself or others have told him or the offending novice monk has told him.
Tathānāsitanti Expelled:
evaṃ nāsitaṃ. expelled in this way.
Samaṇuddeso nāma Novice monk:
sāmaṇero vuccati. novice monk is what is meant.
Upalāpeyya vāti Supports:
tassa pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā uddesaṃ vā paripucchaṃ vā dassāmīti upalāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he supports him, thinking, “I’ll give him a bowl/I’ll give him a robe/I’ll instruct him/I’ll examine him,” he commits an offense entailing confession.
Upaṭṭhāpeyya vāti Is attended on by:
tassa cuṇṇaṃ vā mattikaṃ vā dantakaṭṭhaṃ vā mukhodakaṃ vā sādiyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he accepts bath powder, soap, a toothbrush, or water for rinsing the mouth from him, he commits an offense entailing confession.
Sambhuñjeyya vāti Lives with:
sambhogo nāma dve sambhogā— there are two types of living together: āmisasambhogo ca dhammasambhogo ca. material living together and spiritual living together.
Āmisasambhogo nāma Material living together:
āmisaṃ deti vā paṭiggaṇhāti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he gives or receives material things, he commits an offense entailing confession.
Dhammasambhogo nāma Spiritual living together:
uddisati vā uddisāpeti vā, padena uddisati vā uddisāpeti vā, pade pade āpatti pācittiyassa. he recites or gets the other to recite. If he recites or gets the other to recite by the line, then for every line he commits an offense entailing confession. Akkharāya uddisati vā uddisāpeti vā, akkharakkharāya āpatti pācittiyassa. If he recites or gets the other to recite by the syllable, then for every syllable he commits an offense entailing confession.
Saha vā seyyaṃ kappeyyāti Shares a sleeping place with:
ekacchanne nāsitake samaṇuddese nipanne bhikkhu nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. under the same ceiling: if the monk lies down when the expelled novice monk is already lying down, he commits an offense entailing confession; Bhikkhu nipanne nāsitako samaṇuddeso nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if the expelled novice monk lies down when the monk is already lying down, he commits an offense entailing confession; Ubho vā nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if they both lie down together, he commits an offense entailing confession; Uṭṭhahitvā punappunaṃ nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa. every time they get up and then lie down again, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Nāsitake nāsitakasaññī upalāpeti vā upaṭṭhāpeti vā sambhuñjati vā saha vā seyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the novice monk has been expelled, and he perceives him as expelled, and he supports him or is attended on by him or lives with him or shares a sleeping place with him, he commits an offense entailing confession. Nāsitake vematiko upalāpeti vā upaṭṭhāpeti vā sambhuñjati vā saha vā seyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the novice monk has been expelled, but he is unsure if he has, and he supports him or is attended on by him or lives with him or shares a sleeping place with him, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Nāsitake anāsitakasaññī upalāpeti vā upaṭṭhāpeti vā sambhuñjati vā saha vā seyyaṃ kappeti, anāpatti. If the novice monk has been expelled, but he does not perceive him as expelled, and he supports him or is attended on by him or lives with him or shares a sleeping place with him, there is no offense.

Anāsitake nāsitakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the novice monk has not been expelled, but he perceives him as expelled, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anāsitake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the novice monk has not been expelled, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anāsitake anāsitakasaññī, anāpatti. If the novice monk has not been expelled, and he does not perceive him as expelled, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: anāsitakoti jānāti; if he knows that he has not been expelled; taṃ diṭṭhiṃ paṭinissaṭṭhoti jānāti; if he knows that he has given up that view; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Kaṇṭakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ. The tenth rule, the training rule on Kaṇṭaka, is finished.

Sappāṇakavaggo sattamo. The seventh sub-chapter on containing living beings is finished.

Tassuddānaṃ This is the summary:

Sañciccavadhasappāṇaṃ, Intentionally killing, containing living beings,
ukkoṭaṃ duṭṭhullachādanaṃ; Agitation, concealing what is grave;
Ūnavīsati satthañca, Less than twenty, and group of travelers,
saṃvidhānaṃ ariṭṭhakaṃ; Appointment, on Ariṭṭha;
Ukkhittaṃ kaṇṭakañceva, Ejected, and Kaṇṭaka:
dasa sikkhāpadā imeti. These ten training rules.
