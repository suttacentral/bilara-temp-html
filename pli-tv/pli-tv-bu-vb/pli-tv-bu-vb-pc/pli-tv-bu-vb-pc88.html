Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Ratanavagga The sub-chapter on precious things

88. Tūlonaddhasikkhāpada 88. The training rule on upholstered with cotton down

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mañcampi pīṭhampi tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpenti. At that time the monks from the group of six got beds and benches made upholstered with cotton down. Manussā vihāracārikaṃ āhiṇḍantā passitvā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— When people who were walking about the dwellings saw this, they complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā mañcampi pīṭhampi tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpessanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino”ti. “How can the Sakyan ascetics get beds and benches made upholstered with cotton down? They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the complaints of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mañcampi pīṭhampi tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpessantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six get beds and benches made upholstered with cotton down?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, mañcampi pīṭhampi tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, mañcampi pīṭhampi tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpessatha. Foolish men, how can you get beds and benches made upholstered with cotton down? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā tūlonaddhaṃ kārāpeyya, uddālanakaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk gets a bed or a bench made upholstered with cotton down, it is to be stripped, and he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Mañco nāma A bed:
cattāro mañcā— masārako, bundikābaddho, kuḷīrapādako, āhaccapādako. there are four kinds of beds: one with legs and frame, called _masāraka_; one with legs and frame, called _bundikābaddha_; one with crooked legs; one with detachable legs.
Pīṭhaṃ nāma A bench:
cattāri pīṭhāni— masārakaṃ, bundikābaddhaṃ, kuḷīrapādakaṃ, āhaccapādakaṃ. there are four kinds of benches: one with legs and frame, called _masāraka_; one with legs and frame, called _bundikābaddha_; one with crooked legs; one with detachable legs.
Tūlaṃ nāma Cotton down:
tīṇi tūlāni— rukkhatūlaṃ, latātūlaṃ, poṭakitūlaṃ. there are three kinds of cotton down: cotton down from trees, cotton down from creepers, cotton down from grass.
Kārāpeyyāti Gets […] made:
karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. if he makes one or gets one made, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena uddāletvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ. When he obtains it, it is to be stripped, and he is then to confess an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he makes one or gets one made for the benefit of someone else, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he obtains one that was made by someone else and then uses it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: āyoge, if it is for a back-and-knee strap; kāyabandhane, if it is for a belt; aṃsabaddhake, if it is for a shoulder strap; pattatthavikāya, if it is for a bowl bag; parissāvane, if it is for a water filter; bibbohanaṃ karoti, if he is making a pillow; aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā uddāletvā paribhuñjati, if he obtains one made by another and he strips it before using it; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Tūlonaddhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ. The sixth rule, the training rule on upholstered with cotton down, is finished.
