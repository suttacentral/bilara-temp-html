Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

73. Mohanasikkhāpada 73. The training rule on deception

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anācāraṃ ācaritvā “aññāṇakena āpannāti jānantū”ti pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadanti— At that time the monks from the group of six misbehaved. And they said to each other, “Let’s make the other monks think that we committed these offenses because we didn’t know about the rules.” “idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī”ti. Then, during the recitation of the Monastic Code, they said, “Just now did we find out that this rule too has come down in the Monastic Code, is included in the Monastic Code, and comes up for recitation every half-month.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vakkhanti— “How can the monks from the group of six, while the Monastic Code is being recited, say, ‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’”ti … pe … ‘Just now did we find out that this rule too has come down in the Monastic Code, is included in the Monastic Code, and comes up for recitation every half-month’?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadetha— “Is it true, monks, that you said this?” ‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’”ti?

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vakkhatha— Foolish men, while the Monastic Code is being recited, how can you say, ‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’ti. ‘Just now did we find out that this rule too has come down in the Monastic Code, is included in the Monastic Code, and comes up for recitation every half-month’? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu anvaddhamāsaṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadeyya— ‘During the half-monthly recitation of the Monastic Code, a monk might say, ‘idāneva kho ahaṃ jānāmi, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’ti. “Just now did I find out that this rule too has come down in the Monastic Code, is included in the Monastic Code, and comes up for recitation every half-month.” Tañce bhikkhuṃ aññe bhikkhū jāneyyuṃ nisinnapubbaṃ iminā bhikkhunā dvattikkhattuṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne, ko pana vādo bhiyyo, na ca tassa bhikkhuno aññāṇakena mutti atthi, yañca tattha āpattiṃ āpanno tañca yathādhammo kāretabbo, uttari cassa moho āropetabbo— If other monks know that that monk has previously sat through at least two or three recitations of the Monastic Code, then that monk is not let off because of ignorance, and he is to be dealt with according to the rule. Further, he should be charged with deception: ‘tassa te, āvuso, alābhā, tassa te dulladdhaṃ, yaṃ tvaṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭhiṃ katvā manasi karosī’ti. “It’s a loss for you that you don’t pay proper attention when the Monastic Code is being recited.” Idaṃ tasmiṃ mohanake pācittiyan”ti. And for the act of deception, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Anvaddhamāsanti Half-monthly:
anuposathikaṃ. on every observance day.
Pātimokkhe uddissamāneti During the […] recitation of the Monastic Code:
uddisante. during the reciting.
Evaṃ vadeyyāti Might say:
anācāraṃ ācaritvā— after misbehaving, he thinks, “aññāṇakena āpannoti jānantū”ti pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadeti— “Let them think that I committed these offenses because I didn’t know about the rules.” If, while the Monastic Code is being recited, he then says, “idāneva kho ahaṃ jānāmi, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī”ti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. “Just now did I find out that this rule too has come down in the Monastic Code, is included in the Monastic Code, and comes up for recitation every half-month,” then he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Tañce mohetukāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ aññe bhikkhū jāneyyuṃ nisinnapubbaṃ iminā bhikkhunā dvattikkhattuṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne, ko pana vādo bhiyyo, na ca tassa bhikkhuno aññāṇakena mutti atthi, yañca tattha āpattiṃ āpanno, tañca yathādhammo kāretabbo, uttari cassa moho āropetabbo. If other monks know that the monk who wants deceive has previously sat through at least two or three recitations of the Monastic Code, then that monk is not let off because of ignorance, and he is to be dealt with according to the rule. Further, he should be charged with deception: “Evañca pana, bhikkhave, āropetabbo. “And, monks, he is to be charged in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭhiṃ katvā manasi karoti. This monk so-and-so didn’t pay proper attention when the Monastic Code was being recited. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should charge him with deception. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭhiṃ katvā manasi karoti. This monk so-and-so didn’t pay proper attention when the Monastic Code was being recited. Saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropeti. The Order charges him with deception. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohassa āropanā, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of charging him with deception should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Āropito saṃghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno moho. The monk so-and-so has been charged with deception by the Order. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Anāropite mohe moheti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he deceives, but he is not charged with deception, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āropite mohe moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he deceives, and he is charged with deception, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he deceives, and it is a legitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematiko moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he deceives, and it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he deceives, and it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as illegitimate, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as illegitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: na vitthārena sutaṃ hoti, if he has not heard it in full; ūnakadvattikkhattuṃ vitthārena sutaṃ hoti, if he has heard it fewer than two or three times in full; na mohetukāmassa, if he does not want to deceive; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Mohanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ. The third rule, the training rule on deception, is finished.
