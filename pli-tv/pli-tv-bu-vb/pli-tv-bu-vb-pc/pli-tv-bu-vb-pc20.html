Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Bhūtagāmavagga The sub-chapter on plants

20. Sappāṇakasikkhāpada 20. The training rule on containing living beings

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. At one time the Buddha was staying at Āḷavī at the Aggāḷava Shrine. Tena kho pana samayena āḷavakā bhikkhū navakammaṃ karontā jānaṃ sappāṇakaṃ udakaṃ tiṇampi mattikampi siñcantipi siñcāpentipi. At that time the monks at Āḷavī were doing building work. And they poured water that they knew contained living beings onto grass and clay, and they got others to do the same. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma āḷavakā bhikkhū jānaṃ sappāṇakaṃ udakaṃ tiṇampi mattikampi siñcissantipi siñcāpessantipī”ti. “How can the monks at Āḷavī pour water that they know contains living beings onto grass and clay, and get others to do the same?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āḷavake bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, they told the Master.… “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ sappāṇakaṃ udakaṃ tiṇampi mattikampi siñcathapi siñcāpethapī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ sappāṇakaṃ udakaṃ tiṇampi mattikampi siñcissathapi siñcāpessathapi. Foolish men, how can you pour water that you know contains living beings onto grass and clay, and get others to do the same? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ sappāṇakaṃ udakaṃ tiṇaṃ vā mattikaṃ vā siñceyya vā siñcāpeyya vā, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk pours, or gets poured, water that he knows contains living beings onto grass or clay, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. …The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Jānāti nāma He knows:
sāmaṃ vā jānāti. Aññe vā tassa ārocenti. he knows by himself or others have told him.
Siñceyyāti Pours:
sayaṃ siñcati, āpatti pācittiyassa. he pours it himself.
Siñcāpeyyāti Gets poured:
aññaṃ āṇāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he asks another, he commits an offense entailing confession. Sakiṃ āṇatto bahukampi siñcati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he only asks once, then even if the other pours many times, he commits one offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Sappāṇake sappāṇakasaññī tiṇaṃ vā mattikaṃ vā siñcati vā siñcāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it contains living beings, and he perceives it as containing living beings, and he pours it onto grass or clay, or he gets it poured, he commits an offense entailing confession. Sappāṇake vematiko tiṇaṃ vā mattikaṃ vā siñcati vā siñcāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it contains living beings, but he is unsure if it does, and he pours it onto grass or clay, or he gets it poured, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Sappāṇake appāṇakasaññī tiṇaṃ vā mattikaṃ vā siñcati vā siñcāpeti vā, anāpatti. If it contains living beings, but he perceives it as not containing living beings, and he pours it onto grass or clay, or he gets it poured, there is no offense.

Appāṇake sappāṇakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it does not contain living beings, but he perceives it as containing living beings, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Appāṇake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it does not contain living beings, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Appāṇake appāṇakasaññī, anāpatti. If it does not contain living beings, and he perceives it as not containing living beings, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sappāṇakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ. The tenth rule, the training rule on containing living beings, is finished.

Bhūtagāmavaggo dutiyo. The second sub-chapter on plants is finished.

Tassuddānaṃ This is the summary:

Bhūtaṃ aññāya ujjhāyaṃ, Being, with another,
pakkamantena te duve; Complaining, the two with departing;
Pubbe nikkaḍḍhanāhacca, Before, throwing out, removable,
dvāraṃ sappāṇakena cāti. Door, and containing living beings.
