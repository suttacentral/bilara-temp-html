Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Ratanavagga The sub-chapter on precious things

91. Vassikasāṭikāsikkhāpada 91. The training rule on rainy-season robes

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ vassikasāṭikā anuññātā hoti. At that time the Master had allowed the rainy-season robe for the monks. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū— “bhagavatā vassikasāṭikā anuññātā”ti appamāṇikāyo vassikasāṭikāyo dhārenti. Knowing this, the monks from the group of six used over-sized rainy-season robes. Puratopi pacchatopi ākaḍḍhantā āhiṇḍanti. As they were walking about, they were dragging them along, both in front and behind.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū appamāṇikāyo vassikasāṭikāyo dhāressantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six use over-sized rainy-season robes?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, appamāṇikāyo vassikasāṭikāyo dhārethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, appamāṇikāyo vassikasāṭikāyo dhāressatha. Foolish men, how can you use over-sized rainy-season robes? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Vassikasāṭikaṃ pana bhikkhunā kārayamānena pamāṇikā kāretabbā. ‘If a monk is getting a rainy-season robe made, it should be made to the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ—dīghaso cha vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ aḍḍhateyyā. This is the right size: 1.5 meters long and 67.5 centimeters wide. Taṃ atikkāmayato chedanakaṃ pācittiyan”ti. If it exceeds that, it is to be cut down, and he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Vassikasāṭikā nāma A rainy-season robe:
vassānassa catumāsatthāya. for use during the four months of the rainy season.
Kārayamānenāti Is getting […] made:
karonto vā kārāpento vā. Pamāṇikā kāretabbā. making it himself or getting someone else to make it, it should be made to the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ— dīghaso cha vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ aḍḍhateyyā. This is the right size: 1.5 meters long and 67.5 centimeters wide. Taṃ atikkāmetvā karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If he makes one or gets one made that exceeds that, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena chinditvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ. When he obtains it, it is to be cut down, and he is then to confess an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he makes one or gets one made for the benefit of someone else, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he obtains one that was made by someone else and then uses it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pamāṇikaṃ karoti, if he makes it the right size; ūnakaṃ karoti, if he makes it less than the right size; aññena kataṃ pamāṇātikkantaṃ paṭilabhitvā chinditvā paribhuñjati, if he obtains one made by another that exceeds the right size and he cuts it down before using it; vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, if he makes a canopy, a floor cover, a cloth screen, a mattress, or a pillow; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Vassikasāṭikāsikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ. The ninth rule, the training rule on rainy-season robes, is finished.
