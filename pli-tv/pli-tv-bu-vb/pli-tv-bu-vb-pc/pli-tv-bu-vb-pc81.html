Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

81. Dubbalasikkhāpada 81. The training rule on what is worn out

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. At one time the Buddha was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrel sanctuary. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā dabbo mallaputto saṃghassa senāsanañca paññapeti bhattāni ca uddisati. At that time Venerable Dabba the Mallian, who was the assigner of dwellings and the designator of meals, So cāyasmā dubbalacīvaro hoti. had a robe that was worn out. Tena kho pana samayena saṃghassa ekaṃ cīvaraṃ uppannaṃ hoti. Just then the Order had obtained robe-cloth, Atha kho saṃgho taṃ cīvaraṃ āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa adāsi. and it gave it to Dabba. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks from the group of six complained and criticized it, “yathāsanthutaṃ bhikkhū saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇāmentī”ti. “The monks are diverting the Order’s requisites according to friendship.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū samaggena saṃghena cīvaraṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjissantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six give out robe-cloth together with a complete Order and then criticize it afterwards?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, samaggena saṃghena cīvaraṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjathā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, samaggena saṃghena cīvaraṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjissatha. Foolish men, how can you give out robe-cloth together with a complete Order and then criticize it afterwards? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ, vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu samaggena saṃghena cīvaraṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjeyya ‘yathāsanthutaṃ bhikkhū saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇāmentī’ti, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk gives out robe-cloth together with a complete Order and then criticizes it afterwards, saying, “The monks are giving out the Order’s requisites according to friendship,” he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Samaggo nāma A complete Order:
saṃgho samānasaṃvāsako samānasīmāyaṃ ṭhito. belonging to the same community, staying within the same boundary.
Cīvaraṃ nāma Robe-cloth:
channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ. one of the six kinds of robe-cloth, but not smaller than what can be transferred.
Datvāti Gives out:
sayaṃ datvā. having given it himself.
Yathāsanthutaṃ nāma According to friendship:
yathāmittatā yathāsandiṭṭhatā yathāsambhattatā yathāsamānupajjhāyakatā yathāsamānācariyakatā. according to friendship, according to companionship, according to who one is devoted to, according to having the same preceptor, according to having the same teacher.
Saṃghikaṃ nāma The Order’s:
saṃghassa dinnaṃ hoti pariccattaṃ. given to the Order, given up to the Order.
Lābho nāma Requisites:
cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā, antamaso cuṇṇapiṇḍopi, dantakaṭṭhampi, dasikasuttampi. robe-cloth, almsfood, a dwelling, and medicines; even bath powder, a toothbrush, or a piece of string.
Pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjeyyāti Criticizes it afterwards:
upasampannassa saṃghena sammatassa senāsanapaññāpakassa vā bhattuddesakassa vā yāgubhājakassa vā phalabhājakassa vā khajjabhājakassa vā appamattakavissajjakassa vā cīvaraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. when robe-cloth has been given to someone who is fully ordained and who is the assigner of dwellings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of non-staple food or the distributor of minor requisites, and he has been agreed upon by the Order as such, then if a monk criticizes it, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī cīvaraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as legitimate, and he criticizes it when robe-cloth has been given, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematiko cīvaraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, and he criticizes it when robe-cloth has been given, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī cīvaraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as illegitimate, and he criticizes it when robe-cloth has been given, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he criticizes it when another requisite has been given, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Upasampannassa saṃghena asammatassa senāsanapaññāpakassa vā bhattuddesakassa vā yāgubhājakassa vā phalabhājakassa vā khajjabhājakassa vā appamattakavissajjakassa vā cīvaraṃ vā aññaṃ vā parikkhāraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. When someone who is fully ordained is the assigner of dwellings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of non-staple food or the distributor of minor requisites, but he has not been agreed upon by the Order as such, and a monk criticizes it when robe-cloth or another requisite has been given to him, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannassa saṃghena sammatassa vā asammatassa vā senāsanapaññāpakassa vā bhattuddesakassa vā yāgubhājakassa vā phalabhājakassa vā khajjabhājakassa vā appamattakavissajjakassa vā cīvaraṃ vā aññaṃ vā parikkhāraṃ dinne khiyyati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. When someone who is not fully ordained is the assigner of dwellings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of non-staple food or the distributor of minor requisites, whether he has been agreed upon by the Order as such or not, and a monk criticizes it when robe-cloth or another requisite has been given to him, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, anāpatti. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as illegitimate, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pakatiyā chandā dosā mohā bhayā karontaṃ “kvattho tassa dinnena laddhāpi vinipātessati na sammā upanessatī”ti khiyyati, if he criticizes one who normally acts from desire, ill-will, confusion, or fear, saying, “What’s the point of giving it to him—he’ll ruin it and not make use of it in an appropriate way;” ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Dubbalasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ ekādasamaṃ. The eleventh rule, the training rule on what is worn out, is finished.
