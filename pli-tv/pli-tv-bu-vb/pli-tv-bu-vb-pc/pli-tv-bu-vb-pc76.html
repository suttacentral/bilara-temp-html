Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

76. Amūlakasikkhāpada 76. The training rule on groundlessly

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhuṃ amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃsenti. At that time the monks from the group of six groundlessly accused a monk of an offense entailing suspension.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhuṃ amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃsessantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six groundlessly accuse a monk of an offense entailing suspension?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhuṃ amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃsethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhuṃ amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃsessatha. Foolish men, how can you groundlessly accuse a monk of an offense entailing suspension? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃseyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk groundlessly accuses another monk of an offense entailing suspension, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Bhikkhunti Another monk:
aññaṃ bhikkhuṃ. a different monk.
Amūlakaṃ nāma Groundlessly:
adiṭṭhaṃ assutaṃ aparisaṅkitaṃ. not seen, not heard, not suspected.
Saṃghādisesenāti An offense entailing suspension:
terasannaṃ aññatarena. one of the thirteen.
Anuddhaṃseyyāti Accuses:
codeti vā codāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he accuses him or causes him to be accused, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampanne upasampannasaññī amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃseti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, and he perceives him as fully ordained, and he groundlessly accuses him of an offense entailing suspension, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne vematiko amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃseti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he is unsure if he is, and he groundlessly accuses him of an offense entailing suspension, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī amūlakena saṃghādisesena anuddhaṃseti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he does not perceive him as fully ordained, and he groundlessly accuses him of an offense entailing suspension, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Ācāravipattiyā vā diṭṭhivipattiyā vā anuddhaṃseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he accuses someone of failure in conduct or of failure in view, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannaṃ anuddhaṃseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he accuses one who is not fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he perceives him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, and he does not perceive him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: tathāsaññī codeti vā codāpeti vā, if he accuses someone, or gets someone accused, according to what he has perceived; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Amūlakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ. The sixth rule, the training rule on groundlessly, is finished.
