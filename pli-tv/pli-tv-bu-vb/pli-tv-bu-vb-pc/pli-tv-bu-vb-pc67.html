Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sappāṇakavagga The sub-chapter on containing living beings

67. Saṃvidhānasikkhāpada 67. The training rule on appointments

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchanto aññatarena gāmadvārena atikkamati. At that time a monk who was traveling in the Kosalan country toward Sāvatthī exited through the gateway of a certain village. Aññatarā itthī sāmikena saha bhaṇḍitvā gāmato nikkhamitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavoca— Just then a woman who had had an argument with her husband was leaving the same village. She saw that monk and asked him, “kahaṃ, bhante, ayyo gamissatī”ti? “Venerable, where are you going?”

“Sāvatthiṃ kho ahaṃ, bhagini, gamissāmī”ti. “I’m going to Sāvatthī.”

“Ahaṃ ayyena saddhiṃ gamissāmī”ti. “I’d like to go with you.”

“Eyyāsi, bhaginī”ti. “You may come along.”

Atha kho tassā itthiyā sāmiko gāmato nikkhamitvā manusse pucchi— Soon afterwards that woman’s husband also left that village, asking people, “apāyyo, evarūpiṃ itthiṃ passeyyāthā”ti? “Have you seen such-and-such a woman?”

“Esāyyo, pabbajitena saha gacchatī”ti. “She’s walking along with an ascetic.”

Atha kho so puriso anubandhitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ gahetvā ākoṭetvā muñci. He then followed after them, caught that monk, and gave him a beating before releasing him. Atha kho so bhikkhu aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle padhūpento nisīdi. The monk sat down fuming at the foot of a tree. Atha kho sā itthī taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca— And the woman said to her husband, “nāyyo, so bhikkhu maṃ nippātesi; “This monk didn’t make me go; api ca ahameva tena bhikkhunā saddhiṃ gacchāmi; I was the one who went with him. akārako so bhikkhu; He is innocent. gaccha, naṃ khamāpehī”ti. Go and ask his forgiveness.” Atha kho so puriso taṃ bhikkhuṃ khamāpesi. And he did so.

Atha kho so bhikkhu sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. The monk then went to Sāvatthī, where he told the monks about what had happened. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhu mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissatī”ti … pe … “How can a monk travel on a main road by appointment with a woman?” … “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjasī”ti? “Is it true, monk, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, “… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissasi. Foolish man, how can you travel on a main road by appointment with a woman? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ, uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjeyya, antamaso gāmantarampi, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk travels on a main road by appointment with a woman, even if just between villages, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Mātugāmo nāma A woman:
manussitthī, na yakkhī na petī na tiracchānagatā viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ. a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal. She understands and is capable of discerning bad speech and good speech, what is lewd and what is not lewd.
Saddhinti With:
ekato. together.
Saṃvidhāyāti— By appointment:
“gacchāma bhagini, gacchāmāyya, gacchāmāyya, gacchāma bhagini, ajja vā hiyyo vā pare vā gacchāmā”ti saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. if he makes an appointment like this: he says, “Let’s go,” and she replies, “Yes, let’s go, Venerable;” or she says, “Let’s go, Venerable,” and he replies, “Yes, let’s go;” or he says, “Let’s go today/tomorrow/the day after tomorrow,” then he commits an offense of wrong conduct.
Antamaso gāmantarampīti Even if just between villages:
kukkuṭasampāte gāme gāmantare gāmantare āpatti pācittiyassa. when the villages are a chicken’s flight apart, then for every village interval he commits an offense entailing confession. Agāmake araññe addhayojane addhayojane āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is in an uninhabited area, in the wilderness, then for every six kilometers he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Mātugāme mātugāmasaññī saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a woman, and he perceives her as a woman, and he travels on a main road by appointment with her, even if just between villages, he commits an offense entailing confession. Mātugāme vematiko saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a woman, but he is unsure if it is, and he travels on a main road by appointment with her, even if just between villages, he commits an offense entailing confession. Mātugāme amātugāmasaññī saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a woman, but he does not perceive her as a woman, and he travels on a main road by appointment with her, even if just between villages, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Bhikkhu saṃvidahati mātugāmo na saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the monk makes an appointment, but the woman does not express her agreement, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Yakkhiyā vā petiyā paṇḍakena vā tiracchānagatamanussaviggahitthiyā vā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he travels on a main road by appointment with a female spirit, with a female ghost, with a eunuch, with a female animal in the form of a woman, even if just between villages, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Amātugāme mātugāmasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not a woman, but he perceives it as a woman, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Amātugāme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not a woman, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Amātugāme amātugāmasaññī, anāpatti. If it is not a woman, and he does not perceive it as a woman, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asaṃvidahitvā gacchati, if he goes without an appointment; mātugāmo saṃvidahati bhikkhu na saṃvidahati, if the woman has made an appointment, but he has not expressed his agreement; visaṅketena gacchati, if he goes, but not according to the appointment; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Saṃvidhānasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ. The seventh rule, the training rule on appointments, is finished.
