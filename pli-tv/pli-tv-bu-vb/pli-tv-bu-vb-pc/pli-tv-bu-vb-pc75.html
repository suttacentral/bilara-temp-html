Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

75. Talasattikasikkhāpada 75. The training rule on threatening with the palm of the hand

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā sattarasavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ talasattikaṃ uggiranti. At that time the monks from the group of six raised their hand in anger against the monks from the group of seventeen. Te pahārasamuccitā rodanti. Expecting to be struck, they cried. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu— Other monks asked them why, “kissa tumhe, āvuso, rodathā”ti? “Ime, āvuso, chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā amhākaṃ talasattikaṃ uggirantī”ti. and they told them what had happened.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā sattarasavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ talasattikaṃ uggirissantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six raise their hand in anger against the monks from the group of seventeen?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, kupitā anattamanā sattarasavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ talasattikaṃ uggirathā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, kupitā anattamanā sattarasavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ talasattikaṃ uggirissatha. Foolish men, how can you raise your hand in anger against the monks from the group of seventeen? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa kupito anattamano talasattikaṃ uggireyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk raises a hand in anger against another monk, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Bhikkhussāti Against another monk:
aññassa bhikkhussa. against a different monk.
Kupito anattamanoti In anger:
anabhiraddho āhatacitto khilajāto. discontent, having hatred, hostile.
Talasattikaṃ uggireyyāti Raises a hand:
kāyaṃ vā kāyapaṭibaddhaṃ vā antamaso uppalapattampi uccāreti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if he raises the body or something connected to his body, even if only a lotus leaf, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampanne upasampannasaññī kupito anattamano talasattikaṃ uggirati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, and he perceives him as fully ordained, and he raises a hand against him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne vematiko kupito anattamano talasattikaṃ uggirati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he is unsure if he is, and he raises a hand against him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī kupito anattamano talasattikaṃ uggirati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is one who is fully ordained, but he does not perceive him as fully ordained, and he raises a hand against him in anger, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannassa kupito anattamano talasattikaṃ uggirati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he raises a hand in anger against one who is not fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he perceives him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is one who is not fully ordained, and he does not perceive him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: kenaci viheṭhīyamāno mokkhādhippāyo talasattikaṃ uggirati, if he is being oppressed and he raises a hand for the purpose of freeing himself; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Talasattikasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ. The fifth rule, the training rule on threatening with the palm of the hand, is finished.
