Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Sahadhammikavagga The sub-chapter on legitimately

80. Chandaṃadatvāgamanasikkhāpada 80. The training rule on leaving without giving consent

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena saṃgho sannipatito hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. At that time the Order had assembled on some business. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū cīvarakammaṃ karontā ekassa chandaṃ adaṃsu. The monks from the group of six were busy making robes and so they gave their consent to one among them.

Atha kho saṃgho “yassatthāya sannipatito taṃ kammaṃ karissāmī”ti ñattiṃ ṭhapesi. When the Order was ready to do the legal procedure for which it had assembled, it set forth the motion. Atha kho so bhikkhu— That monk from the group of six thought, “evamevime ekamekassa kammaṃ karonti, kassa tumhe kammaṃ karissathā”ti chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi. “This is just how they do procedures against us one by one, but against whom will you do this one?” and without giving his consent, he got up from his seat and left.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhu saṃghe vinicchayakathāya vattamānāya chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamissatī”ti … pe … “How can that monk get up from his seat and leave without giving his consent, when the Order is in the process of deliberating?” … “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, saṃghe vinicchayakathāya vattamānāya chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamasī”ti? “Is it true, monk, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, “… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṃghe vinicchayakathāya vattamānāya chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamissasi. Foolish man, how can you get up from your seat and leave without giving your consent, when the Order is in the process of deliberating? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu saṃghe vinicchayakathāya vattamānāya chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkameyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘When the Order is in the process of deliberating, if a monk gets up from his seat and leaves without first giving his consent, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Saṃghe vinicchayakathā nāma When the Order is […] deliberating:
vatthu vā ārocitaṃ hoti avinicchitaṃ, ñatti vā ṭhapitā hoti, kammavācā vā vippakatā hoti. when the subject-matter has been announced but the deliberation is not yet concluded, or when the motion has been set forth, or when the announcement is still under way.
Chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkameyyāti— Gets up from his seat and leaves without first giving his consent:
“kathaṃ idaṃ kammaṃ kuppaṃ assa vaggaṃ assa na kareyyā”ti gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. if he leaves, thinking, “How may this procedure be disturbed/what can be done so that the assembly is incomplete/what can be done so that it isn’t done,” then he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Parisāya hatthapāsaṃ vijahantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he is in the process of going beyond arm’s reach of the assembly, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Vijahite āpatti pācittiyassa. When he has gone beyond arm’s reach, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as legitimate, and he gets up from his seat and leaves without first giving his consent, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematiko chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, and he gets up from his seat and leaves without first giving his consent, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī chandaṃ adatvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamati, anāpatti. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as illegitimate, and he gets up from his seat and leaves without first giving his consent, there is no offense.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, anāpatti. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as illegitimate, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: “saṃghassa bhaṇḍanaṃ vā kalaho vā viggaho vā vivādo vā bhavissatī”ti gacchati, if he leaves because he thinks there will be quarreling or dispute in the Order; “saṃghabhedo vā saṃgharāji vā bhavissatī”ti gacchati, if he leaves because he thinks there will be a fracture or schism in the Order; “adhammena vā vaggena vā na kammārahassa vā kammaṃ karissatī”ti gacchati, if he leaves because he thinks the procedure will be illegitimate, done by an incomplete assembly, or done to one who is not subject to a legal procedure; gilāno gacchati, if he leaves because he is sick; gilānassa karaṇīyena gacchati, if he leaves because there is something to be done for someone who is sick; uccārena vā passāvena vā pīḷito gacchati, if he leaves because he needs to relieve himself; “na kammaṃ kopetukāmo puna paccāgamissāmī”ti gacchati, if he leaves with the intention to return, and not because he wants to challenge the procedure; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Chandaṃ adatvā gamanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ. The tenth rule, the training rule on leaving without giving consent, is finished.
