12. Aññavādakasikkhāpada 12. The training rule on evasive speech

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā channo anācāraṃ ācaritvā saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati—Then, when he was examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, he spoke evasively, At that time Venerable Channa was misbehaving. Then, when he was examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, he spoke evasively, “ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā”ti? “Who has committed an offense? What offense has been committed? In regard to what has an offense has been committed? How has an offense been committed? Who are you talking about? What are you talking about?”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarissati— “How can Venerable Channa speak evasively in this way when he is examined about an offense in the midst of the Order?’”… ‘ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’”ti … pe … saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarasi—‘ “Is it true, Channa, that you are speaking like this?” ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’”ti?

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, “… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarissasi— Foolish man, when examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, how can you speak evasively, thus: “ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā”ti. ‘Who has committed an offense? What offense has been committed? In regard to what has an offense has been committed? How has an offense been committed? Who are you talking about? What are you talking about?’ Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …”… vigarahitvā … pe … and after criticizing him … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— he gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “tena hi, bhikkhave, saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropetu. “Well then, monks, let the Order charge Channa with evasive speech. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, ropetabbaṃ. And he is to be charged in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. This monk Channa speaks evasively when examined about an offense in the midst of the Order. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should charge him with evasive speech. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. This monk Channa speaks evasively when examined about an offense in the midst of the Order. Saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropeti. The Order is charging him of evasive speech. Yassāyasmato khamati channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakassa ropanā, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of charging him of evasive speech should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Ropitaṃ saṃghena channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ. The Order has charged the monk Channa of evasive speech. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya … pe … After criticizing Channa in many ways for being difficult to support … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— the Master said, “And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

“Aññavādake pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk speaks evasively, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā channo saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicaranto— Later, when Channa was again being examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, he thought, “āpattiṃ āpajjissāmī”ti tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti. “By speaking evasively I’ll commit an offenses,” and he instead harassed the Order by remaining silent.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ vihesessatī”ti … pe … “When he’s examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, how can Venerable Channa harass the Order by remaining silent?’”… “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ vihesesī”ti? “Is it true, Channa, that you are acting like this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, “… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ vihesessasi. Foolish man, when you are examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, how can you harass the Order by remaining silent? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …”… vigarahitvā … pe … and after criticizing him … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— he gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “tena hi, bhikkhave, saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropetu. “Well then, monks, let the Order charge Channa of harassment. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, ropetabbaṃ. And he is to be charged in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti. This monk Channa, when examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, harasses the Order by remaining silent. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should charge him of harassment. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṃghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti. This monk Channa, when examined about an offense in the midst of the Order, harasses the Order by remaining silent. Saṃgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropeti. The Order is charging him of harassment. Yassāyasmato khamati channassa bhikkhuno vihesakassa ropanā, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of charging him of harassment should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Ropitaṃ saṃghena channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ. The Order has charged the monk Channa of harassment. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya … pe … After criticizing Channa in many ways for being difficult to support … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— the Master said, “And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Aññavādake vihesake pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk speaks evasively or harasses, he commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Aññavādako nāma One who speaks evasively:
saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati— “ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā”ti. Eso aññavādako nāma. when being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, he speaks evasively because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it, saying, “Who has committed an offense? What offense has been committed? In regard to what has an offense has been committed? How has an offense been committed? Who are you talking about? What are you talking about?”— this is called “one who speaks evasively.”
Vihesako nāma One who harasses:
saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti. Eso vihesako nāma. when being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, he harasses the Order by remaining silent because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it— this is called “one who harasses.”

Permutations

Aropite aññavādake saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati— If he has not been charged with evasive speech, but he is being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, and he then speaks evasively because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it, saying, “ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā”ti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. “Who has committed an offense? What offense has been committed? In regard to what has an offense has been committed? How has an offense been committed? Who are you talking about? What are you talking about?” then he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Aropite vihesake saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he has not been charged with harassment, but he is being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, and he then harasses the Order by remaining silent because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it, then he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Ropite aññavādake saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati— If he has been charged with evasive speech, and he is being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, and he then speaks evasively because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it, saying,

“ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā”ti, āpatti pācittiyassa. “Who has committed an offense? What offense has been committed? In regard to what has an offense has been committed? How has an offense been committed? Who are you talking about? What are you talking about?” then he commits an offense entailing confession.

Ropite vihesake saṃghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṃghaṃ viheseti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he has been charged with harassment, and he is being examined in the midst of the Order about an action that was the basis for an offense or about the class of an offense, and he then harasses the Order by remaining silent because he does not want to talk about it or reveal it, then he commits an offense entailing confession.

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as legitimate, and he speaks evasively and he harasses, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematiko aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, and he speaks evasively and he harasses, he commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as illegitimate, and he speaks evasively and he harasses, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and he perceives it as illegitimate, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ajānanto pucchati, if he asks because he does not know; gilāno vā na katheti; if he does not speak because he is sick; “saṃghassa bhaṇḍanaṃ vā kalaho vā viggaho vā vivādo vā bhavissatī”ti na katheti; if he does not speak because he thinks there will be quarreling or disputing in the Order; “saṃghabhedo vā saṃgharāji vā bhavissatī”ti na katheti; if he does not speak because he thinks there will be fracture or schism in the Order; “adhammena vā vaggena vā nakammārahassa vā kammaṃ karissatī”ti na katheti; if he does not speak because he thinks the legal procedure will be illegitimate, done by an incomplete assembly, or done to one who is not subject to a legal procedure; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Aññavādakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ. The second rule, the training rule on evasive speech, is finished.
