Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Sakkaccavagga The sub-chapter on respectfully

33. Sapadānasikkhāpada 33. The training rule on in order

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tahaṃ tahaṃ omasitvā piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjanti … At that time the monks from the group of six ate almsfood picking here and there. …

Final ruling

“Sapadānaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will eat almsfood in order,’ this is how you should train.”

Sapadānaṃ piṇḍapāto bhuñjitabbo. Almsfood is to be eaten in order. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca tahaṃ tahaṃ omasitvā piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk eats almsfood picking here and there out of disrespect, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; aññesaṃ dento omasati, if he picks things out when giving to others; aññassa bhājane ākiranto omasati, if he picks things out when putting into someone else’s vessel; uttaribhaṅge, if it is a non-bean curry; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third training rule is finished.
