Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Kabaḷavagga The sub-chapter on mouthfuls

47. Hatthaniddhunakasikkhāpada 47. The training rule on shaking the hands

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjanti … At that time the monks from the group of six ate shaking their hands. …

Final ruling

“Na hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will not eat shaking the hands,’ this is how you should train.”

Na hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. One should not eat shaking the hands. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect eats shaking his hands, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; kacavaraṃ chaḍḍento hatthaṃ niddhunāti, if he shakes the hand to discard rubbish; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
