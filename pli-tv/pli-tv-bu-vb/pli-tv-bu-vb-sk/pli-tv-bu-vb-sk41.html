Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Kabaḷavagga The sub-chapter on mouthfuls

41. Anāhaṭasikkhāpada 41. The training rule on not brought

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anāhaṭe kabaḷe mukhadvāraṃ vivaranti … At that time the monks from the group of six opened the mouths when a mouthful had not been brought to it. …

Final ruling

“Na anāhaṭe kabaḷe mukhadvāraṃ vivarissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will not open the mouth when a mouthful has not been brought to it,’ this is how you should train.”

Na anāhaṭe kabaḷe mukhadvāraṃ vivaritabbaṃ. One should not open the mouth when a mouthful has not been brought to it. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca anāhaṭe kabaḷe mukhadvāraṃ vivarati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect opens his mouth when a mouthful has not been brought to it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca … pe … if it is unintentional; if he is not mindful; if he does not know; if he is sick; if there are dangers; if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The first training rule is finished.
