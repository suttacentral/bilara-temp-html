Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Pādukavagga The sub-chapter on shoes

67. Oguṇṭhitasikkhāpada 67. The training rule on covered heads

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū oguṇṭhitasīsassa dhammaṃ desenti … At that time the monks from the group of six gave teachings to people whose heads were covered. …

Final ruling

“Na oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will not give teachings to anyone whose head is covered and who is not sick,’ this is how you should train.”

Definitions

Oguṇṭhitasīso nāma Whose head is covered:
sasīsaṃ pāruto vuccati. the upper robe also covers the head is what is meant.

Na oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. One should not give teachings to anyone whose head is covered and who is not sick. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect gives teachings to someone whose head is covered and who is not sick, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; sīsaṃ vivarāpetvā deseti, if he teaches the person after getting them to uncover their head; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
