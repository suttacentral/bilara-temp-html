69. Nīcāsanasikkhāpada 69. The training rule on low seats

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desenti. At that time the monks from the group of six were sitting on low seats while giving teachings to people sitting on high seats.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desessantī”ti … pe … “How could the monks from the group of six sit on low seats while giving teachings to people sitting on high seats?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desessatha. Foolish men, how can you sit on low seats while giving teachings to people sitting on high seats? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …” … vigarahitvā … pe … and after criticizing them … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— he gave a teaching and addressed the monks:

Jataka

“Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, bārāṇasiyaṃ aññatarassa chapakassa pajāpati gabbhinī ahosi. “At one time in Bārāṇasī, monks, there was a low-caste man whose wife became pregnant. Atha kho, bhikkhave, sā chapakī taṃ chapakaṃ etadavoca— She said to him, ‘gabbhinīmhi, ayyaputta. ‘I’m pregnant, sir; Icchāmi ambaṃ khāditun’ti. I crave for mangoes.’

‘Natthi ambaṃ, akālo ambassā’ti. ‘There are no mangoes; it’s the wrong season.’

‘Sace na labhissāmi marissāmī’ti. ‘If I don’t get any, I will die.’

Tena kho pana samayena rañño ambo dhuvaphalo hoti. At that time the king had a mango tree that was always bearing fruit. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so chapako yena so ambo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ ambaṃ abhiruhitvā nilīno acchi. Then that low-caste man went to that mango tree, climbed it, and hid himself. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā purohitena brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ yena so ambo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ucce āsane nisīditvā mantaṃ pariyāpuṇāti. Just then the king and his brahmin counsellor went to that same mango tree. The king then sat on a high seat while learning mantras. Atha kho, bhikkhave, tassa chapakassa etadahosi— The low-caste man thought, ‘yāva adhammiko ayaṃ rājā, yatra hi nāma ucce āsane nisīditvā mantaṃ pariyāpuṇissati. ‘How wrong-headed this king is, in that he sits on a high seat while learning mantras. Ayañca brāhmaṇo adhammiko, yatra hi nāma nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa mantaṃ vācessati. And the brahmin is wrong-headed too, in that he sits on a low seat while teaching the mantras to one sitting on a high seat. Ahañcamhi adhammiko, yohaṃ itthiyā kāraṇā rañño ambaṃ avaharāmi. And I’m wrong-headed too, in that I steal mangoes from the king because of a woman. Sabbamidaṃ carimaṃ katan’ti This is all so low!’ tattheva paripati. And he fell out of the tree right there.

The low-caste man says: ‘Ubho atthaṃ na jānanti, ‘Neither understands what is good,
ubho dhammaṃ na passare; Neither sees the Truth:
Yo cāyaṃ mantaṃ vāceti, Not he who teaches the mantras,
yo cādhammenadhīyati’. Nor he who learns improperly.’

The brahmin replies: ‘Sālīnaṃ odano bhutto, ‘I’ve eaten the finest rice,
sucimaṃsūpasecano; With a curry of pure meat:
Tasmā dhamme na vattāmi, Therefore I’m not practicing the Teaching,
dhammo ariyebhi vaṇṇito’. The Teaching praised by the Noble Ones.’

The low-caste man replies in turn: ‘Dhiratthu taṃ dhanalābhaṃ, ‘A curse it is the gaining of wealth,
yasalābhañca brāhmaṇa; And the gaining of fame, too, brahmin;
Yā vutti vinipātena, These things come with a low rebirth,
adhammacaraṇena vā. Or with wrong-headed behavior.

Paribbaja mahābrahme, ‘Go forth, great brahmin,
pacantaññepi pāṇino; Other beings will do the cooking;
Mā tvaṃ adhammo ācarito, Don’t go against the Teaching,
asmā kumbhamivābhidā’ti. Because then you will break like a jar.’

Tadāpi me, bhikkhave, amanāpā nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa mantaṃ vācetuṃ, kimaṅgaṃ pana etarahi na amanāpā bhavissati nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desetuṃ. Even at that time, monks, the teaching of mantras while sitting on a low seat to someone sitting on a high seat was displeasing to me. How then could the giving of teachings in this way not be displeasing now? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Na nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. ‘“I will not give teachings while sitting on a low seat to anyone sitting on a high seat who is not sick,” this is how you should train.’”

Na nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. One should not give teachings while sitting on a low seat to anyone sitting on a high seat who is not sick. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect gives teachings while sitting on a low seat to someone sitting on a high seat, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca … pe … if it is unintentional; if he is not mindful; if he does not know; if he is sick; if there are dangers; if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The ninth training rule is finished.
