Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Surusuruvagga The sub-chapter on slurping

56. Sasitthakasikkhāpada 56. The training rule on containing rice

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā bhaggesu viharati susumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye. At one time the Buddha was staying in the Bhagga country at Susumāragira in the Bhesakaḷā Grove, the deer park. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū kokanade pāsāde sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍenti. At that time monks in the Kokanada stilt house discarded their bowl-washing water containing rice in inhabited areas. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino”ti. “How could the Sakyan ascetics discard their bowl-washing water containing rice in inhabited areas? They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the complaints of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhū sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessantī”ti … pe … “How can those monks discard their bowl-washing water containing rice in inhabited areas?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that they do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessanti. How can those foolish men discard their bowl-washing water containing rice in inhabited areas? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Na sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. ‘“I will not discard bowl-washing water containing rice in inhabited areas,” this is how you should train.’

Na sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍetabbaṃ. One should not discard bowl-washing water containing rice in an inhabited area. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect discards bowl-washing water containing rice in an inhabited area, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; uddharitvā vā bhinditvā vā paṭiggahe vā nīharitvā vā chaḍḍeti, if he discards it after removing the rice, after breaking it up, into a receptacle, or after taking it outside; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
