Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Parimaṇḍalavagga The sub-chapter on evenly all around

3. Suppaṭicchannasikkhāpada 3. The training rule on well-covered

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kāyaṃ vivaritvā antaraghare gacchanti … At that time the monks from the group of six were walking in inhabited areas with their bodies uncovered. …

Final ruling

“Suppaṭicchanno antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will walk well-covered in inhabited areas,’ this is how you should train.”

Suppaṭicchannena antaraghare gantabbaṃ. Walking in an inhabited area, one should be well-covered. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca kāyaṃ vivaritvā antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk, out of disrespect, walks in an inhabited area with his body uncovered, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third training rule is finished.
