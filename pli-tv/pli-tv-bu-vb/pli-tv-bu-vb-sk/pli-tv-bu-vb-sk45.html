Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Kabaḷavagga The sub-chapter on mouthfuls

45. Kabaḷāvacchedakasikkhāpada 45. The training rule on breaking up a ball of food

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjanti … At that time the monks from the group of six ate by breaking up mouthfuls. …

Final ruling

“Na kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will not eat by breaking up a ball of food,’ this is how you should train.”

Na kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. One should not eat by breaking up balls of food. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk out of disrespect eats by breaking up balls of food, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; khajjake, if it is non-staple food; phalāphale, if it is any kind of fruit; uttaribhaṅge, if it is a non-bean curry; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fifth training rule is finished.
