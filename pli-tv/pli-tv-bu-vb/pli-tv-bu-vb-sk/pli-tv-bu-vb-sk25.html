Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Khambhakatavagga The sub-chapter on arms akimbo

25. Ukkuṭikasikkhāpada 25. The training rule on squatting on the heels

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gacchanti … At that time the monks from the group of six were squatting on their heels while walking in inhabited areas. …

Final ruling

“Na ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will not squat on my heels while walking in inhabited areas,’ this is how you should train.”

Na ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gantabbaṃ. Walking in an inhabited area, one should not squat on one’s heels. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca ukkuṭikāya antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk, out of disrespect, squats on his heels while walking in an inhabited area, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca … pe … if it is unintentional; if he is not mindful; if he does not know; if he is sick; if there are dangers; if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fifth training rule is finished.
