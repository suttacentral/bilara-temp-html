Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Sakkaccavagga The sub-chapter on respectfully

37. Sūpodanaviññattisikkhāpada 37. The training rule on asking for rice and bean curry

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. At that time the monks from the group of six ate rice and bean curry after asking for it for themselves. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissanti. “How could the Sakyan ascetics eat rice and bean curry after asking for it for themselves? Kassa sampannaṃ na manāpaṃ. Who doesn’t like nice food? Kassa sāduṃ na ruccatī”ti. Who doesn’t want tasty food?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the complaints of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissantī”ti … pe … “How can the monks from the group of six eat rice and bean curry after asking for it for themselves?” … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjathā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissatha. Foolish men, how can you eat rice and bean curry after asking for it for yourselves? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

“Na sūpaṃ vā odanaṃ vā attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. ‘“I will not ask for bean curry or rice for myself and then eat it,” this is how you should train.’

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū gilānā honti. Soon afterwards a number of monks were sick. Gilānapucchakā bhikkhū gilāne bhikkhū etadavocuṃ— Monks who were looking after them asked, “kaccāvuso, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyan”ti? “We hope you’re bearing up; we hope you’re getting better?”

“Pubbe mayaṃ, āvuso, sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjāma, tena no phāsu hoti. “Previously we ate rice and bean curry after asking for it, and then we were comfortable. Idāni pana— But now ‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittan’ti kukkuccāyantā na viññāpema, tena no na phāsu hotī”ti. that this has been prohibited by the Master, we don’t ask because we’re afraid of wrongdoing. And because of that we’re not comfortable.”

Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … They told the Master. … anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānena bhikkhunā sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjituṃ. “Monks, I allow a sick monk to eat rice and bean curry after asking for it for himself.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Na sūpaṃ vā odanaṃ vā agilāno attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. ‘“When not sick, I will not ask for bean curry or rice for myself and then eat it,” this is how you should train.’

Na sūpaṃ vā odanaṃ vā agilānena attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjitabbaṃ. When one is not sick, one should not ask for bean curry or rice for oneself and then eat it. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sūpaṃ vā odanaṃ vā agilāno attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk who is not sick, out of disrespect, asks for bean curry or rice for himself and then eats it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; ñātakānaṃ, if it is from relatives; pavāritānaṃ, if it is from those who have given an invitation; aññassatthāya, if it is for the benefit of someone else; attano dhanena, if it is by means of his own property; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
