Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Khambhakatavagga The sub-chapter on arms akimbo

30. Samatittikasikkhāpada 30. The training rule on even levels

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū thūpīkataṃ piṇḍapātaṃ paṭiggaṇhanti … At that time the monks from the group of six received almsfood in a heap. …

Final ruling

“Samatittikaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ paṭiggahessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will receive almsfood on an even level,’ this is how you should train.”

Samatittiko piṇḍapāto paṭiggahetabbo. Almsfood is to be received on an even level. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca thūpīkataṃ piṇḍapātaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk receives almsfood in a heap out of disrespect, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, āpadāsu, if it is unintentional; if he is not mindful; if he does not know; if he is sick; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The tenth training rule is finished.

Khambhakatavaggo tatiyo. The third chapter on arms akimbo is finished.
