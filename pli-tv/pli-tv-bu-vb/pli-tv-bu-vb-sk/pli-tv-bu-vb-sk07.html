Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Sekhiyakaṇḍa The chapter on training

Parimaṇḍalavagga The sub-chapter on evenly all around

7. Okkhittacakkhusikkhāpada 7. The training rule on lowered eyes

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tahaṃ tahaṃ olokentā antaraghare gacchanti … At that time the monks from the group of six were walking in inhabited areas, looking here and there. …

Final ruling

“Okkhittacakkhu antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā”ti. “‘I will walk with lowered eyes in inhabited areas,’ this is how you should train.”

Okkhittacakkhunā antaraghare gantabbaṃ yugamattaṃ pekkhantena. Walking in an inhabited area, one should lower one’s eyes, looking the distance of a plows-length ahead. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca tahaṃ tahaṃ olokento antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If a monk, out of disrespect, walks in an inhabited area, looking here and there, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asañcicca, if it is unintentional; assatiyā, if he is not mindful; ajānantassa, if he does not know; gilānassa, if he is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
