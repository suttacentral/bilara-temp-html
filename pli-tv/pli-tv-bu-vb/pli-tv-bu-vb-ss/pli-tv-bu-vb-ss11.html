Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

Bhedānuvattakasikkhāpada The training rule on supporting a schism

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. At one time the Buddha was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrel sanctuary. Tena kho pana samayena devadatto saṃghabhedāya parakkamati cakkabhedāya. At that time Devadatta was pursuing schism in the Order, a break in transmission. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu— The monks were saying, “adhammavādī devadatto, avinayavādī devadatto. “Devadatta doesn’t speak in accordance with the Teaching or the training. Kathañhi nāma devadatto saṃghabhedāya parakkamissati cakkabhedāyā”ti. How can he pursue schism in the Order?”

Evaṃ vutte, kokāliko kaṭamodakatissako khaṇḍadeviyā putto samuddadatto te bhikkhū etadavocuṃ— But Kokālika, Kaṭamodakatissaka, Khaṇḍadeviyā-putta, and Samuddadatta said to those monks, “māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. “No, Venerables, Dhammavādī devadatto, vinayavādī devadatto. Devadatta does speak in accordance with the Teaching and the training. Amhākañca devadatto chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākampetaṃ khamatī”ti. And he speaks with our consent and agreement. He knows about us and speaks for us, and we approve of this.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhū devadattassa saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā bhavissanti vaggavādakā”ti. “How can these monks support Devadatta’s pursuit of schism in the Order?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū te anuvattake bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … They rebuked those monks in many ways and then told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū devadattassa saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā honti vaggavādakā”ti? He said, “Is it true, monks, that some monks support Devadatta’s pursuit of schism in the Order?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā devadattassa saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā bhavissanti vaggavādakā. Monks, how can those foolish men support Devadatta’s pursuit of schism in the Order? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Tasseva kho pana bhikkhussa bhikkhū honti anuvattakā vaggavādakā eko vā dve vā tayo vā. Te evaṃ vadeyyuṃ— ‘That monk may have one, two, or three monks who side with him and support him, and they may say, ‘māyasmanto etaṃ bhikkhuṃ kiñci avacuttha. “Venerables, don’t correct this monk. Dhammavādī ceso bhikkhu, vinayavādī ceso bhikkhu. He speaks in accordance with the Teaching and the training. Amhākañceso bhikkhu chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākampetaṃ khamatī’ti, And he speaks with our consent and agreement. He knows about us and speaks for us, and we approve of this.” te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi evamassu vacanīyā— The monks should say to those monks, ‘māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha, “No, Venerables, na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī, this monk does not speak in accordance with the Teaching or the training. māyasmantānampi saṃghabhedo ruccittha, Don’t consent to schism in the Order. sametāyasmantānaṃ saṃghena, samaggo hi saṃgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī’ti. Stay with the Order, for a united Order—in concord, in harmony, having a joint recitation—lives in comfort.” Evañca te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi vuccamānā tatheva paggaṇheyyuṃ, te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi yāvatatiyaṃ samanubhāsitabbā tassa paṭinissaggāya. If those monks still continue as before, the monks should press them up to three times to make them stop. Yāvatatiyañce samanubhāsiyamānā taṃ paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ, If they then stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, saṃghādiseso”ti. If not, they commit an offense entailing suspension.’”

Definitions

Tasseva kho panāti That:
tassa saṃghabhedakassa bhikkhuno. that monk who is pursuing schism in the Order.
Bhikkhū hontīti May have […] monks:
aññe bhikkhū honti. may have other monks.
Anuvattakāti Who side with him:
yaṃdiṭṭhiko so hoti yaṃkhantiko yaṃruciko tepi taṃdiṭṭhikā honti taṃkhantikā taṃrucikā. they have the same view, the same belief, the same preference as he does.
Vaggavādakāti Who […] support him:
tassa vaṇṇāya pakkhāya ṭhitā honti. they take his side and are part of his group.
Eko vā dve vā tayo vāti One, two, or three:
eko vā hoti dve vā tayo vā. Te evaṃ vadeyyuṃ— There is one, or two, or three. They may say, “māyasmanto etaṃ bhikkhuṃ kiñci avacuttha, dhammavādī ceso bhikkhu, vinayavādī ceso bhikkhu, “Venerables, don’t correct this monk. He speaks in accordance with the Teaching and the training. amhākañceso bhikkhu chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākampetaṃ khamatī”ti. And he speaks with our consent and agreement. He knows about us and speaks for us, and we approve of this.”
Te bhikkhūti Those monks:
ye te anuvattakā bhikkhū. those monks who side with him.
Bhikkhūhīti The monks:

aññehi bhikkhūhi. Ye passanti ye suṇanti tehi vattabbā— Other monks, those who see it or hear about it. They should say,

“māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. “No, Venerables, Na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī. this monk does not speak in accordance with the Teaching or the training. Māyasmantānampi saṃghabhedo ruccittha. Don’t consent to schism in the Order. Sametāyasmantānaṃ saṃghena. Stay with the Order, Samaggo hi saṃgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī”ti. for a united Order—in concord, in harmony, having a joint recitation—lives in comfort.”

Dutiyampi vattabbā. And they should say this a second Tatiyampi vattabbā. and a third time. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; If they stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If they do not stop, they commit an offense of wrong conduct. Sutvā na vadanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If those who hear about it do not say anything, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Te bhikkhū saṃghamajjhampi ākaḍḍhitvā vattabbā— Those monks, even if they have to be dragged into the middle of the Order, should be to spoken to thus:

“māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. “No, Venerables, Na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī. this monk does not speak in accordance with the Teaching or the training. Māyasmantānampi saṃghabhedo ruccittha. Don’t consent to schism in the Order. Sametāyasmantānaṃ saṃghena. Stay with the Order, Samaggo hi saṃgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī”ti. for a united Order—in concord, in harmony, having a joint recitation—lives in comfort.”

Dutiyampi vattabbā. They should say this a second Tatiyampi vattabbā. and a third time. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; If they stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If they do not stop, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Te bhikkhū samanubhāsitabbā. Should press them:

“Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samanubhāsitabbā. “And, monks, they should be pressed in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Itthannāmo ca itthanāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Monks so-and-so and so-and-so are siding with him and supporting monk so-and-so who is pursuing schism in the Order. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. They are not stopping that activity. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāseyya tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should press them to make them stop. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Monks so-and-so and so-and-so are siding with and supporting monk so-and-so who is pursuing schism in the Order. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. They are not stopping that activity. Saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. The Order presses them to make them stop. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa ca itthannāmassa ca bhikkhūnaṃ samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of pressing them to make them stop should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi … pe … For the second time … tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi— For the third time I speak on this matter: suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṃghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Monks so-and-so and so-and-so are siding with and supporting monk so-and-so who is pursuing schism in the Order. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. They are not stopping that activity. Saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. The Order presses them to make them stop. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa ca itthannāmassa ca bhikkhūnaṃ samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of pressing them to make them stop should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Samanubhaṭṭhā saṃghena itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. The monks so-and-so and so-and-so have been pressed by the Order to make them stop that activity. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, The Order approves and is therefore silent. evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. I will remember it thus.’”

Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, After the motion, they commit an offense of wrong conduct. dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā, After each of the first two announcements, they commit a serious offense. kammavācāpariyosāne āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When the last announcement is finished, they commit an offense entailing suspension. Saṃghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjantānaṃ ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā paṭippassambhanti. For those who commit the offense entailing suspension, the offense of wrong conduct and the serious offenses are annulled. Dve tayo ekato samanubhāsitabbā, taduttari na samanubhāsitabbā. Two or three can be pressed together, but not more than that.

Saṃghādisesoti They commit an offense entailing suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati “saṃghādiseso”ti. … Therefore, too, it is called “an offense entailing suspension.”

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and they perceive it as legitimate, but they do not stop, they commit an offense entailing suspension.

Dhammakamme vematikā na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but they are unsure if it is, and they do not stop, they commit an offense entailing suspension.

Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but they perceive it as illegitimate, and they do not stop, they commit an offense entailing suspension.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but they perceive it as legitimate, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Adhammakamme vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but they are unsure if it is, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and they perceive it as illegitimate, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asamanubhāsantānaṃ, if they have not been pressed; paṭinissajjantānaṃ, if they stop; ummattakānaṃ, if they are insane; khittacittānaṃ, if they are deranged; vedanāṭṭānaṃ, if they are overwhelmed by pain; ādikammikānanti. if they are the first offenders.

Bhedānuvattakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ ekādasamaṃ. The eleventh rule, the training rule on supporting a schism, is finished.
