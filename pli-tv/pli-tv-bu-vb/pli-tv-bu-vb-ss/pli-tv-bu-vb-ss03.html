Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

Duṭṭhullavācāsikkhāpada The training rule on lewd speech

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī araññe viharati. At that time Venerable Udāyī was staying in the wilderness, Tassāyasmato vihāro abhirūpo hoti dassanīyo pāsādiko. in a beautiful dwelling. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā itthiyo ārāmaṃ āgamaṃsu vihārapekkhikāyo. Just then a number of women came to the monastery to see the dwellings. Atha kho tā itthiyo yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; They approached Udāyī upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavocuṃ— and said, “icchāma mayaṃ, bhante, ayyassa vihāraṃ pekkhitun”ti. “Venerable, we would like to see your dwelling.”

Atha kho āyasmā udāyī tā itthiyo vihāraṃ pekkhāpetvā tāsaṃ itthīnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati yācatipi āyācatipi pucchatipi paṭipucchatipi ācikkhatipi anusāsatipi akkosatipi. Then, after showing them his own dwelling, he praised and disparaged their private parts; he requested and implored, asked and enquired, described, instructed, and abused. Yā tā itthiyo chinnikā dhuttikā ahirikāyo tā āyasmatā udāyinā saddhiṃ uhasantipi ullapantipi ujjagghantipi uppaṇḍentipi. Those women among them who were wanton and shameless flirted with Udāyī; they called out to him, laughed with him, and made fun of him. Yā pana tā itthiyo hirimanā tā nikkhamitvā bhikkhū ujjhāpenti— But, after leaving, those among them who had a sense of shame complained to the monks, “idaṃ, bhante, nacchannaṃ nappatirūpaṃ. “Venerables, this isn’t suitable and appropriate. Sāmikenapi mayaṃ evaṃ vuttā na iccheyyāma, kiṃ panāyyena udāyinā”ti. We wouldn’t want to hear this from our own husbands, let alone from Venerable Udāyī.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsissatī”ti. “How can Venerable Udāyī speak lewdly to women?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They rebuked Udāyī in many ways and told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṃghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ paṭipucchi— And the Master had the Order of monks assembled and questioned Udāyī, “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsasī”ti? “Is it true, Udāyī, that you spoke lewdly to women?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā— The Buddha rebuked him, “ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. “It’s not suitable, foolish man, it’s not proper, it’s not worthy of an ascetic, it’s not allowable, it should not to be done. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsissasi. How can you speak lewdly to women? Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito no sarāgāya … pe … Haven’t I given many teachings for the sake of dispassion, not for the sake of passion … kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto. for the stilling of the fevers of sense desire? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāseyya yathā taṃ yuvā yuvatiṃ methunupasaṃhitāhi, saṃghādiseso”ti. ‘If a lustful monk speaks lewd words connected with sexual intercourse to a woman, as a young man might to a young woman, he commits an offense entailing suspension.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Otiṇṇo nāma Lustful:
sāratto apekkhavā paṭibaddhacitto. having lust, longing for, in love with.
Vipariṇatanti
rattampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ, duṭṭhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ mūḷhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ. Api ca rattaṃ cittaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetaṃ vipariṇatanti.
Mātugāmo nāma A woman:
manussitthī, na yakkhī, na petī, na tiracchānagatā. Viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ. a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal. She understands and is capable of discerning bad speech and good speech, what is lewd and what is not lewd.
Duṭṭhullā nāma Lewd words:
vācā vaccamaggapassāvamaggamethunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttā vācā. speech connected with the anus, the vagina, or sexual intercourse.
Obhāseyyāti Speaks:
ajjhācāro vuccati. misconduct is what is meant.
Yathā taṃ yuvā yuvatinti As a young man might to a young woman:
daharo dahariṃ, taruṇo taruṇiṃ, kāmabhogī kāmabhoginiṃ. a male youth to a female youth, a boy to a girl, a male indulging in sense pleasures to a female indulging in sense pleasures.
Methunupasaṃhitāhīti Connected with sexual intercourse:
methunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttāhi. connected with the sexual act.
Saṃghādisesoti He commits an offense entailing suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati saṃghādisesoti. … Therefore, too, it is called “an offense entailing suspension.”

Permutations

Summary

Dve magge ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati, avaṇṇampi bhaṇati, yācatipi, āyācatipi, pucchatipi, paṭipucchatipi, ācikkhatipi, anusāsatipi, akkosatipi. Referring to the anus or the vagina, he praises, disparages, requests, implores, asks, enquires, describes, instructs, abuses.

Sub-definitions

Vaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati nāma Praises:
dve magge thometi vaṇṇeti pasaṃsati. extols, praises, commends the two private orifices.
Avaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati nāma Disparages:
dve magge khuṃseti vambheti garahati. despises, reviles, censures the the anus or the vagina.
Yācati nāma Requests:
dehi me, arahasi me dātunti. saying, “Give to me; you should to give to me.”
Āyācati nāma Implores:
kadā te mātā pasīdissati, saying, “When will you please your mother? kadā te pitā pasīdissati, When will you please your father? kadā te devatāyo pasīdissanti, When will you please the gods? kadā sukhaṇo sulayo sumuhutto bhavissati, When will there be a good opportunity, a good time, a good moment? kadā te methunaṃ dhammaṃ labhissāmīti. When will I have sexual intercourse with you?”
Pucchati nāma Asks:
kathaṃ tvaṃ sāmikassa desi, saying, “How do you give to your husband? kathaṃ jārassa desīti? How do you give to your lover?”
Paṭipucchati nāma Enquires:
evaṃ kira tvaṃ sāmikassa desi, saying, “So you give to your husband like this, evaṃ jārassa desīti. and to your lover like this?”
Ācikkhati nāma Describes:
puṭṭho bhaṇati— “evaṃ dehi. Evaṃ dentā sāmikassa piyā bhavissati manāpā cā”ti. having been asked, he says, “Give like this. When you do, you’ll be dear and pleasing to your husband.”
Anusāsati nāma Instructs:
apuṭṭho bhaṇati— “evaṃ dehi. Evaṃ dentā sāmikassa piyā bhavissati manāpā cā”ti. not having been asked, he says, “Give like this. When you do, you’ll be dear and pleasing to your husband.”
Akkosati nāma Abuses:
animittāsi, saying, “You lack genitals; nimittamattāsi, you have incomplete genitals; alohitāsi, you don’t menstruate; dhuvalohitāsi, you menstruate continuously; dhuvacoḷāsi, you always use a menstruation pad; paggharantīsi, you’re dripping; sikharaṇīsi, you have uterine prolapse; itthipaṇḍakāsi, you lack sexual organs; vepurisikāsi, you are manlike; sambhinnāsi, your anus and vagina are conjoined; ubhatobyañjanāsīti. you’re a hermaphrodite.”

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: atthapurekkhārassa, if he is aiming at what is beneficial; dhammapurekkhārassa, if he is aiming at giving a teaching; anusāsanipurekkhārassa, if he is aiming at giving instruction; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kammaṃ karoti. At one time a woman was doing some work. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca— A monk, being lustful, said to her, “tiṭṭha, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmī”ti … pe … “Stand, Sister, I’ll work.” … nisīda, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmī”ti … pe … “Sit, Sister, I’ll work.” … nipajja, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmī”ti. “Lie down, Sister, I’ll work.” Sā na paṭivijāni. She did not understand. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi … pe … He became remorseful … “anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṃghādisesassa; “There’s no offense entailing suspension, āpatti dukkaṭassā”ti. #10–‍12 but there’s an offense of wrong conduct.”

Duṭṭhullavācāsikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ. The third rule, the training rule on lewd speech, is finished.
