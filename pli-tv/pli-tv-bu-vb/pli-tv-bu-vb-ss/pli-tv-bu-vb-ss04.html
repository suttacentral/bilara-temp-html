Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

Attakāmapāricariyasikkhāpada The training rule on fulfilling one’s own desires

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī sāvatthiyaṃ kulūpako hoti, bahukāni kulāni upasaṅkamati. At that time Venerable Udāyī was associating with and visiting a number of families in Sāvatthī. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī matapatikā abhirūpā hoti dassanīyā pāsādikā. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena tassā itthiyā nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. One morning Udāyī robed up, took his bowl and robe, and went to the house of a beautiful widow, where he sat down on the prepared seat. Atha kho sā itthī yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. That woman approached Udāyī, bowed, and sat down. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho taṃ itthiṃ āyasmā udāyī dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Udāyī then instructed, inspired, and gladdened her with a teaching. Atha kho sā itthī āyasmatā udāyinā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca— And she said, “vadeyyātha, bhante, yena attho. “Venerable, please say what you need. Paṭibalā mayaṃ ayyassa dātuṃ yadidaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāran”ti. I’m able to give robe-cloth, almsfood, a dwelling, and medicines.”

“Na kho te, bhagini, amhākaṃ dullabhā yadidaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā. “It’s not hard for us, Sister, to get those requisites. Api ca yo amhākaṃ dullabho taṃ dehī”ti. Give instead what’s hard for us to get.”

“Kiṃ, bhante”ti? “What’s that, Venerable?”

“Methunadhamman”ti. “Sexual intercourse.”

“Attho, bhante”ti? “Is it wanted now, Venerable?”

“Attho, bhaginī”ti. “It is, Sister.”

“Ehi, bhante”ti, “Come, Venerable,” ovarakaṃ pavisitvā sāṭakaṃ nikkhipitvā mañcake uttānā nipajji. and she entered her bedroom, took off her clothes, and lay back on the bed. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī yena sā itthī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā— Udāyī approached her and spat out, “ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī”ti, “Who would touch this foul-smelling wretch!” niṭṭhubhitvā pakkāmi. and he left.

Atha kho sā itthī ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— That woman then complained and criticized him, “alajjino ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā dussīlā musāvādino. “These Sakyan ascetics are shameless and immoral liars. Ime hi nāma dhammacārino samacārino brahmacārino saccavādino sīlavanto kalyāṇadhammā paṭijānissanti. They claim to live according to the Truth, to be celibate and of good conduct, to be truthful, moral, and of good character. Natthi imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ natthi imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, naṭṭhaṃ imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ naṭṭhaṃ imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, kuto imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ kuto imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, But they don’t have the qualities of an ascetic or a brahmin; apagatā ime sāmaññā apagatā ime brahmaññā. they’ve lost the plot. Kathañhi nāma samaṇo udāyī maṃ sāmaṃ methunadhammaṃ yācitvā, ‘ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī’ti niṭṭhubhitvā pakkamissati. How can the ascetic Udāyī request sexual intercourse from me, but then spit out, ‘Who would touch this foul-smelling wretch!’ and depart? Kiṃ me pāpakaṃ kiṃ me duggandhaṃ, What’s bad about me? How am I foul-smelling? kassāhaṃ kena hāyāmī”ti? In what way am I inferior to whom?”

Aññāpi itthiyo ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— Other women, too, criticized him “alajjino ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā dussīlā musāvādino … pe … in the same way, kathañhi nāma samaṇo udāyī imissā sāmaṃ methunadhammaṃ yācitvā, ‘ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī’ti niṭṭhubhitvā pakkamissati. Kiṃ imissā pāpakaṃ kiṃ imissā duggandhaṃ, kassāyaṃ kena hāyatī”ti?

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tāsaṃ itthīnaṃ ujjhāyantīnaṃ khiyyantīnaṃ vipācentīnaṃ. The monks heard the criticism of those women, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized him, “kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissatī”ti. “How can Venerable Udāyī commend the fulfilling of his own desires in the presence of a woman?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Those monks rebuked Udāyī in many ways and then told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṃghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ paṭipucchi— The Master had the Order of monks assembled and questioned Udāyī, “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsasī”ti? “Is it true, Udāyī, that you commended the fulfilling of your own desires in the presence of a woman?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā— The Buddha rebuked him, “ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. “It’s not suitable, foolish man, it’s not proper, it’s not worthy of an ascetic, it’s not allowable, it’s not to be done. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissasi. How can you commend the fulfilling of your own desires in the presence of a woman? Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito no sarāgāya … pe … Haven’t I given many teachings for the sake of dispassion, not for the sake of passion … kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto? for the stilling of the fevers of sense desire? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāseyya— ‘etadaggaṃ, bhagini, pāricariyānaṃ yā mādisaṃ sīlavantaṃ kalyāṇadhammaṃ brahmacāriṃ etena dhammena paricareyyāti methunupasaṃhitena’, saṃghādiseso”ti. ‘If a lustful monk encourages a woman to satisfy his own needs: “Sister, she provides the highest service who in this way satisfies one like me, who is virtuous, celibate, and of good character,” and if it is a reference to sexual intercourse, he commits an offense entailing suspension.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Otiṇṇo nāma Lustful:
sāratto apekkhavā paṭibaddhacitto. having lust, longing for, in love with.
Vipariṇatanti
rattampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ, duṭṭhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ, mūḷhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ. Api ca rattaṃ cittaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetaṃ vipariṇatanti.
Mātugāmo nāma A woman:
manussitthī, na yakkhī, na petī, na tiracchānagatā. Viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ. a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal. She understands and is capable of discerning bad speech and good speech, what is lewd and what is not lewd.
Mātugāmassa santiketi
mātugāmassa sāmantā, mātugāmassa avidūre.
Attakāmanti His own needs:
attano kāmaṃ attano hetuṃ attano adhippāyaṃ attano pāricariyaṃ. his own sense desires, for the sake of himself, aiming at himself, satisfying himself.
Etadagganti The highest:
etaṃ aggaṃ etaṃ seṭṭhaṃ etaṃ mokkhaṃ etaṃ uttamaṃ etaṃ pavaraṃ. this is the highest, this is the best, this is the foremost, this is the utmost, this is the most excellent.
Yāti She:
khattiyī vā brāhmaṇī vā vessī vā suddī vā. a female aristocrat, a female brahmin, a female merchant, or a female worker.
Mādisanti One like me:
khattiyaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā vessaṃ vā suddaṃ vā. a male aristocrat, a male brahmin, a male merchant, or a male worker.
Sīlavantanti Who is virtuous:
pāṇātipātā paṭivirataṃ, adinnādānā paṭivirataṃ, musāvādā paṭivirataṃ. one who refrains from killing living beings, who refrains from stealing, who refrains from lying.
Brahmacārinti Celibate:
methunadhammā paṭivirataṃ. one who refrains from sexual intercourse.
Kalyāṇadhammo nāma Of good character:
tena ca sīlena tena ca brahmacariyena kalyāṇadhammo hoti. he is one of good character because of that virtue and because of being celibate.
Etena dhammenāti In this way:
methunadhammena. with sexual intercourse.
Paricareyyāti Satisfies:
abhirameyya. gives pleasure to.
Methunupasaṃhitenāti If it is a reference to sexual intercourse:
methunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttena. if it is a reference to the sexual act.
Saṃghādisesoti He commits an offense entailing suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati “saṃghādiseso”ti. … Therefore, too, it is called “an offense entailing suspension.”

Permutations

Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. It is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If the monk encourages the woman to satisfy his own needs, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Itthī ca hoti vematiko … pe … It is a woman, but he is unsure of it … paṇḍakasaññī … It is a woman, but he perceives her as a eunuch … purisasaññī … It is a woman, but he perceives her as a man … tiracchānagatasaññī sāratto ca. It is a woman, but he perceives her as an animal, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti thullaccayassa. If the monk encourages the woman to satisfy his own needs, he commits a serious offense.

Paṇḍako ca hoti paṇḍakasaññī sāratto ca. It is a eunuch, he perceives him as a eunuch, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ paṇḍakassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti thullaccayassa. If the monk encourages the eunuch to satisfy his own needs, he commits a serious offense.

Paṇḍako ca hoti vematiko … pe … It is a eunuch, but he is unsure of it … purisasaññī … It is a eunuch, but he perceives him as a man … tiracchānagatasaññī … It is a eunuch, but he perceives him as an animal … itthisaññī sāratto ca. It is a eunuch, but he perceives him as a woman, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ paṇḍakassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the monk encourages the eunuch to satisfy his own needs, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Puriso ca hoti It is a man. … pe … This section on “It is a man” is to be expanded as in [Saṅghādisesa 2, paragraphs 40–41](pli-tv-bu-vb-ss2#40). tiracchānagato ca hoti tiracchānagatasaññī … pe … It is an animal, he perceives it as an animal … vematiko … It is an animal, but he is unsure of it … itthisaññī … It is an animal, but he perceives it as a woman … paṇḍakasaññī … It is an animal, but he perceives it as a eunuch … purisasaññī sāratto ca. It is an animal, but he perceives it as a man, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ tiracchānagatassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the monk encourages the animal to satisfy his own needs, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dvinnaṃ saṃghādisesānaṃ. If the monk encourages both women to satisfy his own needs, he commits two offenses entailing suspension. … pe … To be expanded as in [Saṅghādisesa 2, paragraphs 45–53](pli-tv-bu-vb-ss2#45).

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. It is a woman and a eunuch, he perceives both as women, and he has lust. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti saṃghādisesena dukkaṭassa. If the monk encourages both of them to satisfy his own needs, he commits one offense entailing suspension and one offense of wrong conduct. … pe … To be expanded as in [Saṅghādisesa 2, paragraphs 54–69](pli-tv-bu-vb-ss2#54).

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: “cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena upaṭṭhahā”ti bhaṇati, if he says, “Attend on us with robe-cloth, almsfood, a dwelling, and medicines;” ummattakassa if he is insane; uādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.
