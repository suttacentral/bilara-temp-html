Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

Vihārakārasikkhāpada The training rule on building dwellings

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato channassa upaṭṭhāko gahapati āyasmantaṃ channaṃ etadavoca— At that time a lay person who was a supporter of Venerable Channa said to him, “vihāravatthuṃ, bhante, jānāhi ayyassa vihāraṃ kārāpessāmī”ti. “Venerable, please find a site for a dwelling, and I’ll have one built for you.”

Atha kho āyasmā channo vihāravatthuṃ sodhento aññataraṃ cetiyarukkhaṃ chedāpesi gāmapūjitaṃ nigamapūjitaṃ nagarapūjitaṃ janapadapūjitaṃ raṭṭhapūjitaṃ. While Venerable Channa was clearing a site for that dwelling, he had a tree felled that had served as a shrine and that was revered by village, town, district, and kingdom. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People grumbled and complained, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā cetiyarukkhaṃ chedāpessanti gāmapūjitaṃ nigamapūjitaṃ nagarapūjitaṃ janapadapūjitaṃ raṭṭhapūjitaṃ. “How can the Sakyan ascetics have such a tree felled? Ekindriyaṃ samaṇā sakyaputtiyā jīvaṃ viheṭhentī”ti. They are hurting life with one faculty.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the criticism of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized “kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo cetiyarukkhaṃ chedāpessati gāmapūjitaṃ … pe … raṭṭhapūjitan”ti. Venerable Channa in the same manner.

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing Venerable Channa in various ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, cetiyarukkhaṃ chedāpesi gāmapūjitaṃ … pe … raṭṭhapūjitan”ti? He said, “Is it true, Channa, that you had a tree felled that had served as a shrine and that was revered by village, town, district, and kingdom?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, cetiyarukkhaṃ chedāpessasi gāmapūjitaṃ nigamapūjitaṃ nagarapūjitaṃ janapadapūjitaṃ raṭṭhapūjitaṃ. “… Foolish man, how can you do such a thing? Jīvasaññino hi, moghapurisa, manussā rukkhasmiṃ. People perceive trees as living beings. Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Mahallakaṃ pana bhikkhunā vihāraṃ kārayamānena sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ bhikkhū abhinetabbā vatthudesanāya. ‘When a monk builds a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for himself, Tehi bhikkhūhi vatthu desetabbaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ. he must get monks to approve a site where no harm will be done and which has a space on all sides. Sārambhe ce bhikkhu vatthusmiṃ aparikkamane mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kāreyya bhikkhū vā anabhineyya vatthudesanāya, saṃghādiseso”ti. If a monk builds a large dwelling on a site where harm will be done and which lacks a space on all sides, or he does not get monks to approve the site, he commits an offense entailing suspension.’”

Definitions

Mahallako nāma A large dwelling:
vihāro sassāmiko vuccati. one with a sponsoring owner is what is meant.
Vihāro nāma Dwelling:
ullitto vā hoti avalitto vā ullittāvalitto vā. plastered inside or plastered outside or plastered both inside and outside.
Kārayamānenāti Builds:
karonto vā kārāpento vā. building it himself or getting someone else to build it.
Sassāmikanti With a sponsoring owner:
añño koci sāmiko hoti itthī vā puriso vā gahaṭṭho vā pabbajito vā. there is another owner, either a woman or a man, either a lay person or one gone forth.
Attuddesanti Intended for himself:
attano atthāya. for his own use.
Bhikkhū abhinetabbā vatthudesanāyāti He must get monks to approve a site:

tena vihārakārakena bhikkhunā vihāravatthuṃ sodhetvā saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— The monk who is building a dwelling should clear a site, then approach the Order, put his upper robe over one shoulder, pay respect at the feet of the senior monks, squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say:

“ahaṃ, bhante, mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. “Venerables, I wish to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for myself. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṃghaṃ vihāravatthuolokanaṃ yācāmī”ti. I request the Order to inspect the site for the dwelling.”

Dutiyampi yācitabbā. He should make his request a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time. Sace sabbo saṃgho ussahati vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ sabbena saṃghena oloketabbaṃ. If the whole Order is able to inspect the site, they should all go. No ce sabbo saṃgho ussahati vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ, If the whole Order is not able to inspect the site, ye tattha honti bhikkhū byattā paṭibalā sārambhaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ aparikkamanaṃ jānituṃ te yācitvā sammannitabbā. then those monks there who are competent and capable—who know where harm will be done and where no harm will be done, and who understands the meaning of space on all sides and a lacks a space on all sides—should be asked and then appointed.

“Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbā. “And, monks, they should be appointed in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Monk so-and-so wishes to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for himself. So saṃghaṃ vihāravatthuolokanaṃ yācati. He is requesting the Order to inspect the site for the dwelling. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū sammanneyya itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should appoint monk so-and-so and monk so-and-so to inspect the site. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Monk so-and-so wishes to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for himself. So saṃghaṃ vihāravatthuolokanaṃ yācati. He is requesting the Order to inspect the site for the dwelling. Saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū sammannati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ. The Order is appointing monk so-and-so and monk so-and-so to inspect the site. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa ca itthannāmassa ca bhikkhūnaṃ sammuti itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of appointing monk so-and-so and monk so-and-so to inspect the site for the dwelling should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Sammatā saṃghena itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ oloketuṃ. Monk so-and-so and monk so-and-so have been appointed by the Order to inspect the site for the dwelling of monk so-and-so. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, The Order approves and is therefore silent. evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. I will remember it thus.’

Tehi sammatehi bhikkhūhi tattha gantvā vihāravatthu oloketabbaṃ; The appointed monks should go and inspect the site for the dwelling sārambhaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ aparikkamanaṃ jānitabbaṃ. to find out if any harm will be done and if it has a space on all sides. Sace sārambhaṃ hoti aparikkamanaṃ, If harm will be done and it lacks a space on all sides, they should say, “māyidha karī”ti vattabbo. ‘Don’t build here.’ Sace anārambhaṃ hoti saparikkamanaṃ, saṃghassa ārocetabbaṃ— If no harm will be done and it has a space on all sides, they should inform the Order: “anārambhaṃ saparikkamanan”ti. ‘No harm will be done and it has a space on all sides.’ Tena vihārakārakena bhikkhunā saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— The monk who is building the dwelling should then approach the Order, put his upper robe over one shoulder, pay respect at the feet of the senior monks, squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say:

“ahaṃ, bhante, mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. ‘Venerables, I wish to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for myself. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṃghaṃ vihāravatthudesanaṃ yācāmī”ti. I request the Order to approve the site for the dwelling.’

Dutiyampi yācitabbā. He should make his request a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should then inform the Order:

“Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Monk so-and-so wishes to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for himself. So saṃghaṃ vihāravatthudesanaṃ yācati. He is requesting the Order to approve the site for the dwelling. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ deseyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should approve the site. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kattukāmo sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Monk so-and-so wishes to build a large dwelling with a sponsoring owner and intended for himself. So saṃghaṃ vihāravatthudesanaṃ yācati. He is requesting the Order to approve the site for the dwelling. Saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthuṃ deseti. The Order approves the site for the dwelling of monk so-and-so. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthussa desanā, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of approving the site for the dwelling should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Desitaṃ saṃghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihāravatthu. The site for the dwelling of monk so-and-so has been approved by the Order. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, The Order approves and is therefore silent. evametaṃ dhārayāmī”ti. I will remember it thus.’”

Sārambhaṃ nāma Where harm will be done:
kipillikānaṃ vā āsayo hoti, upacikānaṃ vā āsayo hoti, undūrānaṃ vā … pe … ahīnaṃ vā … vicchikānaṃ vā … satapadīnaṃ vā … hatthīnaṃ vā … assānaṃ vā … sīhānaṃ vā … byagghānaṃ vā … dīpīnaṃ vā … acchānaṃ vā … taracchānaṃ vā āsayo hoti, yesaṃ kesañci tiracchānagatānaṃ pāṇānaṃ āsayo hoti, it is the abode of ants, termites, rats, snakes, scorpions, centipedes, elephants, horses, lions, tigers, leopards, bears, or hyenas, or any other animals; pubbaṇṇanissitaṃ vā hoti, aparaṇṇanissitaṃ vā hoti, abbhāghātanissitaṃ vā hoti, āghātananissitaṃ vā hoti, susānanissitaṃ vā hoti, uyyānanissitaṃ vā hoti, rājavatthunissitaṃ vā hoti, hatthisālānissitaṃ vā hoti, assasālānissitaṃ vā hoti, bandhanāgāranissitaṃ vā hoti, pānāgāranissitaṃ vā hoti, sūnanissitaṃ vā hoti, racchānissitaṃ vā hoti, caccaranissitaṃ vā hoti, sabhānissitaṃ vā hoti, saṃsaraṇanissitaṃ vā hoti. or it is bordering on a field of grain, a field of vegetables, a slaughtering-place, a place of execution, a charnel ground, a park, a king’s property, an elephant-stable, a horse-stable, a prison, a bar, a slaughterhouse, a vehicle road, a crossroads, a public meeting hall, or a cul-de-sac— Etaṃ sārambhaṃ nāma. this is called “where harm will be done.”
Aparikkamanaṃ nāma Which lacks a space on all sides:
na sakkā hoti yathāyuttena sakaṭena anuparigantuṃ, samantā nisseṇiyā anuparigantuṃ. it is not possible to go around it with a yoked cart, or to go all the way around it with a ladder— Etaṃ aparikkamanaṃ nāma. this is called “which lacks a space on all sides.”
Anārambhaṃ nāma Where no harm will be done:
na kipillikānaṃ vā āsayo hoti … pe … it is not the abode of ants … na saṃsaraṇanissitaṃ vā hoti. it is not bordering on … a cul-de-sac— Etaṃ anārambhaṃ nāma. this is called “where no harm will be done.”
Saparikkamanaṃ nāma Which has a space on all sides:
sakkā hoti yathāyuttena sakaṭena anuparigantuṃ, samantā nisseṇiyā anuparigantuṃ. it is possible to go around it with a yoked cart, or to go all the way around it with a ladder— Etaṃ saparikkamanaṃ nāma. this is called “which has a space on all sides.”
Mahallako nāma A large dwelling:
vihāro sassāmiko vuccati. one with a sponsoring owner is what is meant.
Vihāro nāma Dwelling:
ullitto vā hoti avalitto vā ullittāvalitto vā. plastered inside or plastered outside or plastered both inside and outside.
Kāreyyāti Builds:
karoti vā kārāpeti vā. building it himself or getting someone else to build it.
Bhikkhū vā anabhineyya vatthudesanāyāti Or he does not get monks to approve the site:
ñattidutiyena kammena vihāravatthuṃ na desāpetvā karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. if the site has not been approved through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements, and he then builds a dwelling or gets one built, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Ekaṃ piṇḍaṃ anāgate, āpatti thullaccayassa. When one piece is left to complete the dwelling, he commits a serious offense. Tasmiṃ piṇḍe āgate, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When the last piece is finished, he commits an offense entailing suspension.
Saṃghādisesoti He commits an offense entailing suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati— “saṃghādiseso”ti. … Therefore, too, it is called “an offense entailing suspension.”

Permutations part 1

Building oneself

Bhikkhu vihāraṃ karoti adesitavatthukaṃ sārambhaṃ aparikkamanaṃ, āpatti saṃghādisesena dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ. If a monk builds a dwelling whose site has not been approved, where harm will be done, and which lacks a space on all sides, he commits one offense entailing suspension and two offenses of wrong conduct. Departing and then hearing about wrong building procedure

Bhikkhu samādisitvā pakkamati— A monk appoints someone “vihāraṃ me karothā”ti. to build him a dwelling and then departs. Samādisati ca— He tells them “desitavatthuko ca hotu anārambho ca saparikkamano cā”ti. to build one whose site has been approved, where no harm will be done, and which has a space on all sides, Tassa vihāraṃ karonti adesitavatthukaṃ sārambhaṃ aparikkamanaṃ. but they build one whose site has not been approved, where harm will be done, and which lacks a space on all sides. No ce sāmaṃ vā gaccheyya dūtaṃ vā pahiṇeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he neither goes himself nor sends a message, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Permutations part 2

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: leṇe guhāya tiṇakuṭikāya if it is a shelter, if it is a cave, if it is a grass hut; aññassatthāya vāsāgāraṃ ṭhapetvā if it is built for someone else; sabbattha. if it is anything apart from a dwelling; Anāpatti ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Vihārakārasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ. The seventh rule, the training rule on building dwellings, is finished.
