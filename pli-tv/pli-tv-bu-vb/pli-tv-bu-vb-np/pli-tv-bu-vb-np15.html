Nissaggiyakaṇḍa The chapter on relinquishment

Kosiyavagga The sub-chapter on silk

15. Nisīdanasanthatasikkhāpada 15. The training rule on sitting-blankets

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi—“icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. There the Master addressed the monks, “Monks, I wish to go into solitary retreat for three months. Namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā”ti. No one is to approach me except the one who brings me almsfood.”

“Evaṃ, bhante”ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissuṇitvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena. “Yes, Master,” they replied, and no one approached him except the one who brought him almsfood.

Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyā saṃghena katikā katā hoti— At that time the Order at Sāvatthī made the following agreement: “icchatāvuso, bhagavā temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. “The Master wishes to be on solitary retreat for three months. Na bhagavā kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena. No one is to approach him except the one who brings him almsfood. Yo bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati so pācittiyaṃ desāpetabbo”ti. Anyone who approaches him should be made to confess an offense entailing confession.”

Atha kho āyasmā upaseno vaṅgantaputto, sapariso yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Just then Venerable Upasena Vaṅgantaputta and his followers approached the Master, bowed, and sat down. Āciṇṇaṃ kho panetaṃ buddhānaṃ bhagavantānaṃ āgantukehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ paṭisammodituṃ. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ upasenaṃ vaṅgantaputtaṃ etadavoca— Since it is the custom for Buddhas to greet newly arrived monks, the Master said this to Upasena, “kacci vo, upasena, khamanīyaṃ kacci yāpanīyaṃ, “I hope you’re keeping well, Upasena, I hope you’re comfortable; kaccittha appakilamathena addhānaṃ āgatā”ti? I hope you’re not tired from traveling.”

“Khamanīyaṃ, bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ, bhagavā. “We’re keeping well, Master, we’re comfortable; Appakilamathena ca mayaṃ, bhante, addhānaṃ āgatā”ti. we’re not tired from traveling.”

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato upasenassa vaṅgantaputtassa saddhivihāriko bhikkhu bhagavato avidūre nisinno hoti. Atha kho bhagavā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca— One of Upasena’s students was seated not far from the Master, and the Master said to him, “manāpāni te, bhikkhu, paṃsukūlānī”ti? “Do you like rag-robes, monk?”

“Na kho me, bhante, manāpāni paṃsukūlānī”ti. “I don’t like rag-robes, Venerable Sir.”

“Kissa pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, paṃsukūliko”ti? “Why then do you wear them?”

“Upajjhāyo me, bhante, paṃsukūliko. Evaṃ ahampi paṃsukūliko”ti. “My preceptor wears them, and so I do it too.”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ upasenaṃ vaṅgantaputtaṃ etadavoca—“pāsādikā kho tyāyaṃ, upasena, parisā. And the Master said to Upasena, “Upasena, your followers are inspiring. Kathaṃ tvaṃ, upasena, parisaṃ vinesī”ti? How do you train them?”

“Yo maṃ, bhante, upasampadaṃ yācati tamahaṃ evaṃ vadāmi— “When anyone asks me for the full ordination, Venerable Sir, I tell them this: ‘ahaṃ kho, āvuso, āraññiko piṇḍapātiko paṃsukūliko. ‘I stay in the wilderness, I am an alms-collector, and I am a rag-robe wearer. Sace tvampi āraññiko bhavissasi piṇḍapātiko paṃsukūliko, evāhaṃ taṃ upasampādessāmī’ti. If you, too, will stay in the wilderness, collect alms, and wear rag-robes, I will give you the full ordination.’ Sace me paṭissuṇāti upasampādemi, no ce me paṭissuṇāti na upasampādemi. If they agree, I ordain them; otherwise I don’t. Yo maṃ nissayaṃ yācati tamahaṃ evaṃ vadāmi— And I do the same when anyone asks me for support. ‘ahaṃ kho, āvuso, āraññiko piṇḍapātiko paṃsukūliko. Sace tvampi āraññiko bhavissasi piṇḍapātiko paṃsukūliko, evāhaṃ te nissayaṃ dassāmī’ti. Sace me paṭissuṇāti nissayaṃ demi, no ce me paṭissuṇāti na nissayaṃ demi. Evaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhante, parisaṃ vinemī”ti. It’s in this way that I train my followers.”

“Sādhu sādhu, upasena. Sādhu kho tvaṃ, upasena, parisaṃ vinesi. “Good, Upasena, you train your followers well. Jānāsi pana tvaṃ, upasena, sāvatthiyā saṃghassa katikan”ti? But do you know the agreement made by the Order at Sāvatthī?”

“Na kho ahaṃ, bhante, jānāmi sāvatthiyā saṃghassa katikan”ti. “No.”

“Sāvatthiyā kho, upasena, saṃghena katikā katā— “The Order at Sāvatthī has made the following agreement: ‘icchatāvuso, bhagavā temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. ‘The Master wishes to be on solitary retreat for three months. Na bhagavā kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena. No one is to approach him except the one who brings him almsfood. Yo bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati so pācittiyaṃ desāpetabbo’”ti. Anyone who approaches him should be made to confess an offense entailing confession.’”

“Paññāyissati, bhante, sāvatthiyā saṃgho sakāya katikāya, “Venerable Sir, the Order at Sāvatthī will become known for this agreement. na mayaṃ apaññattaṃ paññapessāma paññattaṃ vā na samucchindissāma, We, however, don’t lay down new rules, nor do we abolish the old ones. yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattissāmā”ti. We practice and undertake the training rules as they are.”

“Sādhu sādhu, upasena, “Good, Upasena. apaññattaṃ na paññapetabbaṃ, paññattaṃ vā na samucchinditabbaṃ, One should not lay down new rules, nor should one abolish the old ones. yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattitabbaṃ. One should practice and undertake the training rules as they are.

Anujānāmi, upasena, ye te bhikkhū āraññikā piṇḍapātikā paṃsukūlikā yathāsukhaṃ maṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamantū”ti. And, Upasena, I allow those monks who stay in the wilderness, who collect alms, and who wear rag-robes to come and see me whenever they please.”

Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhitā honti—“mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ upasenaṃ vaṅgantaputtaṃ pācittiyaṃ desāpessāmā”ti. Just then many monks were standing outside the gateway, thinking, “We’ll make Venerable Upasena confess an offense entailing confession.” Atha kho āyasmā upaseno vaṅgantaputto sapariso uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Upasena and his followers rose from their seats, bowed down, circumambulated the Master with their right side toward him, and left. Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upasenaṃ vaṅgantaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ—“jānāsi tvaṃ, āvuso upasena, sāvatthiyā saṃghassa katikan”ti. And those monks said to Upasena, “Upasena, do you know the agreement of the Order at Sāvatthī?”

“Bhagavāpi maṃ, āvuso, evamāha—‘jānāsi pana tvaṃ, upasena, sāvatthiyā saṃghassa katikan’ti? ‘Na kho ahaṃ, bhante, jānāmi sāvatthiyā saṃghassa katikan’ti. “The Master asked me the same question, and I replied that I didn’t. ‘Sāvatthiyā kho, upasena, saṃghena katikā katā— He then told me what it was, and I said, icchatāvuso, bhagavā temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. Na bhagavā kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena. Yo bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati so pācittiyaṃ desāpetabbo’ti. ‘Paññāyissati, bhante, sāvatthiyā saṃgho sakāya katikāya, ‘Venerable Sir, the Order at Sāvatthī will become known for this agreement. na mayaṃ apaññattaṃ paññapessāma paññattaṃ vā na samucchindissāma, We, however, don’t lay down new rules, nor do we abolish the old ones. yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattissāmā’ti. We practice and undertake the training rules as they are.’ Anuññātāvuso, bhagavatā—‘ye te bhikkhū āraññikā piṇḍapātikā paṃsukūlikā yathāsukhaṃ maṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamantū’”ti. And the Master has allowed those monks who stay in the wilderness, who collect alms, and who wear rag-robes to go and see him whenever they please.”

Atha kho te bhikkhū—“saccaṃ kho āyasmā upaseno āha— Those monks thought, “It’s true what Venerable Upasena says.” ‘na apaññattaṃ paññapetabbaṃ, paññattaṃ vā na samucchinditabbaṃ, yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattitabban’”ti.

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū—“anuññātā kira bhagavatā—‘ye te bhikkhū āraññikā piṇḍapātikā paṃsukūlikā yathāsukhaṃ maṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamantū’”ti. The monks heard that the Master had allowed those monks who stay in the wilderness, who collect alms, and who wear rag-robes to go and see him whenever they please. Te bhagavantaṃ dassanaṃ pihentā santhatāni ujjhitvā āraññikaṅgaṃ piṇḍapātikaṅgaṃ paṃsukūlikaṅgaṃ samādiyiṃsu. Longing to see the Master, they discarded their blankets and undertook the practice of staying in the wilderness, of collecting alms, and of wearing rag-robes.

Atha kho bhagavā sambahulehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ senāsanacārikaṃ āhiṇḍanto addasa santhatāni tahaṃ tahaṃ ujjhitāni. Soon afterwards the Master and a number of monks were walking about the dwellings, and he saw discarded blankets here and there. Passitvā bhikkhū āmantesi—“kassimāni, bhikkhave, santhatāni tahaṃ tahaṃ ujjhitānī”ti? He asked the monks, “Whose are these discarded blankets?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. The monks told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards he gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi dasa atthavase paṭicca— “Well then, monks, I will lay down a training rule for the following ten reasons: saṃghasuṭṭhutāya, saṃghaphāsutāya … for the well-being of the Order, for the comfort of the Order, for the restraint of bad people, for the ease of well-behaved monks, for the restraint of corruptions in the present life, for avoiding corruptions in future lives, to give rise to confidence in those without it, to increase the confidence of those who have it, for the continuation of the true Teaching, and for supporting the training. evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Nisīdanasanthataṃ pana bhikkhunā kārayamānena purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthi ādātabbā dubbaṇṇakaraṇāya, ‘If a monk is getting a sitting-blanket made, he must incorporate a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket in order to make it ugly. anādā ce bhikkhu purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthiṃ navaṃ nisīdanasanthataṃ kārāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. If he gets a new sitting-blanket made without incorporating a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Nisīdanaṃ nāma Sitting(-mat):
sadasaṃ vuccati. one with a border is what is meant.
Santhataṃ nāma A blanket:
santharitvā kataṃ hoti avāyimaṃ. it is made by spreading out, not by weaving.
Kārayamānenāti Getting made:
karonto vā kārāpento vā. making or getting made.
Purāṇasanthataṃ nāma An old blanket:
sakiṃ nivatthampi sakiṃ pārutampi. even worn once.
Sāmantā sugatavidatthi ādātabbā dubbaṇṇakaraṇāyāti He must incorporate a 25 centimeter piece from the border […] in order to make it ugly:
thirabhāvāya vaṭṭaṃ vā caturassaṃ vā chinditvā ekadese vā santharitabbaṃ vijaṭetvā vā santharitabbaṃ. to make it strong, he cuts out a circle or a rectangular piece, and he then incorporates it in one place or he spreads it out after pulling it apart.
Anādā ce bhikkhu purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthinti Without using a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket:
anādiyitvā purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthiṃ navaṃ nisīdanasanthataṃ karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ, If he makes a new sitting-blanket, or has one made, without incorporating a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When he gets the sitting-blanket, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The sitting-blanket should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, nisīdanasanthataṃ anādiyitvā purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthiṃ kārāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. ‘Venerables, this sitting-blanket which I got made without incorporating a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket is to be relinquished. Imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this sitting-blanket back to you.’”

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he makes one, or gets one made, for the sake of another, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: purāṇasanthatassa sāmantā sugatavidatthiṃ ādiyitvā karoti, if he makes one incorporating a 25 centimeter piece from the border of an old blanket; alabhanto thokataraṃ ādiyitvā karoti, if he is not able to obtain such a piece and he makes one incorporating a smaller piece; alabhanto anādiyitvā karoti, if he is not able to obtain such a smaller piece and he makes one without; aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, if he obtains what was made by another and then uses it; vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, if he makes a canopy, a floor cover, a screen-wall, a mattress, or a pillow; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Nisīdanasanthatasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ. The fifth rule, the training rule on sitting-blankets, is finished.
