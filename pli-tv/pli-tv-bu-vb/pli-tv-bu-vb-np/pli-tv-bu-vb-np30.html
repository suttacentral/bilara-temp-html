Nissaggiyakaṇḍa The chapter on relinquishment

Pattavagga The sub-chapter on bowls

30. Pariṇatasikkhāpada 30. The training rule on what is intended

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññatarassa pūgassa saṃghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ hoti— At that time an association had prepared a meal together with robe-cloth for the Order, “bhojetvā cīvarena acchādessāmā”ti. intending to offer the robe-cloth after giving the meal.

Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena so pūgo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; But the monks from the group of six went to that association upasaṅkamitvā taṃ pūgaṃ etadavocuṃ— and said, “dethāvuso, amhākaṃ imāni cīvarānī”ti. “Sirs, give this robe-cloth to us.”

“Na mayaṃ, bhante, dassāma. “Venerables, we can’t do that. Amhākaṃ saṃghassa anuvassaṃ sacīvarabhikkhā paññattā”ti. We’ve prepared our annual alms-offering together with robe-cloth for the Order.”

“Bahū, āvuso, saṃghassa dāyakā, bahū saṃghassa bhattā. “The Order has many donors and supporters. Mayaṃ tumhe nissāya tumhe sampassantā idha viharāma. But since we’re staying here, we depend on you and look to you for support. Tumhe ce amhākaṃ na dassatha, atha ko carahi amhākaṃ dassati? If you don’t give to us, then who will? Dethāvuso, amhākaṃ imāni cīvarānī”ti. So give us this robe-cloth.” Atha kho so pūgo chabbaggiyehi bhikkhūhi nippīḷiyamāno yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ datvā saṃghaṃ bhattena parivisi. Being pressured by the monks from the group of six, that association gave the prepared robe-cloth to them and served the food to the Order.

Ye te bhikkhū jānanti saṃghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ, na ca jānanti chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ dinnanti, te evamāhaṃsu— The monks who knew that a meal together with robe-cloth had been prepared for the Order, but who did not know that the robe-cloth had been given to those monks from the group six, said, “oṇojethāvuso, saṃghassa cīvaran”ti. “You may offer the robe-cloth.”

“Natthi, bhante. “Venerables, there isn’t any. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ ayyā chabbaggiyā attano pariṇāmesun”ti. The robe-cloth we had prepared were diverted by the monks from the group of six to themselves.”

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmessantī”ti. “How can the monks from the group of six divert to themselves things they know were intended for the Order?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmessatha. Foolish men, how can you divert to yourselves things that you know were intended for the Order? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk diverts to himself something that he knows was intended for the Order, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Jānāti nāma He knows:
sāmaṃ vā jānāti aññe vā tassa ārocenti so vā āroceti. he knows by himself or others have told him or the donor has told him.
Saṃghikaṃ nāma For the Order:
saṃghassa dinnaṃ hoti pariccattaṃ. given to the Order, given up to the Order.
Lābho nāma Something:
cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā. Antamaso cuṇṇapiṇḍopi, dantakaṭṭhampi, dasikasuttampi. robe-cloth, almsfood, a dwelling, and medicines; even bath powder, a toothbrush, or a piece of string.
Pariṇataṃ nāma Intended:
dassāma karissāmāti vācā bhinnā hoti. they have said, “We will give/we will prepare.” Attano pariṇāmeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If he diverts it to himself, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When he gets it, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. It should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, jānaṃ saṃghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. ‘Venerables, this thing which I diverted to myself knowing that it was intended for the Order is to be relinquished. Imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this back to you.’”

Permutations

Pariṇate pariṇatasaññī attano pariṇāmeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is intended for the Order and he perceives it as such, and he diverts it to himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Pariṇate vematiko attano pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for the Order, but he is unsure if it is, and he diverts it to himself, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Pariṇate apariṇatasaññī attano pariṇāmeti, anāpatti. If it is intended for the Order, but he does not perceives it as such, and he diverts it to himself, there is no offense.

Saṃghassa pariṇataṃ aññasaṃghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for one Order and he diverts it to another Order or to a shrine, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Cetiyassa pariṇataṃ aññacetiyassa vā saṃghassa vā puggalassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for one shrine and he diverts it to another shrine or to an Order or to an individual, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Puggalassa pariṇataṃ aññapuggalassa vā saṃghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is intended for an individual and he diverts it to another individual or to an Order or to a shrine, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Apariṇate pariṇatasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not intended for the Order, but he perceives it as intended for the Order, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apariṇate vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not intended for the Order, but he is unsure of it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apariṇate apariṇatasaññī, anāpatti. If it is not intended for the Order and he does not perceive it as such, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: kattha demāti pucchiyamāno yattha tumhākaṃ deyyadhammo paribhogaṃ vā labheyya paṭisaṅkhāraṃ vā labheyya ciraṭṭhitiko vā assa yattha vā pana tumhākaṃ cittaṃ pasīdati tattha dethāti bhaṇati, if being asked, “Where may we give?” he says, “Give where your gift goes toward equipment/where it goes toward repairs/where it will last for a long time/where you feel inspired;” ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Pariṇatasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ. The tenth rule, the training rule on what is intended, is finished.

Pattavaggo tatiyo. The third chapter on bowls is finished.

Tassuddānaṃ This is the summary:

Dve ca pattāni bhesajjaṃ, Two on bowls, and tonics,
vassikā dānapañcamaṃ; Rainy season, the fifth on a gift;
Sāmaṃ vāyāpanacceko, Oneself, getting woven, haste,
sāsaṅkaṃ saṃghikena cāti. Risky, and with the Order.

“Uddiṭṭhā kho, āyasmanto, tiṃsa nissaggiyā pācittiyā dhammā. “Venerables, the thirty rules entailing relinquishment and confession have been recited. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi— In regard to this I ask you, ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Dutiyampi pucchāmi— A second time I ask, ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Tatiyampi pucchāmi— A third time I ask, ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Parisuddhetthāyasmanto, tasmā tuṇhī, You are pure in this and therefore silent. evametaṃ dhārayāmī”ti. I will remember it thus.”

Nissaggiyakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The chapter on offenses entailing relinquishment is finished.

Pārājikapāḷi niṭṭhitā. The Canonical text on offenses entailing expulsion is finished.
