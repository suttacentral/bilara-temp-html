Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Nissaggiyakaṇḍa The chapter on relinquishment

Kosiyavagga The sub-chapter on silk

14. Chabbassasikkhāpada 14. The training rule on six years

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpenti. At that time monks were getting blankets made every year. Te yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharanti— They kept on begging and asking, “eḷakalomāni detha. “Give wool; Eḷakalomehi attho”ti. we need wool.” Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People grumbled and complained, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessanti, yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharissanti— “How can the Sakyan ascetics get blankets made every year, begging and asking, ‘eḷakalomāni detha, eḷakalomehi attho’ti. ‘Give wool; we need wool’? Amhākaṃ pana sakiṃ katāni santhatāni pañcapi chapi vassāni honti, yesaṃ no dārakā uhadantipi ummihantipi undūrehipi khajjanti. We only make blankets for ourselves every five or six years, even though our children defecate and urinate on them and they are eaten by rats.” Ime pana samaṇā sakyaputtiyā anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpenti, yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharanti— ‘eḷakalomāni detha, eḷakalomehi attho’”ti.

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the complaints of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhū anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessanti, yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharissanti— “How can those monks act like this?” ‘eḷakalomāni detha, eḷakalomehi attho’”ti.

Atha kho te bhikkhū te anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpenti, yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharanti— “Is it true, monks, that some monks are acting like this?” ‘eḷakalomāni detha, eḷakalomehi attho’”ti?

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā anuvassaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessanti, yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharissanti— How can those foolish men get blankets made every year, begging and asking, ‘eḷakalomāni detha, eḷakalomehi attho’ti. ‘Give wool; we need wool’? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

“Navaṃ pana bhikkhunā santhataṃ kārāpetvā chabbassāni dhāretabbaṃ. ‘If a monk gets a new blanket made, he should keep it for six years. Orena ce channaṃ vassānaṃ taṃ santhataṃ vissajjetvā vā avissajjetvā vā aññaṃ navaṃ santhataṃ kārāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. Whether that blanket has been given away or not, if he gets another new blanket made in less than six years, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosambiyaṃ gilāno hoti. At one time a certain monk at Kosambī was sick. Ñātakā tassa bhikkhuno santike dūtaṃ pāhesuṃ— His relatives sent him a message: “āgacchatu bhadanto mayaṃ upaṭṭhahissāmā”ti. “Come, Venerable, we’ll attend on you.” Bhikkhūpi evamāhaṃsu— Other monks “gacchāvuso, ñātakā taṃ upaṭṭhahissantī”ti. urged him to go, So evamāha— but he said, “bhagavatā, āvuso, sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ— “The Master has laid down a training rule ‘navaṃ pana bhikkhunā santhataṃ kārāpetvā chabbassāni dhāretabban’ti. that a monk who gets a new blanket made must keep it for six years. Ahañcamhi gilāno, na sakkomi santhataṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. Because I’m sick, I’m not able to take my blanket when I travel. Mayhañca vinā santhatā na phāsu hoti. And because I’m not comfortable without it, Nāhaṃ gamissāmī”ti. I cannot go.”

Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master.

Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards he gave a teaching and addressed the monks,

“anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānassa bhikkhuno santhatasammutiṃ dātuṃ. “Monks, I allow you to give a blanket-permission to a sick monk.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, dātabbā. And it should be given in this way. Tena gilānena bhikkhunā saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— After approaching the Order, the sick monk should put his upper robe over one shoulder and pay respect at the feet of the senior monks. He should then squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say, ‘ahaṃ, bhante, gilāno ‘Venerables, I’m sick. na sakkomi santhataṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. I’m not able to take my blanket when I travel. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṃghaṃ santhatasammutiṃ yācāmī’ti. I ask the Order for a blanket-permission.’ Dutiyampi yācitabbā. And he should ask a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should then inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno na sakkoti santhataṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. This monk so-and-so is sick. He is not able to take his blanket when he travels. So saṃghaṃ santhatasammutiṃ yācati. He is asking the Order for a blanket-permission. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno santhatasammutiṃ dadeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should give a blanket-permission to monk so-and-so. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno na sakkoti santhataṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. This monk so-and-so is sick. He is not able to take his blanket when he travels. So saṃghaṃ santhatasammutiṃ yācati. He is asking the Order for a blanket-permission. Saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno santhatasammutiṃ deti. The Order gives a blanket-permission to monk so-and-so. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno santhatasammutiyā dānaṃ, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of giving a blanket-permission to monk so-and-so should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Dinnā saṃghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno santhatasammuti. The Order has given a blanket-permission to monk so-and-so. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, The Order approves and is therefore silent. Kevametaṃ dhārayāmī’ti. I will remember it thus.’

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Navaṃ pana bhikkhunā santhataṃ kārāpetvā chabbassāni dhāretabbaṃ. ‘If a monk gets a new blanket made, he should keep it for six years. Orena ce channaṃ vassānaṃ taṃ santhataṃ vissajjetvā vā avissajjetvā vā aññaṃ navaṃ santhataṃ kārāpeyya, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. Whether that blanket has been given away or not, if he gets another new blanket made in less than six years, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Navaṃ nāma New:
karaṇaṃ upādāya vuccati. newly made is what is meant.
Santhataṃ nāma A blanket:
santharitvā kataṃ hoti avāyimaṃ. it is made by spreading out, not by weaving.
Kārāpetvāti Gets made:
karitvā vā kārāpetvā vā. makes or gets made.
Chabbassāni dhāretabbanti He should keep it for six years:
chabbassaparamatā dhāretabbaṃ. he should keep it for a minimum of six years.
Orena ce channaṃ vassānanti In less than six years:
ūnakachabbassāni. fewer than six years.
Taṃ santhataṃ vissajjetvāti That blanket has been given away:
aññesaṃ datvā. it has been given to others.
Avissajjetvāti Not:
na kassaci datvā. it has not been given to anyone.
Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyāti except if the monks have agreed:
ṭhapetvā bhikkhusammutiṃ aññaṃ navaṃ santhataṃ karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If he makes another new blanket, or gets one made, unless he has the permission of the monks, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When he gets the blanket, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The blanket should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, santhataṃ ūnakachabbassāni kārāpitaṃ, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ. ‘Venerables, this blanket which I got made after less than six years without the permission of the monks is to be relinquished. Imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this blanket back to you.’”

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he gets others to finish what he began himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he gets others to finish what was begun by others, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: chabbassāni karoti, if he makes one after six years; atirekachabbassāni karoti, if he makes one after more than six years; aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, if he makes one, or gets one made, for the sake of another; aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, if he obtains what was made by another and then uses it; vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, if he makes a canopy, a floor cover, a screen-wall, a mattress, or a pillow; bhikkhusammutiyā, if he has the permission of the monks; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Chabbassasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ. The fourth rule, the training rule on six years, is finished.
