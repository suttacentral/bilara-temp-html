26. Suttaviññattisikkhāpada 26. The training rule on asking for thread

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. At one time the Buddha was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrel sanctuary. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū cīvarakārasamaye bahuṃ suttaṃ viññāpesuṃ. At that time the monks from the group of six were making robes, and they asked for a large amount of thread. Katepi cīvare bahuṃ suttaṃ avasiṭṭhaṃ hoti. Yet even when their robes had been sewn, there was much thread left over. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi— They said, “handa mayaṃ, āvuso, aññampi suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpemā”ti. “Well, let’s ask for more thread and get weavers to weave us robe-cloth.” Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpesuṃ. Vītepi cīvare bahuṃ suttaṃ avasiṭṭhaṃ hoti. Yet even when the robe-cloth had been woven, there was much thread left over. Dutiyampi kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpesuṃ. A second time they asked for more thread and got weavers to weave them robe-cloth. Vītepi cīvare bahuṃ suttaṃ avasiṭṭhaṃ hoti. Once again there was much thread left over. Tatiyampi kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpesuṃ. A third time they asked for more thread and got weavers to weave them robe-cloth. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People grumbled and complained, “kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpessantī”ti. “How can the Sakyan ascetics ask for thread and then get weavers to weave them robe-cloth?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The monks heard the complaints of those people, Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the monks of few desires complained and criticized those monks, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpessantī”ti. “How can the monks from the group of six ask for thread and then get weavers to weave them robe-cloth?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpethā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that you did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked them, “… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpessatha. Foolish men, how can you ask for thread and then get weavers to weave you robe-cloth? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk himself asks for thread, and then gets weavers to weave him robe-cloth, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Sāmanti Himself:
sayaṃ viññāpetvā. he himself has asked.
Suttaṃ nāma Thread:
cha suttāni— there are six kinds of thread: khomaṃ kappāsikaṃ koseyyaṃ kambalaṃ sāṇaṃ bhaṅgaṃ. linen, cotton, silk, wool, sunn hemp, and hemp.
Tantavāyehīti Weavers:
pesakārehi vāyāpeti, payoge payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If he gets it woven by weavers, then every effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When he gets the robe-cloth, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The robe-cloth should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ sāmaṃ suttaṃ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi vāyāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. ‘Venerables, this robe-cloth which I got woven by weavers after asking for the thread myself is to be relinquished. Imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this robe-cloth back to you.’”

Permutations

Vāyāpite vāyāpitasaññī, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he got it woven, and he perceives that he did, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Vāyāpite vematiko, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he got it woven, but he is unsure if he did, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Vāyāpite avāyāpitasaññī, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he got it woven, but he perceives that he did not, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Avāyāpite vāyāpitasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he did not get it woven, but he perceives that he did, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Avāyāpite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he did not get it woven, but he is unsure if he did, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Avāyāpite avāyāpitasaññī, anāpatti. If he did not get it woven, and he perceives that he did not, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: cīvaraṃ sibbetuṃ, if it is to sew a robe; āyoge, if it is for a back-and-knee strap; kāyabandhane, if it is for a belt; aṃsabaddhake, if it is for a shoulder strap; pattatthavikāya, if it is for a bowl bag; parissāvane, if it is for a water filter; ñātakānaṃ, if it is from relatives; pavāritānaṃ, if it is from those who have given an invitation; aññassatthāya, if it for the benefit of someone else; attano dhanena, if it is by means of one’s own property; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Suttaviññattisikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ. The sixth rule, the training rule on asking for thread, is finished.
