Mahāvibhaṅga The monks’ rules and their analysis

Nissaggiyakaṇḍa The chapter on relinquishment

Cīvaravagga The sub-chapter on robes

2. Udosita(dutiyakathina)sikkhāpada 2. The training rule on outbuildings

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamanti. At that time the monks stored one of their robes with other monks and then left to travel the countryside with a lower and an upper robe. Tāni cīvarāni ciraṃ nikkhittāni kaṇṇakitāni honti. Because they were stored for a long time, the robes became moldy. Tāni bhikkhū otāpenti. The monks put them out in the sun.

Addasā kho āyasmā ānando senāsanacārikaṃ āhiṇḍanto te bhikkhū tāni cīvarāni otāpente. While walking about the dwellings, Venerable Ānanda saw the monks sunning those robes. Disvāna yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca— He asked them, “kassimāni, āvuso, cīvarāni kaṇṇakitānī”ti? “Whose moldy robes are these?” Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. And they told him what had happened. Āyasmā ānando ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— Venerable Ānanda complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissantī”ti. “How can those monks store one of their robes with other monks and then leave to travel the countryside with a lower and an upper robe?”

Atha kho āyasmā ānando te bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, Venerable Ānanda told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that some monks are acting like this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissanti. How can these foolish men store one of their robes with other monks and then leave to travel the countryside with a lower and an upper robe? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kathine ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘When his robe is finished and the robe-making season has ended, if a monk stays apart from his three robes even for a single day, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosambiyaṃ gilāno hoti. At one time a certain monk at Kosambī was sick. Ñātakā tassa bhikkhuno santike dūtaṃ pāhesuṃ— The relatives of that monk sent him a message: “āgacchatu bhadanto, mayaṃ, upaṭṭhahissāmā”ti. “Come, Venerable, we will attend on you.” Bhikkhūpi evamāhaṃsu—“gacchāvuso, ñātakā taṃ upaṭṭhahissantī”ti. Other monks urged him to go, So evamāha— but he said, “bhagavatāvuso, sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ— “The Master has laid down a training rule ‘na ticīvarena vippavasitabban’ti. that you cannot be apart from your three robes. Ahañcamhi gilāno. Because I am sick, Na sakkomi ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. I’m not able to travel with all three robes, Nāhaṃ gamissāmī”ti. and therefore I cannot go.”

Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards he gave a teaching and addressed the monks:

“anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ dātuṃ. “Monks, I allow you to give permission to a sick monk to stay apart from his three robes.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, dātabbā. And it should be given in this way. Tena gilānena bhikkhunā saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— After approaching the Order, the sick monk should put his upper robe over one shoulder and pay respect at the feet of the senior monks. He should then squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say, ‘ahaṃ, bhante, gilāno. ‘Venerables, I’m sick. Na sakkomi ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. I’m not able to travel with my three robes. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṃghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācāmī’ti. I ask the Order for permission to stay apart from my three robes.’ Dutiyampi yācitabbā. And he should ask a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should then inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno. This monk so-and-so is sick. Na sakkoti ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. He is not able to travel with his three robes. So saṃghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācati. He is asking the Order for permission to stay apart from his three robes. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ dadeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should give permission to monk so-and-so to stay apart from his three robes. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno. This monk so-and-so is sick. Na sakkoti ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. He is not able to travel with his three robes. So saṃghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācati. He is asking the Order for permission to stay apart from his three robes. Saṃgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ deti. The Order gives permission to monk so-and-so to stay apart from his three robes. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiyā dānaṃ, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of giving permission to monk so-and-so to stay apart from his three robes should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Dinnā saṃghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammuti. The Order has given permission to monk so-and-so to stay apart from his three robes. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, The Order approves and is therefore silent. evametaṃ dhārayāmī’ti. I will remember it thus.’

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kathine ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘When his robe is finished and the robe-making season has ended, if a monk stays apart from his three robes even for a single day, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Niṭṭhitacīvarasminti When his robe is finished:
bhikkhuno cīvaraṃ kataṃ vā hoti naṭṭhaṃ vā vinaṭṭhaṃ vā daḍḍhaṃ vā cīvarāsā vā upacchinnā. the monk has made a robe, or the robe-cloth has been lost, destroyed, or burnt, or he has abandoned his expectation of receiving further robe-cloth.
Ubbhatasmiṃ kathineti The robe-making season has ended:
aṭṭhannaṃ mātikānaṃ aññatarāya mātikāya ubbhataṃ hoti, saṃghena vā antarā ubbhataṃ hoti. it has ended according to one of the eight summaries or the Order has ended it, whichever comes first.
Ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyyāti If a monk stays apart from his three robes even for a single day:
saṅghāṭiyā vā uttarāsaṅgena vā antaravāsakena vā. from the outer robe, from the upper robe, or from the sarong .
Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyāti Except if the monks have agreed:
ṭhapetvā bhikkhusammutiṃ. unless he has the permission of the monks.
Nissaggiyaṃ hotīti Entailing relinquishment:
saha aruṇuggamanā nissaggiyaṃ hoti. it becomes subject to relinquishment at dawn.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The robe should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1/23-29), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ rattivippavutthaṃ aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā nissaggiyaṃ, ‘Venerables, this robe which I have stayed apart from for one day without the permission of the monks is to be relinquished. imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this robe back to you.’”

Exposition

A village

Gāmo ekūpacāro nāma “A village with a single entrance” refers to the following.

ekakulassa gāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Antogāme cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antogāme vatthabbaṃ. In an enclosed village belonging to one clan, if the robe is kept within the village, one must stay within the village. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. If the village is unenclosed, one must stay in the house where the robe is kept, or not go beyond arm’s reach of the house.

Nānākulassa gāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ sabhāye vā dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. In an enclosed village belonging to many clans, if the robe is kept in a house, one must stay in that house, in the public meeting hall, or at the gateway to the village, or not go beyond arm’s reach of the public meeting hall or the gateway. Sabhāyaṃ gacchantena hatthapāse cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā sabhāye vā vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. If one puts aside the robe within arm’s reach while going to the public meeting hall, one must stay in the public meeting hall, or at the gateway to the village, or not go beyond arm’s reach of either. Sabhāye cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā sabhāye vā vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. If the robe is kept in the public meeting hall, one must stay in the public meeting hall, or at the gateway to the village, or not go beyond arm’s reach of either. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. If the village is unenclosed, one must stay in the house where the robe is kept, or not go beyond arm’s reach of the house.

Nānākulassa rukkhamūlaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ. At the foot of a tree belonging to many clans, one must not go beyond arm’s reach of the robe.

In the open

Ajjhokāso ekūpacāro nāma agāmake araññe samantā sattabbhantarā ekūpacāro, Out in the open with one neighborhood, in an uninhabited area, in the wilderness, one neighborhood is the area covered by a circle with radius 100 meters. tato paraṃ nānūpacāro. Whatever lies beyond that is many neighbourhoods.

Vippavutthe vippavutthasaññī aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he has stayed apart and he perceives that he has stayed apart, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Vippavutthe vematiko, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he has stayed apart, but he is unsure if he has, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Vippavutthe avippavutthasaññī, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If he has stayed apart, but he perceives that he has not, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Appaccuddhaṭe paccuddhaṭasaññī … pe … If he has not relinquished the robe, but he perceives that he has, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. avissajjite vissajjitasaññī … If he has not given away the robe, but he perceives that he has, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. anaṭṭhe naṭṭhasaññī … If he has not lost the robe, but he perceives that he has, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. avinaṭṭhe vinaṭṭhasaññī … If the robe is not destroyed, but he perceives that it is, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. adaḍḍhe daḍḍhasaññī If the robe is not burnt, but he perceives that it is, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. avilutte viluttasaññī, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If the robe is not stolen, but he perceives that it is, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Nissaggiyaṃ cīvaraṃ anissajjitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he uses a robe which should be relinquished without first relinquishing it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Avippavutthe vippavutthasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he has not stayed apart, but he perceives that he has, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Avippavutthe vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he has not stayed apart, but he is unsure if he has, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Avippavutthe avippavutthasaññī, anāpatti. If he has not stayed apart and he perceives that he has not stayed apart, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: antoaruṇe paccuddharati, vissajjeti, nassati, vinassati, ḍayhati, acchinditvā gaṇhanti, vissāsaṃ gaṇhanti, if before dawn it has been relinquished, given away, lost, destroyed, burnt, stolen, or taken on trust; bhikkhusammutiyā, if he has the permission of the monks; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Udosita(dutiyakathina)sikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ. The second rule, the training rule on outbuildings, is finished.
