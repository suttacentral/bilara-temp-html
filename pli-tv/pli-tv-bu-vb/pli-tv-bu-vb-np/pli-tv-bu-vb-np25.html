25. Cīvaraacchindanasikkhāpada 25. The training rule on taking robe-cloth back

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto bhātuno saddhivihārikaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca— At that time Venerable Upananda the Sakyan said to his brother’s student, “ehāvuso, janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmā”ti. “Come, let’s go wandering in the country.”

“Nāhaṃ, bhante, gamissāmi; “I can’t go, Venerable, dubbalacīvaromhī”ti. my robes are worn.”

“Ehāvuso, ahaṃ te cīvaraṃ dassāmī”ti “I’ll give you a robe.” tassa cīvaraṃ adāsi. And he gave him a robe.

Assosi kho so bhikkhu— Soon afterwards that monk heard “bhagavā kira janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissatī”ti. that the Master was about to go wandering in the country. Atha kho tassa bhikkhuno etadahosi— He thought, “na dānāhaṃ āyasmatā upanandena sakyaputtena saddhiṃ janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmi, bhagavatā saddhiṃ janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmī”ti. “Now I won’t go wandering with Venerable Upananda, but with the Master.” Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca— Then, when Upananda said, “ehi dāni, āvuso, janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmā”ti. “Let’s go,” “Nāhaṃ, bhante, tayā saddhiṃ janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmi, bhagavatā saddhiṃ janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissāmī”ti. he replied, “I’m not going with you, but with the Master.”

“Yampi tyāhaṃ, āvuso, cīvaraṃ adāsiṃ, mayā saddhiṃ janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissatī”ti, “Well, the robe I gave you is going with me,” kupito anattamano acchindi. and he just took it back in anger.

Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. That monk told other monks what had happened. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— And the monks of few desires complained and criticized Upananda, “kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindissatī”ti. “How can Venerable Upananda give away a robe and then take it back in anger?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing him in many ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindī”ti? “Is it true, Upananda, that you acted like this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha rebuked him, “… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindissasi. Foolish man, how can you give a robe to a monk and then take it back in anger? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindeyya vā acchindāpeyya vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a monk himself gives robe-cloth to a monk, but then, in anger, takes it back or gets it taken back, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Yo panāti A:
yo yādiso … pe … whoever …
bhikkhūti Monk:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti. … The monk who has been given the full ordination by a complete Order through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.
Bhikkhussāti To a monk:
aññassa bhikkhussa. to another monk.
Sāmanti Himself:
sayaṃ datvā. he himself has given it.
Cīvaraṃ nāma Robe-cloth:
channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ, vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ. one of the six kinds of robe-cloth, but not smaller than what can be transferred.
Kupito anattamanoti In anger:
anabhiraddho āhatacitto khilajāto. discontent, having hatred, hostile.
Acchindeyyāti Takes […] back:
sayaṃ acchindati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. if he takes it back himself, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.
Acchindāpeyyāti Gets […] taken back:
aññaṃ āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. if he asks another, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Sakiṃ āṇatto bahukampi acchindati, nissaggiyaṃ hoti. If he only asks once, then even if the other takes back many times, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The robe-cloth should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ bhikkhussa sāmaṃ datvā acchinnaṃ nissaggiyaṃ imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … ‘Venerables, this robe-cloth which I took back after giving it to a monk is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this robe-cloth back to you.’”

Permutations

Upasampanne upasampannasaññī cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindati vā acchindāpeti vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If the other monk is fully ordained and he perceives him as fully ordained, and after giving him robe-cloth, he takes it back in anger or gets it taken back, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Upasampanne vematiko cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindati vā acchindāpeti vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If the other monk is fully ordained, but he is unsure if he is, and after giving him robe-cloth, he takes it back in anger or gets it taken back, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī cīvaraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindati vā acchindāpeti vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If the other monk is fully ordained, but he perceives him as not fully ordained, and after giving him robe-cloth, he takes it back in anger or gets it taken back, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindati vā acchindāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, after giving him another requisite, he takes it back in anger or gets it taken back, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannassa cīvaraṃ vā aññaṃ vā parikkhāraṃ datvā kupito anattamano acchindati vā acchindāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, after giving robe-cloth or another requisite to a person who is not fully ordained, he takes it back in anger or gets it taken back, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other monk is not fully ordained, but he perceives him as fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other monk is not fully ordained, but he is unsure if he is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other monk is not fully ordained, and he perceives him as not fully ordained, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: so vā deti, if the other monk gives it back; tassa vā vissasanto gaṇhāti, if he takes it on trust from him; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Cīvaraacchindanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ. The fifth rule, the training rule on taking robe-cloth back, is finished.
