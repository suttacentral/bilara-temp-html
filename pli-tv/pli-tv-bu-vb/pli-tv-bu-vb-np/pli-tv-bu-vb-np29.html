29. Sāsaṅkasikkhāpada 29. The training rule on what is risky

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū vutthavassā āraññakesu senāsanesu viharanti. At that time monks who had completed the rains residence were staying in wilderness dwellings. Kattikacorakā bhikkhū— Thieves who were active during the month of Kattika attacked the monks, thinking, “laddhalābhā”ti paripātenti. “They have obtained things.”

Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. The monks told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards the Master gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “anujānāmi, bhikkhave, āraññakesu senāsanesu viharantena tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitun”ti. “Monks, I allow monks who are staying in wilderness dwellings to store one of their three robes in an inhabited area.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū—“bhagavatā anuññātaṃ āraññakesu senāsanesu viharantena tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitun”ti tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasanti. Knowing that the Master had made this allowance, monks stored one of their three robes in inhabited areas, and they stayed apart from them for more than six days. Tāni cīvarāni nassantipi vinassantipi ḍayhantipi undūrehipi khajjanti. The robes were lost, destroyed, burnt, and eaten by rats. Bhikkhū duccoḷā honti lūkhacīvarā. As a consequence those monks became poorly dressed. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu— Other monks asked them why, “kissa tumhe, āvuso, duccoḷā lūkhacīvarā”ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. and they told them what had happened. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasissantī”ti. “How can those monks store one of their three robes in an inhabited area and then stay apart from it for more than six days?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū te anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … After criticizing those monks in many ways, they told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that some monks do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasissanti. Monks, how can those foolish men store one of their three robes in an inhabited area and then stay apart from it for more than six days? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha— And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

“Upavassaṃ kho pana kattikapuṇṇamaṃ yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanāni sāsaṅkasammatāni sappaṭibhayāni tathārūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharanto ākaṅkhamāno tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipeyya, siyā ca tassa bhikkhuno kocideva paccayo tena cīvarena vippavāsāya. ‘There are wilderness dwellings that are considered risky and dangerous. After observing the Kattika full moon that ends the rainy season, a monk who is staying in such a dwelling may store one of his three robes in an inhabited area, if he so desires, so long as he has a reason for staying apart from that robe. Chārattaparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tena cīvarena vippavasitabbaṃ. Tato ce uttari vippavaseyya, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. He should stay apart from that robe for six days at the most. If he stays apart from it beyond that, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Upavassaṃ kho panāti After observing:
vuṭṭhavassānaṃ. after completing the rainy season.
Kattikapuṇṇamanti The Kattika full moon that ends the rainy season:
kattikacātumāsinī vuccati. the fourth full moon of the rainy season in the month of Kattika is what is meant.
Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanānīti There are wilderness dwellings:
āraññakaṃ nāma it is called a wilderness dwelling senāsanaṃ pañcadhanusatikaṃ pacchimaṃ. if it is at least one kilometer away from any inhabited area.
Sāsaṅkaṃ nāma Risky:
ārāme ārāmūpacāre corānaṃ niviṭṭhokāso dissati, bhuttokāso dissati, ṭhitokāso dissati, nisinnokāso dissati, nipannokāso dissati. in the monastery, or in the vicinity of the monastery, thieves have been seen camping, eating, standing, seated, or lying down.
Sappaṭibhayaṃ nāma Dangerous:
ārāme ārāmūpacāre corehi manussā hatā dissanti, viluttā dissanti, ākoṭitā dissanti. in the monastery, or in the vicinity of the monastery, thieves have been seen injuring, robbing, or beating people.
Tathārūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharantoti A monk who is staying in such a dwelling:
evarūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharanto. a monk who is staying in that kind of dwelling.
Ākaṅkhamānoti If he so desires:
icchamāno. if he so wishes.
Tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaranti One of his three robes:
saṅghāṭiṃ vā uttarāsaṅgaṃ vā antaravāsakaṃ vā. the outer robe, the upper robe, or the sarong.
Antaraghare nikkhipeyyāti May store […] in an inhabited area:
samantā gocaragāme nikkhipeyya. may store it anywhere in his alms village.
Siyā ca tassa bhikkhuno kocideva paccayo tena cīvarena vippavāsāyāti So long as he has a reason for staying apart from that robe:
siyā paccayo siyā karaṇīyaṃ. if there is a reason, if there is something to be done.
Chārattaparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tena cīvarena vippavasitabbanti He should stay apart from that robe for six days at the most:
chārattaparamatā vippavasitabbaṃ. he should stay apart from it for six days at the maximum.
Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyāti Except if the monks have agreed:
ṭhapetvā bhikkhusammutiṃ. unless he has the permission of the monks.
Tato ce uttari vippavaseyyāti If he stays apart from it beyond that:
sattame aruṇuggamane nissaggiyaṃ hoti. the robe becomes subject to relinquishment at dawn on the seventh day.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. The robe should be relinquished to an Order, a group, or an individual. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it should be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in [Relinquishment 1, paragraphs 13–17](pli-tv-bu-vb-np1#13), with appropriate substitutions.

idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ atirekachārattaṃ vippavutthaṃ, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ. ‘Venerables, this robe which I have stayed apart from for more than six days without the permission of the monks is to be relinquished. Imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmīti … pe … I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyāti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyunti … pe … you should give … āyasmato dammīti. ‘I give this robe back to you.’”

Permutations

Atirekachāratte atirekasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is more than six days and he perceives it as more and he is staying apart from it, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Atirekachāratte vematiko vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is more than six days, but he is unsure if it is, and he is staying apart from it, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Atirekachāratte ūnakasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is more than six days, but he does not perceives it as more, and he is staying apart from it, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Appaccuddhaṭe paccuddhaṭasaññī … pe … If the determination has not been given up, but he perceives it as given up … avissajjite vissajjitasaññī … If the robe has not been given away, but he perceives it as given away … anaṭṭhe naṭṭhasaññī … If the robe has not been lost, but he perceives it as lost … avinaṭṭhe vinaṭṭhasaññī … If the robe has not been destroyed, but he perceives it as destroyed … adaḍḍhe daḍḍhasaññī … If the robe has not been burnt, but he perceives it as burnt … avilutte viluttasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If the robe has not been stolen, but he perceives it as stolen, and he is staying apart from it, then, except if the monks have agreed, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.

Nissaggiyaṃ cīvaraṃ anissajjitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he uses a robe that should be relinquished without first relinquishing it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ūnakachāratte atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is less than six days, but he perceives it as more, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ūnakachāratte vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is less than six days, but he is unsure if it is, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ūnakachāratte ūnakasaññī, anāpatti. If it is less than six days and he perceives it as less, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: chārattaṃ vippavasati, if he stays apart from the robe for six days; ūnakachārattaṃ vippavasati, if he stays apart from the robe for less than six days; chārattaṃ vippavasitvā puna gāmasīmaṃ okkamitvā vasitvā pakkamati, if, after staying apart from it for six days, he stays overnight within the village boundary and then departs; antochārattaṃ paccuddharati, vissajjeti, nassati, vinassati, ḍayhati, acchinditvā gaṇhanti, vissāsaṃ gaṇhanti, if within the six days he gives up the determination, or the robe is given away, lost, destroyed, burnt, stolen, or taken on trust; bhikkhusammutiyā, if he has the permission of the monks; ummattakassa, if he is insane; ādikammikassāti. if he is the first offender.

Sāsaṅkasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ. The ninth rule, the training rule on what is risky, is finished.
