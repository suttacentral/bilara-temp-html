Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

2. Corīvuṭṭhāpikāsikkhāpada 2. The training rule on giving the full ordination to criminals

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyaṃ aññatarassa licchavissa pajāpati aticārinī hoti. At that time in Vesālī the wife of a certain Licchavī man was unfaithful. Atha kho so licchavi taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca— He said to her, “sādhu viramāhi, anatthaṃ kho te karissāmī”ti. “If you don’t stop I will punish you.” Evampi vuccamānā nādiyi. But she did not heed his warning.

Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyaṃ licchavigaṇo sannipatito hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Just then in Vesālī the Licchavī clan had assembled on some business. Atha kho so licchavi te licchavayo etadavoca— And that Licchavī man said to them, “ekaṃ me, ayyo, itthiṃ anujānāthā”ti. “Sirs, please give me permission in regard to my wife.”

“Kā nāma sā”ti? “What is it with her?”

“Mayhaṃ pajāpati aticarati, taṃ ghātessāmī”ti. “She’s unfaithful, and I want to kill her.”

“Jānāhī”ti. “You may go ahead.”

Assosi kho sā itthī— When his wife heard “sāmiko kira maṃ ghātetukāmo”ti. that her husband wanted to kill her, Varabhaṇḍaṃ ādāya sāvatthiṃ gantvā titthiye upasaṅkamitvā pabbajjaṃ yāci. she took their most valuable possessions and went to Sāvatthī. There she approached ascetics of non-Buddhist sects and asked for the going forth, Titthiyā na icchiṃsu pabbājetuṃ. but they refused. Bhikkhuniyo upasaṅkamitvā pabbajjaṃ yāci. She then went to the Buddhist nuns and again asked for the going forth, Bhikkhuniyopi na icchiṃsu pabbājetuṃ. but they too refused. Thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ upasaṅkamitvā bhaṇḍakaṃ dassetvā pabbajjaṃ yāci. She then went to the nun Thullanandā, showed her the goods, and once again asked for the going forth. Thullanandā bhikkhunī bhaṇḍakaṃ gahetvā pabbājesi. Thullanandā took the goods and gave her the going forth.

Atha kho so licchavi taṃ itthiṃ gavesanto sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhunīsu pabbajitaṃ disvāna yena rājā pasenadi kosalo tenupasaṅkami; That Licchavī man then went to Sāvatthī in search of his wife. When he saw that she had been given the going forth with the nuns, he went to King Pasenadi of Kosala upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ etadavoca— and said, “pajāpati me, deva, varabhaṇḍaṃ ādāya sāvatthiṃ anuppattā. “Sir, my wife took our most valuable possessions and came to Sāvatthī. Taṃ devo anujānātū”ti. Please give me permission in regard to her.”

“Tena hi, bhaṇe, vicinitvā ācikkhā”ti. “Well then, find her and then inform me.”

“Diṭṭhā, deva, bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā”ti. “Sir, I’ve seen her. She’s gone forth with the nuns.”

“Sace, bhaṇe, bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā, na sā labbhā kiñci kātuṃ. “If she’s gone forth with the nuns, there’s nothing that can be done. Svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, caratu brahmacariyaṃ sammā dukkhassa antakiriyāyā”ti. The Teaching of the Master is well-proclaimed. Let her practice the spiritual life for the full ending of suffering.”

Atha kho so licchavi ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— Then that Licchavī man complained and criticized the nuns, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo coriṃ pabbājessantī”ti. “How can the nuns give the going forth to a criminal?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tassa licchavissa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. The nuns heard the complaints of that Licchavī man, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā coriṃ pabbājessatī”ti. “How could Venerable Thullanandā give the going forth to a criminal?” Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … The nuns told the monks. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī coriṃ pabbājetī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī coriṃ pabbājessati. How can the nun Thullanandā give the going forth to a criminal? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī jānaṃ coriṃ vajjhaṃ viditaṃ anapaloketvā rājānaṃ vā saṃghaṃ vā gaṇaṃ vā pūgaṃ vā seṇiṃ vā aññatra kappā vuṭṭhāpeyya, ayampi bhikkhunī paṭhamāpattikaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. ‘If a nun, without getting permission from the king or the Order or a community or an association or a society, gives the full ordination to a woman who she knows is a criminal and who is known as sentenced to death, then, except when it is allowable, that nun too has committed an immediate offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Jānāti nāma Knows:
sāmaṃ vā jānāti aññe vā tassā ārocenti, sā vā āroceti. she knows by herself or others have told her or she has told her.
Corī nāma A criminal:
yā pañcamāsakaṃ vā atirekapañcamāsakaṃ vā agghanakaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyati, esā corī nāma. anyone who has stolen anything worth five _māsakas_ coins or more is called “a criminal.”
Vajjhā nāma Sentenced to death:
yaṃ katvā vajjhappattā hoti. she has been sentenced to death because of her action.
Viditā nāma Is known:
aññehi manussehi ñātā hoti “vajjhā esā”ti. it is known to other people that she has been sentenced to death.
Anapaloketvāti Without getting permission from:
anāpucchā. without having asked.
Rājā nāma The king:
yattha rājā anusāsati, rājā apaloketabbo. where a king governs, permission should be received from the king.
Saṃgho nāma The Order:
bhikkhunisaṃgho vuccati, bhikkhunisaṃgho apaloketabbo. what is meant is the Order of nuns, and permission should be received from that Order.
Gaṇo nāma A community:
yattha gaṇo anusāsati, gaṇo apaloketabbo. where a community governs, permission should be received from that community.
Pūgo nāma An association:
yattha pūgo anusāsati, pūgo apaloketabbo. where an association governs, permission should be received from that association.
Seṇi nāma A society:
yattha seṇi anusāsati, seṇi apaloketabbo. where a society governs, permission should be received from that society.
Aññatra kappāti Except when it is allowable:
ṭhapetvā kappaṃ. unless it is allowable.
Kappaṃ nāma Allowable:
dve kappāni— titthiyesu vā pabbajitā hoti aññāsu vā bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā. there are two allowable situations: she has gone forth with the ascetics of another sect or she has gone forth with the Buddhist nuns. Aññatra kappā “vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti gaṇaṃ vā ācariniṃ vā pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā pariyesati, sīmaṃ vā sammannati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, intending to give the full ordination, she searches for a group, a teacher, a bowl, or a robe, or she establishes a boundary, then, except when it is allowable, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. After the motion, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā. After each of the first two announcements, she commits a serious offense. Kammavācāpariyosāne upajjhāyāya āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When the last announcement is finished, the preceptor commits an offense entailing suspension; Gaṇassa ca ācariniyā ca āpatti dukkaṭassa. the group and the teacher commit an offense of wrong conduct.
Ayampīti That […] too:
purimaṃ upādāya vuccati. this is said with reference to the preceding offense.
Paṭhamāpattikanti An immediate offense:
saha vatthujjhācārā āpajjati asamanubhāsanāya. there is an offense as soon as the misconduct is committed, and no pressing is required.
Nissāraṇīyanti Sending away:
saṃghamhā nissārīyati. she is sent away from the Order.
Saṃghādisesanti Suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati saṃghādisesoti. … Therefore, too, it is called an offense entailing suspension.

Permutations

Coriyā corisaññā aññatra kappā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If she is a criminal, and the nun perceives her as a criminal, and she gives her the full ordination, except when it is allowable, she commits an offense entailing suspension. Coriyā vematikā aññatra kappā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is a criminal, but the nun is unsure if she is, and she gives her the full ordination, except when it is allowable, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Coriyā acorisaññā aññatra kappā vuṭṭhāpeti, anāpatti. If she is a criminal, but the nun does not perceive her as a criminal, and she gives her the full ordination, except when it is allowable, there is no offense.

Acoriyā corisaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not a criminal, but the nun perceives her as a criminal, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Acoriyā vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not a criminal, but the nun is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Acoriyā acorisaññā, anāpatti. If she is not a criminal, and the nun does not perceive her as a criminal, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ajānantī vuṭṭhāpeti, if she gives her the full ordination without knowing that she is a criminal; apaloketvā vuṭṭhāpeti, if she gives her the full ordination after getting permission; kappakataṃ vuṭṭhāpeti, if she gives her the full ordination when it is allowable; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dutiyasaṃghādisesasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second offense entailing suspension is finished.
