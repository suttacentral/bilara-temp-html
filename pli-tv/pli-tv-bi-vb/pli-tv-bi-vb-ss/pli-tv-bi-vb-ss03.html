Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

3. Ekagāmantaragamanasikkhāpada 3. The training rule on walking in uninhabited areas

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhaddāya kāpilāniyā antevāsinī bhikkhunī bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ bhaṇḍitvā gāmakaṃ ñātikulaṃ agamāsi. At that time a nun who was a student of Bhaddā Kāpilānī had an argument with the nuns and then went to her relatives’ village. Bhaddā kāpilānī taṃ bhikkhuniṃ apassantī bhikkhuniyo pucchi—“kahaṃ itthannāmā, na dissatī”ti. Because she had disappeared, Bhaddā Kāpilānī asked the nuns, “Where’s so-and-so? She’s not to be seen.”

“Bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ, ayye, bhaṇḍitvā na dissatī”ti. “Venerable, she’s disappeared because she had an argument with the nuns.”

“Ammā, amukasmiṃ gāmake etissā ñātikulaṃ. Tattha gantvā vicinathā”ti. “My dears, her relatives live in such-and-such a village. Go there and look for her.”

Bhikkhuniyo tattha gantvā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ passitvā etadavocuṃ—“kissa tvaṃ, ayye, ekikā āgatā, kaccisi appadhaṃsitā”ti? The nuns went there, and when they saw her they said to her, “Venerable, why did you go alone? We hope you weren’t assaulted?”

“Appadhaṃsitāmhi, ayye”ti. “I wasn’t assaulted, Venerables.”

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī ekā gāmantaraṃ gacchissatī”ti … pe … “How can a nun walk in an uninhabited area by herself?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā gāmantaraṃ gacchatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā gāmantaraṃ gacchissati. How can a nun walk in an uninhabited area by herself? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

First preliminary ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī ekā gāmantaraṃ gaccheyya, ayampi bhikkhunī paṭhamāpattikaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. ‘If a nun walks in an uninhabited area by herself, then that nun too has committed an immediate offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhunīnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the nuns.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena dve bhikkhuniyo sāketā sāvatthiṃ addhānamaggappaṭipannā honti. On one occasion two nuns were traveling on the main road from Sāketa to Sāvatthī. Antarāmagge nadī taritabbā hoti. On the way they had to cross a river, Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo nāvike upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ—“sādhu no, āvuso, tārethā”ti. and they approached a boatman and said, “Please take us across.”

“Nāyye, sakkā ubho sakiṃ tāretun”ti. “Venerables, I’m not able to take both of you across at once.” Eko ekaṃ uttāresi. And so they crossed individually, alone with the boatman. Uttiṇṇo uttiṇṇaṃ dūsesi. When he had crossed with the first nun, he raped her. Anuttiṇṇo anuttiṇṇaṃ dūsesi. And when he had returned to the first bank, he raped the other nun as well. Tā pacchā samāgantvā pucchiṃsu— Afterwards, when they were walking together, they asked each other, “kaccisi, ayye, appadhaṃsitā”ti? “Venerable, I hope you weren’t assaulted?”

“Padhaṃsitāmhi, ayye. “I was assaulted. Tvaṃ pana, ayye, appadhaṃsitā”ti? And you, Venerable, were you assaulted?”

“Padhaṃsitāmhi, ayye”ti. “I was assaulted, too.”

Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Then those nuns went to Sāvatthī and told the nuns there what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī ekā nadīpāraṃ gacchissatī”ti. “How can a nun cross a river by herself?” Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Then those nuns told the monks, Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … who in turn told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā nadīpāraṃ gacchatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā nadīpāraṃ gacchissati. How can a nun cross a river by herself? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Second preliminary ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī ekā vā gāmantaraṃ gaccheyya, ekā vā nadīpāraṃ gaccheyya, ayampi bhikkhunī paṭhamāpattikaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. ‘If a nun walks in an uninhabited area by herself or crosses a river by herself, then that nun too has committed an immediate offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhunīnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the nuns.

Third sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhuniyo kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchantā sāyaṃ aññataraṃ gāmaṃ upagacchiṃsu. On one occasion a number of nuns were walking through the Kosalan country on their way to Sāvatthī, when one evening they arrived at a certain village. Tattha aññatarā bhikkhunī abhirūpā hoti dassanīyā pāsādikā. Aññataro puriso tassā bhikkhuniyā saha dassanena paṭibaddhacitto hoti. One of those nuns was beautiful and graceful, and a certain man fell in love with her as soon as he saw her. Atha kho so puriso tāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ seyyaṃ paññapento tassā bhikkhuniyā seyyaṃ ekamantaṃ paññāpesi. Then, as he was preparing sleeping places for those nuns, he prepared hers to one side. Atha kho sā bhikkhunī sallakkhetvā—“pariyuṭṭhito ayaṃ puriso; sace rattiṃ āgacchissati, vissaro me bhavissatī”ti, And that nun thought, “This man is obsessed with me. If I go there for the night, I’ll get into trouble.” bhikkhuniyo anāpucchā aññataraṃ kulaṃ gantvā seyyaṃ kappesi. Then, without telling the nuns, she went to a certain family and slept there.

Atha kho so puriso rattiṃ āgantvā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ gavesanto bhikkhuniyo ghaṭṭesi. Then, when night had arrived that man went searching for that nun, and as he did so he knocked against the other nuns. Bhikkhuniyo taṃ bhikkhuniṃ apassantiyo evamāhaṃsu—“nissaṃsayaṃ kho sā bhikkhunī purisena saddhiṃ nikkhantā”ti. Because they could not see that nun, those nuns said, “No doubt she has left with a man.”

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī tassā rattiyā accayena yena tā bhikkhuniyo tenupasaṅkami. Bhikkhuniyo taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ—“kissa tvaṃ, ayye, purisena saddhiṃ nikkhantā”ti? When the night was over that nun returned to the nuns, and they said to her, “Venerable, why did you leave with a man?”

“Nāhaṃ, ayye, purisena saddhiṃ nikkhantā”ti. “I didn’t leave with a man, Venerables.”

Bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. She then told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī ekā rattiṃ vippavasissatī”ti … pe … “How can a nun spend the night apart by herself?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā rattiṃ vippavasatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā rattiṃ vippavasissati. How can a nun spent the night apart by herself? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Third preliminary ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī ekā vā gāmantaraṃ gaccheyya, ekā vā nadīpāraṃ gaccheyya, ekā vā rattiṃ vippavaseyya, ayampi bhikkhunī paṭhamāpattikaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. ‘If a nun walks in an uninhabited area by herself or crosses a river by herself or spends the night apart by herself, then that nun too has committed an immediate offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhunīnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the nuns.

Fourth sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhuniyo kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ addhānamaggappaṭipannā honti. On one occasion a number of nuns were walking on a main road through the Kosalan country on their way to Sāvatthī. Tattha aññatarā bhikkhunī vaccena pīḷitā ekikā ohīyitvā pacchā agamāsi. One of the nuns, needing to defecate, stayed behind by herself, and then followed behind the others. Manussā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ passitvā dūsesuṃ. People saw her and raped her. Atha kho sā bhikkhunī yena tā bhikkhuniyo tenupasaṅkami. Bhikkhuniyo taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ—“kissa tvaṃ, ayye, ekikā ohīnā, kaccisi appadhaṃsitā”ti? That nun then went to the other nuns, and they said to her, “Venerable, why did you stay behind by yourself? We hope you weren’t assaulted?”

“Padhaṃsitāmhi, ayye”ti. “I was assaulted, Venerables.”

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī ekā gaṇamhā ohīyissatīti … pe … “How can a nun stay behind the group by herself?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā gaṇamhā ohīyatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī ekā gaṇamhā ohīyissati. How can a nun stay behind the group by herself? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī ekā vā gāmantaraṃ gaccheyya, ekā vā nadīpāraṃ gaccheyya, ekā vā rattiṃ vippavaseyya, ekā vā gaṇamhā ohīyeyya, ayampi bhikkhunī paṭhamāpattikaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. ‘If a nun walks in an uninhabited area by herself or crosses a river by herself or spends the night apart by herself or stays behind a group by herself, then that nun too has committed an immediate offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Ekā vā gāmantaraṃ gaccheyyāti Walks in an uninhabited area by herself:
parikkhittassa gāmassa parikkhepaṃ paṭhamaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti thullaccayassa, if she crosses the boundary of an enclosed village with her first foot, she commits a serious offense. dutiyaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If she then crosses it with her second foot, she commits an offense entailing suspension. Aparikkhittassa gāmassa upacāraṃ paṭhamaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti thullaccayassa. If she goes beyond the vicinity of an unenclosed village with her first foot, she commits a serious offense. Dutiyaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If she then goes beyond with her second foot, she commits an offense entailing suspension.
Ekā vā nadīpāraṃ gaccheyyāti Or crosses a river by herself:
nadī nāma timaṇḍalaṃ paṭicchādetvā yattha katthaci uttarantiyā bhikkhuniyā antaravāsako temiyati. wherever, after covering the three circles, the sarong gets wet when the nun is crossing, this is called a “river.” Paṭhamaṃ pādaṃ uttarantiyā āpatti thullaccayassa. When she has crossed with the first foot, she commits a serious offense. Dutiyaṃ pādaṃ uttarantiyā āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When she has crossed with the second foot, she commits an offense entailing suspension.
Ekā vā rattiṃ vippavaseyyāti Or spends the night apart by herself:
saha aruṇuggamanā dutiyikāya bhikkhuniyā hatthapāsaṃ vijahantiyā āpatti thullaccayassa. if she is in the process of going beyond arm’s reach of her companion nun at dawn, she commits a serious offense. Vijahite āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When she has gone beyond, she commits an offense entailing suspension.
Ekā vā gaṇamhā ohīyeyyāti Or stays behind a group by herself:
agāmake araññe dutiyikāya bhikkhuniyā dassanūpacāraṃ vā savanūpacāraṃ vā vijahantiyā āpatti thullaccayassa. if, in an uninhabited area, in the wilderness, she is in the process of going beyond eyesight or earshot of her companion nun, she commits a serious offense. Vijahite āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When she has gone beyond, she commits an offense entailing suspension.
Ayampīti That […] too:
purimāyo upādāya vuccati. this is said with reference to the preceding offenses.
Paṭhamāpattikanti An immediate offense:
saha vatthujjhācārā āpajjati asamanubhāsanāya. there is an offense as soon as the misconduct is committed, and no pressing is required.
Nissāraṇīyanti Sending away:
saṃghamhā nissārīyati. she is sent away from the Order.
Saṃghādisesoti Suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati saṃghādisesoti. … Therefore, too, it is called an offense entailing suspension.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: dutiyikā bhikkhunī pakkantā vā hoti vibbhantā vā kālaṅkatā vā pakkhasaṅkantā vā, if her companion nun has left or disrobed or died or joined another faction; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Tatiyasaṃghādisesasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third offense entailing suspension is finished.
