Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Saṃghādisesakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing suspension

12. Pāpasamācārasikkhāpada 12. The training rule on bad behavior

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā antevāsikā bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. At that time the student nuns of the nun Thullanandā were constantly socializing, were behaving badly, and had a bad reputation, and they were harassing the Order of nuns and hiding one another’s offenses.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharissanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā”ti … pe … “How can those nuns constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns are acting like this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharissanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. How can nuns constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses?” Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Bhikkhuniyo paneva saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. ‘If nuns constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses, Tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhunīhi evamassu vacanīyā— then the nuns should correct them in this way: ‘bhaginiyo kho saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. “Sisters, you constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses. Viviccathāyye. Vivekaññeva bhaginīnaṃ saṃgho vaṇṇetī’ti. Be secluded, Venerables. The Order praises seclusion for the Sisters.” Evañca tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhunīhi vuccamānā tatheva paggaṇheyyuṃ, tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhunīhi yāvatatiyaṃ samanubhāsitabbā tassa paṭinissaggāya. If those nuns still continue as before, the nuns should press them up to three times to make them stop. Yāvatatiyañce samanubhāsīyamānā taṃ paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; If they then stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, imāpi bhikkhuniyo yāvatatiyakaṃ dhammaṃ āpannā nissāraṇīyaṃ saṃghādisesan”ti. If not, then those nuns too have committed a third-time offense entailing sending away and suspension.’”

Definitions

Bhikkhuniyo panevāti Nuns:
upasampannāyo vuccanti. what is meant is that they are fully ordained.
Saṃsaṭṭhā viharantīti Constantly socialize:
saṃsaṭṭhā nāma ananulomikena kāyikavācasikena saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti. they constantly socialize with improper bodily and verbal action.
Pāpācārāti Behave badly:
pāpakena ācārena samannāgatā. having bad behavior.
Pāpasaddāti Have a bad reputation:
pāpakena kittisaddena abbhuggatā. a bad reputation has spread about them.
Pāpasilokāti Are notorious:
pāpakena micchājīvena jīvitaṃ kappenti. they make a living by means of a bad and wrong livelihood.
Bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikāti Harass the Order of nuns:
aññamaññissā kamme karīyamāne paṭikkosanti. they protest on each other’s behalf when legal procedures are being carried out against them.
Aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikāti Hide each other’s offenses:
aññamaññaṃ vajjaṃ paṭicchādenti. they hide one another’s offenses.
Tā bhikkhuniyoti Them:
yā tā saṃsaṭṭhā bhikkhuniyo. those nuns who constantly socialize.
Bhikkhunīhīti The nuns:

aññāhi bhikkhunīhi. Yā passanti yā suṇanti tāhi vattabbā— Other nuns, those who see it or hear about it. They should say,

“bhaginiyo kho saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. “Sisters, you constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses. Viviccathāyye. Vivekaññeva bhaginīnaṃ saṃgho vaṇṇetī”ti. Be secluded, Venerables. The Order praises seclusion for the Sisters.”

Dutiyampi vattabbā. And they should say this a second Tatiyampi vattabbā. and a third time. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; If they stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If they do not stop, they commit an offense of wrong conduct. Sutvā na vadanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If those who heard about it do not say anything, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Tā bhikkhuniyo saṃghamajjhampi ākaḍḍhitvā vattabbā— Those nuns, even if they have to be dragged into the middle of the Order, should be spoken to thus:

“bhaginiyo kho saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. “Sisters, you constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses. Viviccathāyye. Vivekaññeva bhaginīnaṃ saṃgho vaṇṇetī”ti. Be secluded, Venerables. The Order praises seclusion for the Sisters.”

Dutiyampi vattabbā. They should say this a second Tatiyampi vattabbā. and a third time. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; If they stop, all is well. no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If they do not stop, they commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Tā bhikkhuniyo samanubhāsitabbā. Should press them:

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samanubhāsitabbā. “And, monks, they should be pressed in this way. Byattāya bhikkhuniyā paṭibalāya saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable nun should inform the Order:

“Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Itthannāmā ca itthannāmā ca bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. The nuns so-and-so and so-and-so constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses. Tā taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. They keep on doing it. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhuniyo samanubhāseyya tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should press them to make them stop. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Itthannāmā ca itthannāmā ca bhikkhuniyo saṃsaṭṭhā viharanti pāpācārā pāpasaddā pāpasilokā, bhikkhunisaṃghassa vihesikā, aññamaññissā vajjappaṭicchādikā. The nuns so-and-so and so-and-so constantly socialize, behave badly, have a bad reputation, are notorious, harass the Order of nuns, and hide each other’s offenses. Tā taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. They keep on doing it. Saṃgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhuniyo samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. The Order presses them to make them stop. Yassā ayyāya khamati itthannāmāya ca itthannāmāya ca bhikkhunīnaṃ samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, sā tuṇhassa; Any nun who approves of pressing them to make them stop should remain silent. yassā nakkhamati, sā bhāseyya. Any nun who does not approve should speak up.

Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi … pe … For the second time I speak on this matter … tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi … pe …. For the third time I speak on this matter …

Samanubhaṭṭhā saṃghena, itthannāmā ca itthannāmā ca bhikkhuniyo tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. The nuns so-and-so and so-and-so have been pressed by the Order to make them stop. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, After the motion, they commit an offense of wrong conduct. dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā, After each of the first two announcements, they commit a serious offense. kammavācāpariyosāne āpatti saṃghādisesassa. When the last announcement is finished, they commit an offense entailing suspension. Saṃghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjantīnaṃ ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā paṭippassambhanti. If they commit the offense entailing suspension, the offense of wrong conduct and the serious offenses are annulled. Dve tisso ekato samanubhāsitabbā. Tatuttari na samanubhāsitabbā. Two or three may be pressed at once, but not more than that.

Imāpi bhikkhuniyoti Those nuns too:
purimāyo upādāya vuccanti. this is said with reference to the preceding offenses.
Yāvatatiyakanti Third-time:
yāvatatiyaṃ samanubhāsanāya āpajjanti, na saha vatthujjhācārā. there is an offense when they have been pressed for the third time, not as soon as the misconduct has been committed.
Nissāraṇīyanti Sending away:
saṃghamhā nissārīyati. they are sent away from the Order.
Saṃghādisesoti Suspension:
… pe … tenapi vuccati saṃghādisesoti. … Therefore, too, it is called an offense entailing suspension.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññā na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as legitimate, and she does not stop, she commits an offense entailing suspension. Dhammakamme vematikā na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, and she does not stop, she commits an offense entailing suspension. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññā na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṃghādisesassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as illegitimate, and she does not stop, she commits an offense entailing suspension.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as legitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as illegitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: asamanubhāsantīnaṃ, if she has not been pressed; paṭinissajjantīnaṃ, if she stops; ummattikānaṃ, if she is insane; ādikammikānanti. if she is the first offender.

Navamasaṃghādisesasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The twelfth offense entailing suspension is finished.
