Pāṭidesanīyā 2: Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

2. Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

2.5 Pāṭidesanīyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing acknowledgment

2.5.2–8 Tela-madhu-phāṇita-maccha-maṃsa-khīra-dadhiviññāpanasikkhāpada 2-8. The training rules on asking for oil/honey/sugar/fish/meat/milk/curds

Origin story

First sub-story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo telaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. At that time the nuns from the group of six ate oil after asking for it . …pe… madhuṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … ate honey after asking for it. …pe… phāṇitaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … ate sugar after asking for it. …pe… macchaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … ate fish after asking for it. …pe… maṃsaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … ate meat after asking for it. …pe… khīraṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … drank milk after asking for it. …pe… dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. … ate curds after asking for them.

Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjissanti. “How can nuns eat curds after asking for them? Kassa sampannaṃ na manāpaṃ, kassa sāduṃ na ruccatī”ti. Who doesn’t like nice food? Who doesn’t want tasty food?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjissantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns from the group of six eat curds after asking for them?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that those nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjissanti. How can the nuns from the group of six eat curds after asking for them? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

First preliminary ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjeyya, paṭidesetabbaṃ tāya bhikkhuniyā— ‘If a nun asks for curds and then eats them, she must acknowledge it: ‘gārayhaṃ, ayye, dhammaṃ āpajjiṃ asappāyaṃ pāṭidesanīyaṃ, taṃ paṭidesemī’”ti. “I have done a blameworthy and unsuitable thing which is to be acknowledged. I acknowledge it.”’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhunīnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti. In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the nuns.

Second sub-story

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo gilānā honti. Soon afterwards a number of nuns were sick. Gilānapucchikā bhikkhuniyo gilānā bhikkhuniyo etadavocuṃ— The nuns who were looking after them asked, “kacci, ayye, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyan”ti? “We hope you’re bearing up; we hope you’re getting better?”

“Pubbe mayaṃ, ayye, dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjimhā, tena no phāsu hoti; “Previously we ate curds after asking for them, and then we were comfortable. idāni pana ‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittan’ti kukkuccāyantā na viññāpema, tena no na phāsu hotī”ti … pe … But now that this has been prohibited by the Master, we don’t ask because we’re afraid of wrongdoing. And because of that we’re not comfortable.” …

bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … They told the Master. … anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānāya bhikkhuniyā dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjituṃ. “Monks, I allow a sick nun to eat curds after asking for them.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And so, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī agilānā (telaṃ…pe… madhuṃ…pe… phāṇitaṃ…pe… macchaṃ…pe… maṃsaṃ…pe… khīraṃ…pe… ) dadhiṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjeyya, paṭidesetabbaṃ tāya bhikkhuniyā— ‘If a nun who is not sick asks for (oil … honey … sugar … fish … meat … milk … ) curds and then eats them, she must acknowledge it: ‘gārayhaṃ, ayye, dhammaṃ āpajjiṃ asappāyaṃ pāṭidesanīyaṃ, taṃ paṭidesemī’”ti. “I have done a blameworthy and unsuitable thing which is to be acknowledged. I acknowledge it.”’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Agilānā nāma Not sick:
yassā vinā telanā phāsu hoti. one who is comfortable without curds.
Gilānā nāma Sick:
yassā vinā telanā na phāsu hoti. one who is not comfortable without curds.
Telaṃ nāma Oil:
tilatelaṃ sāsapatelaṃ madhukatelaṃ eraṇḍatelaṃ vasātelaṃ. sesame oil, mustard seed oil, honey tree oil, castor oil, oil from tallow.
Madhu nāma Honey:
makkhikāmadhu. honey from bees.
Phāṇitaṃ nāma Sugar:
ucchumhā nibbattaṃ. from sugar cane.
Maccho nāma Fish:
odako vuccati. what lives in water is what is meant.
Maṃsaṃ nāma Meat:
yesaṃ maṃsaṃ kappati tesaṃ maṃsaṃ. the meat of those animals whose meat is allowable.
Khīraṃ nāma Milk:
gokhīraṃ vā ajikākhīraṃ vā mahiṃsakhīraṃ vā yesaṃ maṃsaṃ kappati tesaṃ khīraṃ. milk from cows, milk from goats, milk from buffaloes, or milk from whatever animal whose meat is allowable.
Dadhi nāma Curds:
tesaññeva dadhi. curd from those same animals.

Agilānā attano atthāya viññāpeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If she is not sick and she asks for her own benefit, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena “bhuñjissāmī”ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. When she receives it with the intention of eating it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. For every mouthful, she commits an offense entailing acknowledgment.

Permutations

Agilānā agilānasaññā telaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjati, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is not sick, and she does not perceive herself as sick, and she eats curds after asking for them, she commits an offense entailing acknowledgment. Agilānā vematikā telaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjati, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is not sick, but she is unsure of it, and she eats curds after asking for them, she commits an offense entailing acknowledgment. Agilānā gilānasaññā telaṃ viññāpetvā bhuñjati, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. If she is not sick, but she perceives herself as sick, and she eats curds after asking for them, she commits an offense entailing acknowledgment.

Gilānā agilānasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is sick, but she does not perceive herself as sick, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Gilānā vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is sick, but she is unsure if she is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Gilānā gilānasaññā anāpatti. If she is sick, and she perceives herself as sick, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: gilānāya, if she is sick; gilānā hutvā viññāpetvā agilānā bhuñjati, if she asked for them when she was sick, but eats them when she is no longer sick; gilānāya sesakaṃ bhuñjati, if she eats the leftovers from one who is sick; ñātakānaṃ, if it is from relatives; pavāritānaṃ, if it is from those who have given an invitation; aññassatthāya, if it is for the benefit of someone else; attano dhanena, if it is by means of her own property; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Aṭṭhamapāṭidesanīyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The eighth training rule entailing acknowledgment is finished.

Uddiṭṭhā kho, ayyāyo, aṭṭha pāṭidesanīyā dhammā. “Venerables, the eight rules on offenses entailing acknowledgment have been recited. Tatthāyyāyo pucchāmi— In regard to this I ask you, “kaccittha parisuddhā”? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Dutiyampi pucchāmi— A second time I ask, “kaccittha parisuddhā”? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Tatiyampi pucchāmi— A third time I ask, “kaccittha parisuddhā”? ‘Are you pure in this?’ Parisuddhetthāyyāyo, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti. You are pure in this and therefore silent. I will remember it thus.”

Bhikkhunivibhaṅge pāṭidesanīyakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The chapter on offenses entailing acknowledgment in the nuns’ analysis is finished.
