Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Cittāgāravagga The sub-chapter on pleasure houses

41. Rājāgārasikkhāpada 41. The training rule on royal houses

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena rañño pasenadissa kosalassa uyyāne cittāgāre paṭibhānacittaṃ kataṃ hoti. At that time a picture had been drawn in the decorated pleasure-house in King Pasenadi of Kosala’s park. Bahū manussā cittāgāraṃ dassanāya gacchanti. Many people went to see the pleasure-house, Chabbaggiyāpi bhikkhuniyo cittāgāraṃ dassanāya agamaṃsu. and so did the nuns from the group of six. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo cittāgāraṃ dassanāya gacchissanti, seyyathāpi gihiniyo kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “How can nuns go to see the pleasure-house? They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo cittāgāraṃ dassanāya gacchissantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns from the group of six go to see a pleasure-house?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo cittāgāraṃ dassanāya gacchantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that those nuns did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo cittāgāraṃ dassanāya gacchissanti. How can the nuns from the group of six go to see a pleasure-house? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī rājāgāraṃ vā cittāgāraṃ vā ārāmaṃ vā uyyānaṃ vā pokkharaṇiṃ vā dassanāya gaccheyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun goes to see a royal house or a pleasure-house or a park or a garden or a lotus pond, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Rājāgāraṃ nāma A royal house:
yattha katthaci rañño kīḷituṃ ramituṃ kataṃ hoti. wherever one has been built for a king to play and enjoy himself.
Cittāgāraṃ nāma A pleasure-house:
yattha katthaci manussānaṃ kīḷituṃ ramituṃ kataṃ hoti. wherever one has been built for a people to play and enjoy themselves.
Ārāmo nāma A park:
yattha katthaci manussānaṃ kīḷituṃ ramituṃ kato hoti. wherever one has been made for a people to play and enjoy themselves.
Uyyānaṃ nāma A garden:
yattha katthaci manussānaṃ kīḷituṃ ramituṃ kataṃ hoti. wherever one has been made for a people to play and enjoy themselves.
Pokkharaṇī nāma A lotus pond:
yattha katthaci manussānaṃ kīḷituṃ ramituṃ katā hoti. wherever one has been made for a people to play and enjoy themselves.

Dassanāya gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is on her way to see them, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Yattha ṭhitā passati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Wherever she stands to see them, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dassanūpacāraṃ vijahitvā punappunaṃ passati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Every time she moves outside the range of sight and then sees them again, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Ekamekaṃ dassanāya gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is on her way to see any one of them, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Yattha ṭhitā passati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Wherever she stands to see it, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dassanūpacāraṃ vijahitvā punappunaṃ passati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Every time she moves outside the range of sight and then sees it again, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ārāme ṭhitā passati, if she sees it while standing in the monastery; gacchantī vā āgacchantī vā passati, if she sees it while coming or going; sati karaṇīye gantvā passati, if she goes on some business and then sees it; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The first training rule is finished.
