Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

29. Kālaatikkamanasikkhāpada 29. The training rule on going beyond the time

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā upaṭṭhākakulaṃ thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavoca— At that time a family who was supporting the nun Thullanandā said to her, “sace mayaṃ, ayye, sakkoma, bhikkhunisaṃghassa cīvaraṃ dassāmā”ti. “Venerable, if we’re able, we’ll give robe-cloth to the Order of nuns.”

Tena kho pana samayena vassaṃvuṭṭhā bhikkhuniyo cīvaraṃ bhājetukāmā sannipatiṃsu. Just then nuns who had completed the rainy-season residence had assembled to distribute robe-cloth. Thullanandā bhikkhunī tā bhikkhuniyo etadavoca— But Thullanandā said to them, “āgametha, ayye, atthi bhikkhunisaṃghassa cīvarapaccāsā”ti. “Please wait, Venerables. The Order is expecting more cloth.” Bhikkhuniyo thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ— The nuns said to her, “gacchāyye, taṃ cīvaraṃ jānāhī”ti. “Go, Venerable, and find out about it.” Thullanandā bhikkhunī yena taṃ kulaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te manusse etadavoca— She then went to that family and said to them, “dethāvuso, bhikkhunisaṃghassa cīvaran”ti. “Please give the robe-cloth to the Order of nuns.”

“Na mayaṃ, ayye, sakkoma bhikkhunisaṃghassa cīvaraṃ dātun”ti. “We’re not able to give robe-cloth to the Order.”

Thullanandā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Thullanandā told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā dubbalacīvarapaccāsāya cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmessatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā let the robe season pass because of an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dubbalacīvarapaccāsāya cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmetī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dubbalacīvarapaccāsāya cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmessati. How can the nun Thullanandā let the robe season pass because of an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dubbalacīvarapaccāsāya cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun lets the robe season pass because of an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Dubbalacīvarapaccāsā nāma An uncertain expectation of robe-cloth:
“sace mayaṃ sakkoma, dassāma karissāmā”ti vācā bhinnā hoti. they have said, “If we’re able, then we’ll give, then we’ll act.”
Cīvarakālasamayo nāma Robe season:
anatthate kathine vassānassa pacchimo māso, atthate kathine pañca māsā. for one who has not done the robe-making ceremony, it is the last month of the rainy season; for one who has done the robe-making ceremony, it is the five month period.
Cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmeyyāti Lets the robe season pass:
anatthate kathine vassānassa pacchimaṃ divasaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. for one who has not done the robe-making ceremony, if she goes beyond the last day of the rainy season, she commits an offense entailing confession. Atthate kathine kathinuddhāradivasaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. For one who has done the robe-making ceremony, if she goes beyond the day on which the robe-making season ends, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dubbalacīvare dubbalacīvarasaññā cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, and she perceives it as such, and she lets the robe season pass, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dubbalacīvare vematikā cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, but she is unsure if it is, and she lets the robe season pass, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dubbalacīvare adubbalacīvarasaññā cīvarakālasamayaṃ atikkāmeti, anāpatti. If it is an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, but she does not perceive it as such, and she lets the robe season pass, there is no offense.

Adubbalacīvare dubbalacīvarasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, but she perceives it as an uncertain expectation, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adubbalacīvare vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adubbalacīvare adubbalacīvarasaññā, anāpatti. If it is not an uncertain expectation of robe-cloth, and she does not perceive it as an uncertain expectation, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ānisaṃsaṃ dassetvā nivāreti, if she hinders it after showing a benefit; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The ninth training rule is finished.
