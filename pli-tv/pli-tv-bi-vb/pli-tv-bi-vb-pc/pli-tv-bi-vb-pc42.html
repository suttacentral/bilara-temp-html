Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Cittāgāravagga The sub-chapter on pleasure houses

42. Āsandiparibhuñjanasikkhāpada 42. The training rule on using high seats

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo āsandimpi pallaṅkampi paribhuñjanti. At that time the nuns used high and luxurious seats. Manussā vihāracārikaṃ āhiṇḍantā passitvā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— When people who were walking about the dwellings saw this, they complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo āsandimpi pallaṅkampi paribhuñjissanti, seyyathāpi gihiniyo kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “How can nuns use high and luxurious seats? They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo āsandimpi pallaṅkampi paribhuñjissantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns use high and luxurious seats?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo āsandimpi pallaṅkampi paribhuñjantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do that?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo āsandimpi pallaṅkampi paribhuñjissanti. How can nuns use high and luxurious seats? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī āsandiṃ vā pallaṅkaṃ vā paribhuñjeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun uses a high or luxurious seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Āsandī nāma A high seat:
atikkantappamāṇā vuccati. one that is over-size is what is meant.
Pallaṅko nāma A luxurious seat:
āharimehi vāḷehi kato hoti. one decorated with images of wild animals.
Paribhuñjeyyāti Uses:
tasmiṃ abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she sits down or lies down on it, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: āsandiyā pāde chinditvā paribhuñjati, if she uses a high seat after cutting off the legs; pallaṅkassa vāḷe bhinditvā paribhuñjati, if she uses a luxurious seat after removing the decorations with images of wild animals; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second training rule is finished.
