Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Lasuṇavagga The sub-chapter on garlic

6. Upatiṭṭhanasikkhāpada 6. The training rule on attending on

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena ārohanto nāma mahāmatto bhikkhūsu pabbajito hoti. At that time a government official called Ārohanta had been given the full ordination as a monk Tassa purāṇadutiyikā bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā hoti. and his former wife as a nun. Tena kho pana samayena so bhikkhu tassā bhikkhuniyā santike bhattavissaggaṃ karoti. On one occasion that monk had his meal in the presence of that nun. Atha kho sā bhikkhunī tassa bhikkhuno bhuñjantassa pānīyena ca vidhūpanena ca upatiṭṭhitvā accāvadati. Then, while he was eating, she attended on him with drinks and a fan, and she flirted with him. Atha kho so bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuniṃ apasādeti— But the monk dismissed her, saying, “mā, bhagini, evarūpaṃ akāsi. “Don’t do that; Netaṃ kappatī”ti. it’s not allowable.”

“Pubbe maṃ tvaṃ evañca evañca karosi, idāni ettakaṃ na sahasī”ti— “Before you did such and such to me, but now you’re not able to take even this much.” pānīyathālakaṃ matthake āsumbhitvā vidhūpanena pahāraṃ adāsi. And she dropped the water vessel upside down and struck him with the fan.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī bhikkhussa pahāraṃ dassatī”ti … pe … “How can a nun strike a monk?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī bhikkhussa pahāraṃ adāsī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī bhikkhussa pahāraṃ dassati. “How can a nun strike a monk? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhussa bhuñjantassa pānīyena vā vidhūpanena vā upatiṭṭheyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If, when a monk is eating, a nun attends on him with water or a fan, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Bhikkhussāti A monk:
upasampannassa. fully ordained.
Bhuñjantassāti Is eating:
pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññataraṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjantassa. is eating any of the five staple food.
Pānīyaṃ nāma Water:
yaṃ kiñci pānīyaṃ. any kind of drink.
Vidhūpanaṃ nāma A fan:
yā kāci bījanī. any kind of fan.
Upatiṭṭheyyāti Attends on:
hatthapāse tiṭṭhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she stands within arm’s reach, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampanne upasampannasaññā pānīyena vā vidhūpanena vā upatiṭṭhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he is fully ordained, and she perceives him as fully ordained, and she attends on him with drinks or with a fan, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne vematikā pānīyena vā vidhūpanena vā upatiṭṭhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he is fully ordained, but she is unsure if he is, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññā pānīyena vā vidhūpanena vā upatiṭṭhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If he is fully ordained, but she does not perceive him as fully ordained, and she attends on him with drinks or with a fan, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Hatthapāsaṃ vijahitvā upatiṭṭhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she attends on him from beyond arm’s reach, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Khādanīyaṃ khādantassa upatiṭṭhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she attends on him when he is eating non-staple food, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannassa upatiṭṭhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she attends on one who is not fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anupasampanne upasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he is not fully ordained, but she perceives him as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he is not fully ordained, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he is not fully ordained, and she does not perceive him as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: deti, if she gives it; dāpeti, if she gets someone else to give it; anupasampannaṃ āṇāpeti, if she asks someone who is not fully ordained to do it; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
