Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Ārāmavagga The sub-chapter on monasteries

60. Pasākhejātasikkhāpada 60. The training rule on what is growing on the thigh

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī pasākhe jātaṃ gaṇḍaṃ purisena saddhiṃ ekenekā bhedāpesi. At that time a nun, being alone with a man, had an abscess growing on her thigh ruptured. Atha kho so puriso taṃ bhikkhuniṃ dūsetuṃ upakkami. Then that man tried to rape that nun, Sā vissaramakāsi. and she cried out. Bhikkhuniyo upadhāvitvā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ— The nuns came rushing to and asked her why. “kissa tvaṃ, ayye, vissaramakāsī”ti?

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. And she told them what had happened.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī pasākhe jātaṃ gaṇḍaṃ purisena saddhiṃ ekenekā bhedāpessatī”ti … pe … “How could a nun, being alone with a man, get an abscess growing on her thigh ruptured?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī pasākhe jātaṃ gaṇḍaṃ purisena saddhiṃ ekenekā bhedāpetī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī pasākhe jātaṃ gaṇḍaṃ purisena saddhiṃ ekenekā bhedāpessati. How can a nun, being alone with a man, get an abscess growing on her thigh ruptured? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī pasākhe jātaṃ gaṇḍaṃ vā rudhitaṃ vā anapaloketvā saṃghaṃ vā gaṇaṃ vā purisena saddhiṃ ekenekā bhedāpeyya vā phālāpeyya vā dhovāpeyya vā ālimpāpeyya vā bandhāpeyya vā mocāpeyya vā, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun, alone with a man, without asking permission from the Order or a group, gets an abscess or a wound situated on her thigh ruptured or split open or washed or anointed or bandaged or unwrapped, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Pasākhaṃ nāma Thigh:
adhonābhi ubbhajāṇumaṇḍalaṃ. below the navel and above the knees.
Jātanti Situated:
tattha jātaṃ. situated there.
Gaṇḍo nāma An abscess:
yo koci gaṇḍo. any kind of abscess.
Rudhitaṃ nāma A wound:
yaṃ kiñci vaṇaṃ. any kind of sore.
Anapaloketvāti Without asking permission:
anāpucchā. without having asked for permission.
Saṃgho nāma The Order:
bhikkhunisaṃgho vuccati. the Order of nuns is what is meant.
Gaṇo nāma A group:
sambahulā bhikkhuniyo vuccanti. several nuns is what is meant.
Puriso nāma A man:
manussapuriso, na yakkho, na peto, na tiracchānagato, viññū paṭibalo dūsetuṃ. a human male, not a male spirit, not a male ghost, not a male animal, one who understands and is capable of raping.
Saddhinti With:
ekato. together.
Ekenekāti Alone:
puriso ceva hoti bhikkhunī ca. just the man and the nun.

“Bhindā”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Rupture it,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Bhinne āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been ruptured, she commits an offense entailing confession. “Phālehī”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Split it open,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Phālite āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been split open, she commits an offense entailing confession. “Dhovā”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Wash it,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dhovite āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been washed, she commits an offense entailing confession. “Ālimpā”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Anoint it,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Litte āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been anointed, she commits an offense entailing confession. “Bandhāhī”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Bandage it,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Baddhe āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been bandaged, she commits an offense entailing confession. “Mocehī”ti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she says, “Unwrap it,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Mutte āpatti pācittiyassa. When it has been unwrapped, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: apaloketvā bhedāpeti vā phālāpeti vā dhovāpeti vā ālimpāpeti vā bandhāpeti vā mocāpeti vā, if she asks for permission and then gets it ruptured or split open or washed or anointed or bandaged or unwrapped; yā kāci viññū dutiyikā hoti, if she has a female companion who understands; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The tenth training rule is finished.

Ārāmavaggo chaṭṭho. The sixth chapter on monasteries is finished.
