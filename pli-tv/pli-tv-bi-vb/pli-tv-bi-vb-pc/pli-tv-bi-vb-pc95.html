Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Chattupāhanavagga The sub-chapter on sunshades and sandals

95. Pañhāpucchanasikkhāpada 95. The training rule on asking questions

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo anokāsakataṃ bhikkhuṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti. At that time the nuns asked questions of monks who had not given leave. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo anokāsakataṃ bhikkhuṃ pañhaṃ pucchissantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns ask questions of monks who have not given leave?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo anokāsakataṃ bhikkhuṃ pañhaṃ pucchantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo anokāsakataṃ bhikkhuṃ pañhaṃ pucchissanti. How can nuns ask questions of monks who have not given leave? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī anokāsakataṃ bhikkhuṃ pañhaṃ puccheyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun asks a question of a monk who has not given leave, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Anokāsakatanti Who has not given leave:
anāpucchā. without asking for permission.
Bhikkhunti A monk:
upasampannaṃ. one who is fully ordained.
Pañhaṃ puccheyyāti Asks a question:
suttante okāsaṃ kārāpetvā vinayaṃ vā abhidhammaṃ vā pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she gets leave to ask concerning the discourses, but she asks concerning the Monastic Law or philosophy, she commits an offense entailing confession. Vinaye okāsaṃ kārāpetvā suttantaṃ vā abhidhammaṃ vā pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she gets leave to ask concerning the Monastic Law, but she asks concerning the discourses or philosophy, she commits an offense entailing confession. Abhidhamme okāsaṃ kārāpetvā suttantaṃ vā vinayaṃ vā pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she gets leave to ask concerning philosophy, but she asks concerning the discourses or the Monastic Law, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Anāpucchite anāpucchitasaññā pañhaṃ pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, and she perceives that she has not, and she asks a question, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite vematikā pañhaṃ pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she is unsure of it, and she asks a question, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite āpucchitasaññā pañhaṃ pucchati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she perceives that she has, and she asks a question, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Āpucchite anāpucchitasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she perceives that she has not, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she is unsure if she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite āpucchitasaññā, anāpatti. If she has asked for permission, and she perceives that she has, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: okāsaṃ kārāpetvā pucchati, if she asks after getting leave; anodissa okāsaṃ kārāpetvā yattha katthaci pucchati, if she gets leave, but not in regard to a specific subject, and she then asks about any subject; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dvādasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The twelfth training rule is finished.
