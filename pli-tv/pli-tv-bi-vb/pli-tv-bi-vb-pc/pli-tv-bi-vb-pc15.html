Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Rattandhakāravagga The sub-chapter on the dark of the night

15. Anāpucchāpakkamanasikkhāpada 15. The training rule on leaving without telling

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī aññatarassa kulassa kulūpikā hoti niccabhattikā. At that time a certain nun was associating with a family from which she received a regular meal. Atha kho sā bhikkhunī pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena taṃ kulaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āsane nisīditvā sāmike anāpucchā pakkāmi. One morning, after robing up, she took her bowl and robe, and went to that family, where she sat down on a seat. She then left without telling the lay people. Tassa kulassa dāsī gharaṃ sammajjantī taṃ āsanaṃ bhājanantarikāya pakkhipi. Soon afterwards a slave-woman was sweeping the house and she put that seat in between some vessels. Manussā taṃ āsanaṃ apassantā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ— Not seeing the seat, the people there asked that nun, “kahaṃ taṃ, ayye, āsanan”ti? “Venerable, where’s that seat?”

“Nāhaṃ taṃ, āvuso, āsanaṃ passāmī”ti. “I don’t know.”

“Dethāyye, taṃ āsanan”ti paribhāsitvā niccabhattaṃ pacchindiṃsu. “Venerable, give back the seat.” And after scolding her, they made an end of her regular meal. Atha kho te manussā gharaṃ sodhentā taṃ āsanaṃ bhājanantarikāya passitvā taṃ bhikkhuniṃ khamāpetvā niccabhattaṃ paṭṭhapesuṃ. Then, while those people were cleaning the house, they saw that seat in between the vessels. They then asked that nun for forgiveness and restored her regular meal.

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. That nun then told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī purebhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā āsane nisīditvā sāmike anāpucchā pakkamissatī”ti … pe … “How can a nun visit a family before the meal, sit down on a seat, and then depart without telling the lay people?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī purebhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā āsane nisīditvā sāmike anāpucchā pakkamatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave bhikkhunī purebhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā āsane nisīditvā sāmike anāpucchā pakkamissati. How can a nun visit a family before the meal, sit down on a seat, and then depart without telling the lay people? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī purebhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā āsane nisīditvā sāmike anāpucchā pakkameyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun visits families before the meal, sits down on a seat, and then leaves without telling the lay people, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Purebhattaṃ nāma Before the meal:
aruṇuggamanaṃ upādāya yāva majjhanhikā. from dawn until midday.
Kulaṃ nāma Families:
cattāri kulāni— khattiyakulaṃ, brāhmaṇakulaṃ, vessakulaṃ, suddakulaṃ. there are four kinds of families: aristocratic families, brahmin families, merchant families, worker families.
Upasaṅkamitvāti Visits:
tattha gantvā. goes there.
Āsanaṃ nāma Seat:
pallaṅkassa okāso vuccati. a place for sitting cross-legged is what is meant.
Nisīditvāti Sits down:
tasmiṃ nisīditvā. sits down on that seat.
Sāmike anāpucchā pakkameyyāti Leaves without telling the lay people:
yo tasmiṃ kule manusso viññū taṃ anāpucchā anovassakaṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti pācittiyassa. if, without telling a person in that family who understands, she goes beyond the roof-cover of that house, she commits an offense entailing confession. Ajjhokāse upacāraṃ atikkāmentiyā āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is out in the open and she goes beyond the vicinity of the seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Anāpucchite anāpucchitasaññā pakkamati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not told, and she does not perceive that she has, and she leaves, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite vematikā pakkamati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not told, but she is unsure of it, and she leaves, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite āpucchitasaññā pakkamati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not told, but she perceives that she has, and she leaves, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Pallaṅkassa anokāse āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not a place for sitting cross-legged, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite anāpucchitasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has told, but she does not perceive that she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has told, but she is unsure if she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite āpucchitasaññā, anāpatti. If she has told, and she perceives that she has, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: āpucchā gacchati, if she leaves after telling; asaṃhārime, if the seat is not movable; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fifth training rule is finished.
