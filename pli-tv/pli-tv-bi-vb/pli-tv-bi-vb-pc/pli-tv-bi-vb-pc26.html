Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

26. Gaṇacīvarasikkhāpada 26. The training rule on robes for a group

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā upaṭṭhākakulaṃ thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavoca— At that time a family who was supporting the nun Thullanandā said to her, “bhikkhunisaṃghassa, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassāmā”ti. “Venerable, we’re going to give a robe-cloth to the Order of nuns.”

Thullanandā bhikkhunī— She replied, “tumhe bahukiccā bahukaraṇīyā”ti antarāyaṃ akāsi. “You are very busy.” And she created an obstacle for them.

Tena kho pana samayena tassa kulassa gharaṃ ḍayhati. Soon afterwards the house of that family burned down. Te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— They then complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā amhākaṃ deyyadhammaṃ antarāyaṃ karissati. “How can Venerable Thullanandā create an obstacle for our gift? Ubhayenāmha paribāhirā bhogehi ca puññena cā”ti. We are now without both possessions and merit.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā gaṇassa cīvaralābhaṃ antarāyaṃ karissatī”ti … pe … “How can Venerable Thullanandā create an obstacle for a group to obtain robe-cloth?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī gaṇassa cīvaralābhaṃ antarāyaṃ akāsī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī gaṇassa cīvaralābhaṃ antarāyaṃ karissati. How can the nun Thullanandā create an obstacle for a group to obtain robe-cloth? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī gaṇassa cīvaralābhaṃ antarāyaṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun creates an obstacle for a group to obtain a robe-cloth, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Gaṇo nāma A group:
bhikkhunisaṃgho vuccati. the Order of nuns is what is meant.
Cīvaraṃ nāma Robe-cloth:
channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ. one of the six kinds of robe-cloth, but not smaller than what can be transferred.
Antarāyaṃ kareyyāti Creates an obstacle:
“kathaṃ ime cīvaraṃ na dadeyyun”ti antarāyaṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she creates an obstacle, thinking, “What can be done so that these people don’t give robe-cloth?”, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ antarāyaṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she creates an obstacle for another requisite, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Sambahulānaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ vā ekabhikkhuniyā vā anupasampannāya vā cīvaraṃ vā aññaṃ vā parikkhāraṃ antarāyaṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she creates an obstacle for a number of nuns or a single nun or someone who is not fully ordained to obtain robe-cloth or another requisite, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ānisaṃsaṃ dassetvā nivāreti, if she obstructs them after showing them a benefit; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
