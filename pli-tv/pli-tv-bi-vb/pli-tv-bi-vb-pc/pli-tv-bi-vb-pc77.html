77. Sikkhamāna-navuṭṭhāpanasikkhāpada 77. The training rule on not giving the full ordination to trainee nuns

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā sikkhamānā thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ upasaṅkamitvā upasampadaṃ yāci. At that time a trainee nun went to the nun Thullanandā and asked her for the full ordination. Thullanandā bhikkhunī taṃ sikkhamānaṃ— Thullanandā told her, “sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti vatvā, neva vuṭṭhāpeti na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti. “Venerable, if you give me robe-cloth, I will give you the full ordination.” But she neither gave her the full ordination, nor made any effort to get her fully ordained.

Atha kho sā sikkhamānā bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. That trainee nun told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā sikkhamānaṃ— “How could Venerable Thullanandā tell a trainee nun, ‘sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmī’ti vatvā, neva vuṭṭhāpessati na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissatī”ti … pe … ‘Venerable, if you give me robe-cloth, I will give you the full ordination,’ and then neither give her the full ordination, nor make any effort to get her fully ordained?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī sikkhamānaṃ— “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?” ‘sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmī’ti vatvā, neva vuṭṭhāpeti na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karotī”ti?

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī sikkhamānaṃ— How can the nun Thullanandā tell a trainee nun, ‘sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmī’ti vatvā, neva vuṭṭhāpessati na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissati. ‘Venerable, if you give me robe-cloth, I will give you the full ordination,’ and then neither give her the full ordination, nor make any effort to get her fully ordained? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī sikkhamānaṃ—‘sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi, evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmī’ti vatvā, sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva vuṭṭhāpeyya na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun tells a trainee nun, “Venerable, if you give me robe-cloth, I will give you the full ordination,” but she then neither gives her the full ordination nor makes any effort to get her fully ordained, then, if there was no obstruction, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Sikkhamānā nāma A trainee nun:
dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhā. one who has trained for two years in the six rules.
Sace me tvaṃ, ayye, cīvaraṃ dassasi evāhaṃ taṃ vuṭṭhāpessāmīti Venerable, if you give me robe-cloth, I will give you the full ordination:
evāhaṃ taṃ upasampādessāmi. I will give you the full ordination.
Sā pacchā anantarāyikinīti If there was no obstruction:
asati antarāye. if there was no obstacle.
Neva vuṭṭhāpeyyāti Neither gives her the full ordination:
na sayaṃ vuṭṭhāpeyya. does not herself give her the full ordination.
Na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyyāti Nor makes any effort to get her fully ordained:
na aññaṃ āṇāpeyya. does not ask anyone else to give her the full ordination.

“Neva vuṭṭhāpessāmi na vuṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissāmī”ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte āpatti pācittiyassa. If she thinks, “I will neither give her the full ordination nor make any effort to get her fully ordained,” then by the mere fact of giving up her duty, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sati antarāye, if there is an obstacle; pariyesitvā na labhati, if she searches for someone to give her the full ordination, but is unable to find anyone; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
