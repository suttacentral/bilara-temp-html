Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Ārāmavagga The sub-chapter on monasteries

56. Abhikkhukāvāsasikkhāpada 56. The training rule on monasteries without monks

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhuniyo gāmakāvāse vassaṃvuṭṭhā sāvatthiṃ agamaṃsu. At that time a number of nuns who had spent the rainy-season residence in a village monastery went to Sāvatthī. Bhikkhuniyo tā bhikkhuniyo etadavocuṃ— Other nuns asked those nuns, “katthāyyāyo vassaṃvuṭṭhā? “Venerables, where did you spend the rainy-season residence? Kacci ovādo iddho ahosī”ti? We hope the instruction was effective?”

“Natthayye, tattha bhikkhū; “There are no monks there. kuto ovādo iddho bhavissatī”ti. So how could the instruction be effective?”

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasissantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns spend the rainy season in a monastery without monks?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns did that?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasissanti. How can nuns spend the rainy season in a monastery without monks? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vaseyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun spends the rainy season in a monastery without monks, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Abhikkhuko nāma A monastery without monks:
āvāso na sakkā hoti ovādāya vā saṃvāsāya vā gantuṃ. she is not able to go to the instruction or to formal meetings of the community. “Vassaṃ vasissāmī”ti senāsanaṃ paññapeti pānīyaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ upaṭṭhapeti pariveṇaṃ sammajjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she thinks, “I will stay here for the rainy season,” and she prepares a dwelling, sets out drinking water and water for washing, and sweeps the precincts, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Saha aruṇuggamanā āpatti pācittiyassa. At dawn, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: vassupagatā bhikkhū pakkantā vā honti vibbhantā vā kālaṅkatā vā pakkhasaṅkantā vā, if the monks who entered the rainy-season there depart or disrobe or die or join another faction; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
