Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

27. Paṭibāhanasikkhāpada 27. The training rule on blocking

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhunisaṃghassa akālacīvaraṃ uppannaṃ hoti. At that time out-of-season robe-cloth was given to the Order of nuns, Atha kho bhikkhunisaṃgho taṃ cīvaraṃ bhājetukāmo sannipati. and the Order assembled to share it out. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā antevāsiniyo bhikkhuniyo pakkantā honti. Just then the student nuns of the nun Thullanandā were away, Thullanandā bhikkhunī tā bhikkhuniyo etadavoca— and Thullanandā said this to the nuns: “ayye, bhikkhuniyo pakkantā, na tāva cīvaraṃ bhājīyissatī”ti. “Venerables, the nuns are away. The robe-cloth is not to be distributed until they get back.” Cīvaravibhaṅgaṃ paṭibāhi. And she blocked the distribution of robe-cloth. Bhikkhuniyo na tāva cīvaraṃ bhājīyissatīti pakkamiṃsu. Since the robe-cloth was not to be distributed until those nuns returned, the other nuns left. Thullanandā bhikkhunī antevāsinīsu bhikkhunīsu āgatāsu taṃ cīvaraṃ bhājāpesi. When those student nuns returned, Thullanandā distributed that robe-cloth.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā dhammikaṃ cīvaravibhaṅgaṃ paṭibāhissatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā block a legitimate distribution of robe-cloth?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ cīvaravibhaṅgaṃ paṭibāhatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ cīvaravibhaṅgaṃ paṭibāhissati. How can the nun Thullanandā block a legitimate distribution of robe-cloth? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ cīvaravibhaṅgaṃ paṭibāheyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun blocks a legitimate distribution of robe-cloth, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Dhammiko nāma A legitimate distribution of robe-cloth:
cīvaravibhaṅgo samaggo bhikkhunisaṃgho sannipatitvā bhājeti. it is distributed by a complete Order of nuns.
Paṭibāheyyāti Blocks:
kathaṃ imaṃ cīvaraṃ na bhājeyyāti paṭibāhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she blocks it with the thought, “What can be done so that this robe-cloth isn’t distributed?”, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dhammike dhammikasaññā paṭibāhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as legitimate, and she blocks it, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammike vematikā paṭibāhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, and she blocks it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dhammike adhammikasaññā paṭibāhati, anāpatti. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as illegitimate, and she blocks it, there is no offense.

Adhammike dhammikasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as legitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammike vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammike adhammikasaññā, anāpatti. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as illegitimate, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ānisaṃsaṃ dassetvā paṭibāhati, if she blocks it after showing a benefit; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The seventh training rule is finished.
