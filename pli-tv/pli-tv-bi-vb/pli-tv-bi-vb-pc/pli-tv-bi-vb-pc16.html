Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Rattandhakāravagga The sub-chapter on the dark of the night

16. Anāpucchāabhinisīdanasikkhāpada 16. The training rule on sitting down without asking for permission

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandā bhikkhunī pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdatipi abhinipajjatipi. At that time the nun Thullanandā visited families after the meal, and she sat down and lay down on seats without asking the lay people for permission. Manussā thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ hirīyamānā āsane neva abhinisīdanti na abhinipajjanti. Feeling shy because of Thullanandā, people there neither sat down nor lay down. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— They then complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdissatipi abhinipajjissatipī”ti. “How can Venerable Thullanandā visit families after the meal, and then sit down and lie down on seats without asking the lay people for permission?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdissatipi abhinipajjissatipī”ti … pe … “How can Venerable Thullanandā visit families after the meal, and then sit down and lie down on seats without asking the lay people for permission?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdatipi abhinipajjatipī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdissatipi abhinipajjissatipi. How can the nun Thullanandā visit families after the meal, and then sit down and lie down on seats without asking the lay people for permission? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī pacchābhattaṃ kulāni upasaṅkamitvā sāmike anāpucchā āsane abhinisīdeyya vā abhinipajjeyya vā, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun visits families after the meal, and then sits down or lies down on a seat without asking the lay people for permission, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Pacchābhattaṃ nāma After the meal:
majjhanhike vītivatte yāva atthaṅgate sūriye. when the middle of the day has passed, until sunset.
Kulaṃ nāma Families:
cattāri kulāni— khattiyakulaṃ, brāhmaṇakulaṃ, vessakulaṃ, suddakulaṃ. there are four kinds of families: aristocratic families, brahmin families, merchant families, worker families.
Upasaṅkamitvāti Visits:
tattha gantvā. goes there.
Sāmike anāpucchāti Without asking the lay people for permission:
yo tasmiṃ kule manusso sāmiko dātuṃ, taṃ anāpucchā. not having asked permission from a person in that family who has the authority to approve it.
Āsanaṃ nāma Seat:
pallaṅkassa okāso vuccati. a place for sitting cross-legged is what is meant.
Abhinisīdeyyāti Sits down:
tasmiṃ abhinisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she sits down on that seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.
Abhinipajjeyyāti Lies down:
tasmiṃ abhinipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she lies down on that seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Anāpucchite anāpucchitasaññā āsane abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, and she does not perceive that she has, and she sits down or lies down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite vematikā āsane abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she is unsure of it, and she sits down or lies down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite āpucchitasaññā āsane abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she perceives that she has, and she sits down or lies down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Pallaṅkassa anokāse āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not a place for sitting cross-legged, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite anāpucchitasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she does not perceive that she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she is unsure if she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite āpucchitasaññā, anāpatti. If she has asked for permission, and she perceives that she has, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: āpucchā āsane abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, if she sits down or lies down after asking for permission; dhuvapaññatte, if the seat is permanently meant for her; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
