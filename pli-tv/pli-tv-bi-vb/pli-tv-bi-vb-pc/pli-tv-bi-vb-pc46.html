Cittāgāravagga The sub-chapter on pleasure houses

46. Bhojanadānasikkhāpada 46. The training rule on giving food

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandā bhikkhunī naṭānampi naṭakānampi laṅghakānampi sokajjhāyikānampi kumbhathūṇikānampi sahatthā khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ deti— At that time the nun Thullanandā personally gave food to performers, dancers, acrobats, magicians, and drummers, saying, “mayhaṃ parisati vaṇṇaṃ bhāsathā”ti. “Please praise me in public.” Naṭāpi naṭakāpi laṅghakāpi sokajjhāyikāpi kumbhathūṇikāpi thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā parisati vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti— And they did so: “ayyā thullanandā bahussutā bhāṇikā visāradā paṭṭā dhammiṃ kathaṃ kātuṃ; “Venerable Thullanandā is a learned reciter; she’s confident and skilled at giving teachings. detha ayyāya, karotha ayyāyā”ti. Give to her; work for her!”

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā agārikassa sahatthā khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ dassatī”ti … pe … “How can Venerable Thullanandā personally give food to lay people?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī agārikassa sahatthā khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ detī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā does this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī agārikassa sahatthā khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ dassati. How can the nun Thullanandā personally give food to lay people? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī agārikassa vā paribbājakassa vā paribbājikāya vā sahatthā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā dadeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun personally gives staple or non-staple food to a lay person or a male wanderer or a female wanderer, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Agāriko nāma Lay person:
yo koci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati. anyone who lives in a house.
Paribbājako nāma A male wanderer:
bhikkhuñca sāmaṇerañca ṭhapetvā yo koci paribbājakasamāpanno. any wanderer apart from Buddhist monks and novice monks.
Paribbājikā nāma A female wanderer:
bhikkhuniñca sikkhamānañca sāmaṇeriñca ṭhapetvā yā kāci paribbājikasamāpannā. any female wanderer apart from Buddhist nuns, trainee nuns, and novice nuns.
Khādanīyaṃ nāma Non-staple food:
pañca bhojanāni— apart from the five staple foods udakadantaponaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma. and water and tooth-cleaners, the rest is called “non-staple food.”
Bhojanīyaṃ nāma Staple food:
pañca bhojanāni—odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ. there are five kinds of staple food: cooked grain, porridge, flour, fish, and meat.
Dadeyyāti Gives:
kāyena vā kāyapaṭibaddhena vā nissaggiyena vā deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she gives by body or by what is connected to the body or by releasing, she commits an offense entailing confession. Udakadantaponaṃ deti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she gives water or tooth-cleaners, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: dāpeti na deti, if she does not give, but gets it given; upanikkhipitvā deti, if she gives by placing it near the person; bāhirālepaṃ deti, if she gives ointments for external use; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
