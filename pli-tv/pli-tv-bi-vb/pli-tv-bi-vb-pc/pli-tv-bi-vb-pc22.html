Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

22. Udakasāṭikasikkhāpada 22. The training rule on bathing robes

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavatā bhikkhunīnaṃ udakasāṭikā anuññātā hoti. At that time the Master had allowed bathing robes for the nuns. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo— “bhagavatā udakasāṭikā anuññātā”ti appamāṇikāyo udakasāṭikāyo dhāresuṃ. Knowing this, the nuns from the group of six used over-sized bathing robes. Puratopi pacchatopi ākaḍḍhantā āhiṇḍanti. As they were walking about, they were dragging them along, both from the front and from behind.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo appamāṇikāyo udakasāṭikāyo dhāressantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns from the group of six use over-sized bathing robes?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo appamāṇikāyo udakasāṭikāyo dhārentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nuns from the group of six do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo appamāṇikāyo udakasāṭikāyo dhāressanti. How can the nuns from the group of six use over-sized bathing robes? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Udakasāṭikaṃ pana bhikkhuniyā kārayamānāya pamāṇikā kāretabbā. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ—dīghaso catasso vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ dve vidatthiyo. Taṃ atikkāmentiyā chedanakaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun is getting a bathing robe made, it should be made the right size. This is the right size: 1 meter long and 50 centimeters wide. If it exceeds that, it is to be cut down, and she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Udakasāṭikā nāma A bathing robe:
yāya nivatthā nahāyati. wearing it as a sarong, she bathes.
Kārayamānāyāti Is getting […] made:
karontiyā vā kārāpentiyā vā. Pamāṇikā kāretabbā. making it herself or getting someone else to make it, it should be made the right size. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ— dīghaso catasso vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ dve vidatthiyo. This is the right size: 1 meter long and 50 centimeters wide. Taṃ atikkāmetvā karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If she makes one or gets one made that exceeds that, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena chinditvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ. When she obtains it, it is to be cut down, and she is then to confess an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she finishes what she began herself, she commits an offense entailing confession. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she gets others to finish what she began herself, she commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she finishes herself what was begun by others, she commits an offense entailing confession. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she gets others to finish what was begun by others, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she makes one or gets one made for the benefit of someone else, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she obtains one that was made by someone else and then uses it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pamāṇikaṃ karoti, if she makes it the right size; ūnakaṃ karoti, if she makes it less than the right size; aññena kataṃ pamāṇātikkantaṃ paṭilabhitvā chinditvā paribhuñjati, if she obtains one made by another that exceeds the right size and she cuts it down before using it; vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, if she makes a canopy, a carpet, a cloth screen, a mattress, or a pillow; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second training rule is finished.
