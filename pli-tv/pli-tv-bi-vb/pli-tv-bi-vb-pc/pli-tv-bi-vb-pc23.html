Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

23. Cīvarasibbanasikkhāpada 23. The training rule on sewing robes

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarissā bhikkhuniyā mahagghe cīvaradusse cīvaraṃ dukkaṭaṃ hoti dussibbitaṃ. At that time a robe had been badly made and badly sewn from the expensive robe-cloth belonging to a certain nun. Thullanandā bhikkhunī taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavoca— The nun Thullanandā then said to her, “sundaraṃ kho idaṃ te, ayye, cīvaradussaṃ; “Venerable, this robe-cloth is beautiful, cīvarañca kho dukkaṭaṃ dussibbitan”ti. but the robe is badly made and badly sewn.”

“Visibbemi, ayye, sibbissasī”ti? “If I unstitch it, will you sew it?”

“Āmāyye, sibbissāmī”ti. “Yes.”

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī taṃ cīvaraṃ visibbetvā thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā adāsi. Then that nun unstitched the robe and gave it to Thullanandā. Thullanandā bhikkhunī— However, although Thullanandā repeatedly said she would sew it, “sibbissāmi sibbissāmī”ti neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti. she neither sewed it herself, nor did she make any effort to get someone else to sew it.

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. That nun then told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ visibbāpetvā neva sibbissati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā have another nun’s robe unstitched, and then neither sew it herself, nor make any effort to get someone else to sew it?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ visibbāpetvā neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karotī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ visibbāpetvā neva sibbissati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissati. How can the nun Thullanandā have another nun’s robe unstitched, and then neither sew it herself, nor make any effort to get someone else to sew it? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbeyya na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyya, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun, after unstitching another nun’s robe or getting it unstitched, neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Bhikkhuniyāti Another nun’s:
aññāya bhikkhuniyā. a different nun.
Cīvaraṃ nāma Robe:
channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ. one of the six kinds of robe.
Visibbetvāti Unstitching:
sayaṃ visibbetvā. she unstitches it herself.
Visibbāpetvāti Getting […] unstitched:
aññaṃ visibbāpetvā. she gets someone else to unstitch it.
Sā pacchā anantarāyikinīti Unless there was an obstruction:
asati antarāye. there was no obstacle.
Neva sibbeyyāti She neither sews:
na sayaṃ sibbeyya. she does not sew it herself.
Na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyyāti Nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew:
na aññaṃ āṇāpeyya. she does not ask anyone else.
Aññatra catūhapañcāhāti Unless […] it was just a period of four or five days:
ṭhapetvā catūhapañcāhaṃ. except for a period of four or five days.

“Neva sibbissāmi na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissāmī”ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte āpatti pācittiyassa. If she thinks, “I will not sew it, nor make any effort to get someone else to sew it,” then by the mere fact of giving up the duty, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampannāya upasampannasaññā cīvaraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, and she perceives her as such, and she unstitches her robe or gets it unstitched, and then neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya vematikā cīvaraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she is unsure if she is, and she unstitches her robe or gets it unstitched, and then neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya anupasampannasaññā cīvaraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she does not perceive her as such, and she unstitches her robe or gets it unstitched, and then neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she unstitches another requisite or gets it unstitched, and then neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya cīvaraṃ vā aññaṃ vā parikkhāraṃ visibbetvā vā visibbāpetvā vā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva sibbati na sibbāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, aññatra catūhapañcāhā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she unstitches her robe or another requisite, or gets it unstitched, and then neither sews it herself nor makes any effort to get someone else to sew it, then, unless there was an obstruction or it was just a period of four or five days, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anupasampannāya upasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she perceives her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya anupasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she does not perceive her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sati antarāye, if there is an obstacle; pariyesitvā na labhati, if she searches for someone to sew, but does not find anyone; karontī catūhapañcāhaṃ atikkāmeti, if she is doing it, but she goes beyond four or five days; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third training rule is finished.
