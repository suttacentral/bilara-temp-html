Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Chattupāhanavagga The sub-chapter on sunshades and sandals

94. Anāpucchāsikkhāpada 94. The training rule on not asking for permission

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo bhikkhussa purato anāpucchā āsane nisīdanti. At that time the nuns sat down on a seat in front of monks without asking for permission. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo bhikkhussa purato anāpucchā āsane nisīdissantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns sit down on a seat in front of monks without asking for permission?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo bhikkhussa purato anāpucchā āsane nisīdantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo bhikkhussa purato anāpucchā āsane nisīdissanti. How can nuns sit down on a seat in front of monks without asking for permission? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhussa purato anāpucchā āsane nisīdeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun sits down on a seat in front of a monk without asking for permission, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Bhikkhussa puratoti In front of a monk:
upasampannassa purato. in front of one who is fully ordained.
Anāpucchāti Without asking for permission:
anapaloketvā. without having asked for permission.
Āsane nisīdeyyāti Sits down on a seat:
antamaso chamāyapi nisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa. even if she sits down on the ground, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Anāpucchite anāpucchitasaññā āsane nisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, and she perceives that she has not, and she sits down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite vematikā āsane nisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she is unsure if she has, and she sits down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession. Anāpucchite āpucchitasaññā āsane nisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she has not asked for permission, but she perceives that she has, and she sits down on a seat, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Āpucchite anāpucchitasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she perceives that she has not, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she has asked for permission, but she is unsure if she has, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Āpucchite āpucchitasaññā, anāpatti. If she has asked for permission, and she perceives that she has, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: āpucchā āsane nisīdati, if she sits down on the seat after asking for permission; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Ekādasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The eleventh training rule is finished.
