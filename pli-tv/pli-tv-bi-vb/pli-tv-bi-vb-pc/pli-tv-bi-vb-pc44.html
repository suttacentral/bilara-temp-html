Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Cittāgāravagga The sub-chapter on pleasure houses

44. Gihiveyyāvaccasikkhāpada 44. The training rule on providing services for lay people

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo gihiveyyāvaccaṃ karonti. At that time the nuns were providing services for lay people. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo gihiveyyāvaccaṃ karissantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns provide services for lay people?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo gihiveyyāvaccaṃ karontī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo gihiveyyāvaccaṃ karissanti. How can nuns provide services for lay people? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī gihiveyyāvaccaṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun provides services for a lay person, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Gihiveyyāvaccaṃ nāma Services for a lay person:
agārikassa yāguṃ vā bhattaṃ vā khādanīyaṃ vā pacati, sāṭakaṃ vā veṭhanaṃ vā dhovati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she cooks rice-porridge, a meal, or non-staple food for a lay person, or she washes clothes or a turban for them, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: yāgupāne, if it is a rice-porridge drink; saṃghabhatte, if it is a meal for the Order; cetiyapūjāya, if it is to venerate a stupa; attano veyyāvaccakarassa yāguṃ vā bhattaṃ vā khādanīyaṃ vā pacati, sāṭakaṃ vā veṭhanaṃ vā dhovati, if she cooks rice-porridge, a meal, or non-staple food for her own service-provider, or she washes clothes or a turban for them; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Catutthasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fourth training rule is finished.
