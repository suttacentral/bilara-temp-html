Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

30. Kathinuddhārasikkhāpada 30. The training rule on the end of the robe-making season

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarena upāsakena saṃghaṃ uddissa vihāro kārāpito hoti. At that time a lay follower had built a dwelling for the Order. So tassa vihārassa mahe ubhatosaṃghassa akālacīvaraṃ dātukāmo hoti. He wanted to give out-of-season robe-cloth to both Orders at the presentation ceremony for that dwelling. Tena kho pana samayena ubhatosaṃghassa kathinaṃ atthataṃ hoti. Just then both Orders had done the robe-making ceremony. Atha kho so upāsako saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā kathinuddhāraṃ yāci. That lay follower then approached the Order and asked it to make an end of the robe-making season.

Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards the Master gave a teaching and addressed the monks, “anujānāmi, bhikkhave, kathinaṃ uddharituṃ. “Monks, I allow you to end the robe-making season.

Evañca pana bhikkhave kathinaṃ uddharitabbaṃ. And it should be ended in this way. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable monk should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho kathinaṃ uddhareyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should end the robe-making season. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, bhante, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Saṃgho kathinaṃ uddharati. The Order ends the robe-making season. Yassāyasmato khamati kathinassa uddhāro, so tuṇhassa; Any monk who approves of ending the robe-making season should remain silent. yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya. Any monk who does not approve should speak up.

Ubbhataṃ saṃghena kathinaṃ, khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The robe-making season has been ended by the Order. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Atha kho so upāsako bhikkhunisaṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā kathinuddhāraṃ yāci. Then that lay follower went to the Order of nuns and asked them to end the robe-making season. Thullanandā bhikkhunī— The nun Thullanandā, “cīvaraṃ amhākaṃ bhavissatī”ti kathinuddhāraṃ paṭibāhi. aiming to get robe-cloth for herself, blocked the Order from ending the robe-making season. Atha kho so upāsako ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— That lay follower complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo amhākaṃ kathinuddhāraṃ na dassantī”ti. “How could the nuns not end the robe-making season for us?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tassa upāsakassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. The nuns heard the complaints of that lay follower. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā dhammikaṃ kathinuddhāraṃ paṭibāhissatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā block a legitimate ending of the robe-making season?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ kathinuddhāraṃ paṭibāhatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ kathinuddhāraṃ paṭibāhissati. How can the nun Thullanandā block a legitimate ending of the robe-making season? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dhammikaṃ kathinuddhāraṃ paṭibāheyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun blocks a legitimate ending of the robe-making season, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Dhammiko nāma A legitimate ending of the robe-making season:
kathinuddhāro samaggo bhikkhunisaṃgho sannipatitvā uddharati. a complete Order of nuns brings it to an end.
Paṭibāheyyāti Blocks:
“kathaṃ idaṃ kathinaṃ na uddhareyyā”ti paṭibāhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she blocks it, thinking, “What can be done so that the robe-making season doesn’t end?” she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Dhammike dhammikasaññā paṭibāhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as legitimate, and she blocks it, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammike vematikā paṭibāhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, and she blocks it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dhammike adhammikasaññā paṭibāhati, anāpatti. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as illegitimate, and she blocks it, there is no offense.

Adhammike dhammikasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as legitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammike vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammike adhammikasaññā, anāpatti. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as illegitimate, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: ānisaṃsaṃ dassetvā paṭibāhati, if she blocks it after showing a benefit; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The tenth training rule is finished.

Naggavaggo tatiyo. The third chapter on nakedness is finished.
