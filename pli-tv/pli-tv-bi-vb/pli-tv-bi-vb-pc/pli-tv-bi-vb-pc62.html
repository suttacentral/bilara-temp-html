Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Gabbhinivagga The sub-chapter on pregnant women

62. Pāyantisikkhāpada 62. The training rule on breastfeeding

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpenti. At that time the nuns gave the full ordination to a woman who was breastfeeding. Sā piṇḍāya carati. When she walked for alms, Manussā evamāhaṃsu— people said, “dethāyyāya bhikkhaṃ, sadutiyikā ayyā”ti. “Give almsfood to the nun. She has a companion.” Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti. “How can the nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is breastfeeding?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is breastfeeding?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpessanti. How can the nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is breastfeeding? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī pāyantiṃ vuṭṭhāpeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun gives the full ordination to a woman who is breastfeeding, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Pāyantī nāma A woman who is breastfeeding:
mātā vā hoti dhāti vā. she is a mother or a wet-nurse.
Vuṭṭhāpeyyāti Gives the full ordination:
upasampādeyya. gives the full ordination. “Vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti gaṇaṃ vā ācariniṃ vā pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā pariyesati, sīmaṃ vā sammannati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, intending to give the full ordination, she searches for a group, a teacher, a bowl, or a robe, or she establishes a boundary, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. After the motion, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dvīhi kammavācāhi dukkaṭā. After each of the first two announcements, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kammavācāpariyosāne upajjhāyāya āpatti pācittiyassa. When the last announcement is finished, the preceptor commits an offense entailing confession; Gaṇassa ca ācariniyā ca āpatti dukkaṭassa. the group and the teacher commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Permutations

Pāyantiyā pāyantisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she is breastfeeding, and she perceives her as breastfeeding, and she gives her the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Pāyantiyā vematikā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is breastfeeding, but she is unsure if she is, and she gives her the full ordination, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Pāyantiyā apāyantisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, anāpatti. If she is breastfeeding, but she does not perceive her as breastfeeding, and she gives her the full ordination, there is no offense.

Apāyantiyā pāyantisaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not breastfeeding, but she perceives her as breastfeeding, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apāyantiyā vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not breastfeeding, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Apāyantiyā apāyantisaññā, anāpatti. If she is not breastfeeding, and she does not perceive her as breastfeeding, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pāyantiṃ apāyantisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, if she is breastfeeding, but she does not perceive her as breastfeeding, and she gives her the full ordination; apāyantiṃ apāyantisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, if she is not breastfeeding, and she does not perceive her as breastfeeding, and she gives her the full ordination; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second training rule is finished.
