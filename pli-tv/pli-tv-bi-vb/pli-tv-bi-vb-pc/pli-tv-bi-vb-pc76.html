Kumāribhūtavagga The sub-chapter on unmarried girls

76. Khīyanadhammasikkhāpada 76. The training rule on criticizing

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena caṇḍakāḷī bhikkhunī bhikkhunisaṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā vuṭṭhāpanasammutiṃ yācati. At that time the nun Caṇḍakāḷī approached the Order of nuns and asked for approval to give a full ordination. Atha kho bhikkhunisaṃgho caṇḍakāḷiṃ bhikkhuniṃ paricchinditvā— The Order of nuns “alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenā”ti, vuṭṭhāpanasammutiṃ na adāsi. decided that she should not, and so they did not give her the approval. Caṇḍakāḷī bhikkhunī “sādhū”ti paṭissuṇi. Caṇḍakāḷī consented.

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhunisaṃgho aññāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ vuṭṭhāpanasammutiṃ deti. Soon afterwards the Order of nuns gave approval to other nuns to give full ordinations. Caṇḍakāḷī bhikkhunī ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— Caṇḍakāḷī complained and criticized it, “ahameva nūna bālā, ahameva nūna alajjinī; “So it seems I’m ignorant and shameless, yaṃ saṃgho aññāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ vuṭṭhāpanasammutiṃ deti, mayhameva na detī”ti. since the Order gives approval to other nuns to give full ordinations, but not to me.”

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā caṇḍakāḷī— “How can the nun Caṇḍakāḷī ‘alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenā’ti vuccamānā ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjissatī”ti … pe … consent to not being approved to give a full ordination, and then criticize it afterwards?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, caṇḍakāḷī bhikkhunī ‘alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenā’ti vuccamānā ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Caṇḍakāḷī did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, caṇḍakāḷī bhikkhunī— How can the nun Caṇḍakāḷī ‘alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenā’ti vuccamānā ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjissati. consent to not being approved to give a full ordination, and then criticize it afterwards? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī—‘alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenā’ti vuccamānā ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun is told, “Venerable, you have given enough full ordinations for now,” and she consents, saying, “It is well,” but then criticizes it afterwards, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Alaṃ tāva te, ayye, vuṭṭhāpitenāti Venerable, you have given enough full ordinations for now:
alaṃ tāva te, ayye, upasampāditena. Venerable, you have given enough full ordinations for now. “Sādhū”ti paṭissuṇitvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she consents, saying, ‘It is well,’ but then criticizes it afterwards, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: pakatiyā chandā dosā mohā bhayā karontaṃ khiyyati, if she criticizes one who normally acts from desire, ill-will, confusion, or fear; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
