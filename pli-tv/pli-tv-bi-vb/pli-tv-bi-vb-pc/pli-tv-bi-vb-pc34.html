Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Tuvaṭṭavagga The sub-chapter on lying down

34. Naupaṭṭhāpanasikkhāpada 34. The training rule on not getting an attendant

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandā bhikkhunī dukkhitaṃ sahajīviniṃ neva upaṭṭheti na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti. At that time a student of the nun Thullanandā was sick, but she neither attended on her nor made any effort to get someone else to attend on her.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā dukkhitaṃ sahajīviniṃ neva upaṭṭhessati na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā not attend on a sick student, nor get anyone to attend on her?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dukkhitaṃ sahajīviniṃ neva upaṭṭheti na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karotī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā acted like this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī dukkhitaṃ sahajīviniṃ neva upaṭṭhessati na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissati. How can the nun Thullanandā not attend on a sick student, nor get anyone to attend on her? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dukkhitaṃ sahajīviniṃ neva upaṭṭheyya na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun does not attend on a sick student, nor makes any effort to get someone else to attend on her, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Dukkhitā nāma Sick:
gilānā vuccati. ill is what is meant.
Sahajīvinī nāma Student:
saddhivihārinī vuccati. a pupil is what is meant.
Neva upaṭṭheyyāti Does not attend on:
na sayaṃ upaṭṭheyya. does not herself attend on her.
Na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ kareyyāti Nor makes any effort to get someone else to attend on her:
na aññaṃ āṇāpeyya. she does not ask anyone else.

“Neva upaṭṭhessāmi, na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karissāmī”ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte āpatti pācittiyassa. If she thinks, “I will not attend on her, nor will I make any effort to get anyone else to attend on her,” then by the mere fact of giving up her duty, she commits an offense entailing confession. Antevāsiniṃ vā anupasampannaṃ vā neva upaṭṭheti, na upaṭṭhāpanāya ussukkaṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she does not attend, nor makes any effort to get anyone else to attend, on a disciple or one who is not fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sati antarāye, if there is an obstacle; pariyesitvā na labhati, if she searches but does not find an attendant; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Catutthasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fourth training rule is finished.
