Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Tuvaṭṭavagga The sub-chapter on lying down

33. Aphāsukaraṇasikkhāpada 33. The training rule on making uncomfortable

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandā bhikkhunī bahussutā hoti bhāṇikā visāradā paṭṭā dhammiṃ kathaṃ kātuṃ. At that time the nun Thullanandā was a learned reciter, and she was confident and skilled at giving teachings. Bhaddāpi kāpilānī bahussutā hoti bhāṇikā visāradā paṭṭā dhammiṃ kathaṃ kātuṃ uḷārasambhāvitā. Bhaddā Kāpilānī, too, was a learned reciter who was confident and skilled at giving teachings, and she was respected for her excellence. Manussā— “ayyā bhaddā kāpilānī bahussutā bhāṇikā visāradā paṭṭā dhammiṃ kathaṃ kātuṃ uḷārasambhāvitā”ti bhaddaṃ kāpilāniṃ paṭhamaṃ payirupāsitvā, pacchā thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ payirupāsanti. Because of this, people visited Bhaddā Kāpilānī first and then Thullanandā. Thullanandā bhikkhunī issāpakatā— Being jealous, Thullanandā thought, “imā kira appicchā santuṭṭhā pavivittā asaṃsaṭṭhā yā imā saññattibahulā viññattibahulā viharantī”ti bhaddāya kāpilāniyā purato caṅkamatipi tiṭṭhatipi nisīdatipi seyyampi kappeti uddisatipi uddisāpetipi sajjhāyampi karoti. “These ones always ask for things and tell people what to do, and yet they are supposedly contented and have few desires, supposedly secluded and not socializing.” And in front of Bhaddā Kāpilānī she walked back and forth, stood, sat down, and lay down, and she recited, got others to recite, and practiced recitation.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā ayyāya bhaddāya kāpilāniyā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karissatī”ti … pe … “How could Venerable Thullanandā intentionally make Bhaddā Kāpilānī uncomfortable?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhaddāya kāpilāniyā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karotī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhaddāya kāpilāniyā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karissati. How can the nun Thullanandā intentionally make Bhaddā Kāpilānī uncomfortable? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā sañcicca aphāsuṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun intentionally makes another nun uncomfortable, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Bhikkhuniyāti Another nun:
aññāya bhikkhuniyā. a different nun.
Sañciccāti Intentionally:
jānantī sañjānantī cecca abhivitaritvā vītikkamo. knowing, perceiving, having intended, having decided, she transgresses.
Aphāsuṃ kareyyāti— Makes […] uncomfortable:
“iminā imissā aphāsu bhavissatī”ti anāpucchā purato caṅkamati vā tiṭṭhati vā nisīdati vā seyyaṃ vā kappeti uddisati vā uddisāpeti vā sajjhāyaṃ vā karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. if, without asking for permission, but thinking, “In this way she will be uncomfortable,” she walks back and forth in front of her, or she stands, sits down, or lies down in front of her, or she recites, gets others to recite, or practices recitation in front of her, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampannāya upasampannasaññā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, and she perceives her as such, and she intentionally makes her uncomfortable, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya vematikā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she is unsure if she is, and she intentionally makes her uncomfortable, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya anupasampannasaññā sañcicca aphāsuṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she does not perceive her as such, and she intentionally makes her uncomfortable, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannāya sañcicca aphāsuṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she intentionally makes her uncomfortable, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya upasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she perceives her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya anupasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she does not perceive her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: na aphāsuṃ kattukāmā āpucchā purato caṅkamati vā tiṭṭhati vā nisīdati vā seyyaṃ vā kappeti uddisati vā uddisāpeti vā sajjhāyaṃ vā karoti, if, after asking for permission and not desiring to make her uncomfortable, she walks back and forth in front of her, or she stands, sits down, or lies down in front of her, or she recites, gets others to recite, or practices recitation in front of her; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third training rule is finished.
