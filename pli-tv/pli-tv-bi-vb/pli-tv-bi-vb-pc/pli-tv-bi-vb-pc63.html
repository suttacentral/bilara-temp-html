63. Sikkhamānasikkhāpada 63. The training rule on trainee nuns

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpenti. At that time the nuns gave the full ordination to trainee nuns who had not trained for two years in the six rules. Tā bālā honti abyattā na jānanti kappiyaṃ vā akappiyaṃ vā. They were ignorant and incompetent, and they did not know what was allowable and what was not.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns give the full ordination to trainee nuns who have not trained for two years in the six rules?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpessanti. How can the nuns give the full ordination to trainee nuns who have not trained for two years in the six rules? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …” … vigarahitvā … pe … and after criticizing them … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— he gave a teaching and addressed the monks:

“anujānāmi, bhikkhave, sikkhamānāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ dātuṃ. “Monks, a trainee nun should be given approval to train in the six rules for two years. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, dātabbā. And it should be given in this way.

Tāya sikkhamānāya saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā bhikkhunīnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— After approaching the Order of nuns, that trainee nun should put her upper robe over one shoulder and pay respect at the feet of the nuns. She should then squat on her heels, put the palms of her hands together, and say, ‘ahaṃ, ayye, itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya sikkhamānā. ‘Venerables, I, so-and-so, am a trainee nun under Venerable so-and-so. Saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācāmī’ti. I ask the Order for approval to train for two years in the six rules.’ Dutiyampi yācitabbā. And she should ask a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time.

Byattāya bhikkhuniyā paṭibalāya saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable nun should then inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya sikkhamānā saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācati. So-and-so, who is a trainee nun under Venerable so-and-so, is asking the Order for approval to train in the six rules for two years. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ saṃgho itthannāmāya sikkhamānāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ dadeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should give approval to trainee nun so-and-so to train in the six rules for two years. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya sikkhamānā saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācati. So-and-so, who is a trainee nun under Venerable so-and-so, is asking the Order for approval to train in the six rules for two years. Saṃgho itthannāmāya sikkhamānāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ deti. The Order gives approval to trainee nun so-and-so to train in the six rules for two years. Yassā ayyāya khamati itthannāmāya sikkhamānāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiyā dānaṃ, sā tuṇhassa; Any nun who approves of giving approval to trainee nun so-and-so to train in the six rules for two years should remain silent. yassā nakkhamati, sā bhāseyya. Any nun who does not approve should speak up.

Dinnā saṃghena itthannāmāya sikkhamānāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammuti. The Order has given approval to trainee nun so-and-so to train in the six rules for two years. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’

Sā sikkhamānā “evaṃ vadehī”ti vattabbā— That trainee nun should be told to say this: “pāṇātipātā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi. ‘I undertake to abstain from killing living beings for two years without transgression. Adinnādānā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi. I undertake to abstain from stealing for two years without transgression. Abrahmacariyā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi. I undertake to abstain from sexual activity for two years without transgression. Musāvādā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi. I undertake to abstain from lying for two years without transgression. Surāmerayamajjappamādaṭṭhānā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi. I undertake to abstain from alcohol which causes heedlessness for two years without transgression. Vikālabhojanā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmī”ti. I undertake to abstain from eating at the wrong time for two years without transgression.’”

Atha kho bhagavā tā bhikkhuniyo anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya … pe … Then, after criticizing those nuns in many ways for being difficult to support … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— “And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun gives the full ordination to a trainee nun who has not trained in the six rules for two years, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Dve vassānīti Two years:
dve saṃvaccharāni. two years.
Asikkhitasikkhā nāma Who has not trained:
sikkhā vā na dinnā hoti, dinnā vā sikkhā kupitā. the training has not been given to her, or the training has been given to her, but she has failed in it.
Vuṭṭhāpeyyāti Gives the full ordination:
upasampādeyya. gives the full ordination. “Vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti gaṇaṃ vā ācariniṃ vā pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā pariyesati, sīmaṃ vā sammannati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, intending to give the full ordination, she searches for a group, a teacher, a bowl, or a robe, or she establishes a boundary, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. After the motion, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dvīhi kammavācāhi dukkaṭā. After each of the first two announcements, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kammavācāpariyosāne upajjhāyāya āpatti pācittiyassa. When the last announcement is finished, the preceptor commits an offense entailing confession; Gaṇassa ca ācariniyā ca āpatti dukkaṭassa. the group and the teacher commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as legitimate, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematikā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as illegitimate, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as legitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as illegitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhaṃ sikkhamānaṃ vuṭṭhāpeti, if she gives the full ordination to a trainee nun who has trained in the six rules for two years; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The third training rule is finished.
