Theravāda Vinaya Theravāda Collection on Monastic Law

Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Gabbhinivagga The sub-chapter on pregnant women

61. Gabbhinisikkhāpada 61. The training rule on pregnant women

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpenti. At that time the nuns gave the full ordination to a woman who was pregnant. Sā piṇḍāya carati. When she walked for alms, Manussā evamāhaṃsu— people said, “dethāyyāya bhikkhaṃ, garubhārā ayyā”ti. “Give almsfood to the nun. She’s carrying a heavy burden.”

Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti. “How can the nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is pregnant?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is pregnant?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpessanti. How can the nuns give the full ordination to a woman who is pregnant? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī gabbhiniṃ vuṭṭhāpeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun gives the full ordination to a woman who is pregnant, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Gabbhinī nāma A woman who is pregnant:
āpannasattā vuccati. a woman with child.
Vuṭṭhāpeyyāti Gives the full ordination:
upasampādeyya. gives the full ordination. “Vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti gaṇaṃ vā ācariniṃ vā pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā pariyesati, sīmaṃ vā sammannati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, intending to give the full ordination, she searches for a group, a teacher, a bowl, or a robe, or she establishes a boundary, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. After the motion, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dvīhi kammavācāhi dukkaṭā. After each of the first two announcements, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kammavācāpariyosāne upajjhāyāya āpatti pācittiyassa. When the last announcement is finished, the preceptor commits an offense entailing confession; Gaṇassa ca ācariniyā ca āpatti dukkaṭassa. the group and the teacher commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Permutations

Gabbhiniyā gabbhinisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If she is pregnant, and she perceives her as pregnant, and she gives her the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Gabbhiniyā vematikā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is pregnant, but she is unsure if she is, and she gives her the full ordination, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Gabbhiniyā agabbhinisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, anāpatti. If she is pregnant, but she does not perceive her as pregnant, and she gives her the full ordination, there is no offense.

Agabbhiniyā gabbhinisaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not pregnant, but she perceives her as pregnant, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Agabbhiniyā vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she is not pregnant, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Agabbhiniyā agabbhinisaññā, anāpatti. If she is not pregnant, and she does not perceive her as pregnant, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: gabbhiniṃ agabbhinisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, if she is pregnant, but she does not perceive her as pregnant, and she gives her the full ordination; agabbhiniṃ agabbhinisaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, if she is not pregnant, and she does not perceive her as pregnant, and she gives her the full ordination; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The first training rule is finished.
