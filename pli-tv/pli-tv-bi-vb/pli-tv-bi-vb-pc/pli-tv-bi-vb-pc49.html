Cittāgāravagga The sub-chapter on pleasure houses

49. Tiracchānavijjāpariyāpuṇanasikkhāpada 49. The training rule on studying pointless knowledge

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇanti. At that time the nuns from the group of six were studying pointless knowledge. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— People complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇissanti, seyyathāpi gihiniyo kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “How can nuns study pointless knowledge? They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those people, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇissantī”ti … pe … “How can the nuns from the group of six study pointless knowledge?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇantī”ti? “Is it true, monks, those nuns do that?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇissanti. How can the nuns from the group of six study pointless knowledge? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī tiracchānavijjaṃ pariyāpuṇeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun studies pointless knowledge, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Tiracchānavijjā nāma Pointless knowledge:
yaṃ kiñci bāhirakaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ. whatever is external to the Buddha’s Teaching, not connected with what is beneficial.
Pariyāpuṇeyyāti Learns:
padena pariyāpuṇāti, pade pade āpatti pācittiyassa. if she learns by the line, then for every line she commits an offense entailing confession. Akkharāya pariyāpuṇāti, akkharakkharāya āpatti pācittiyassa. If she learns by the syllable, then for every syllable she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: lekhaṃ pariyāpuṇāti, if she learns writing; dhāraṇaṃ pariyāpuṇāti, if she learns to memorize; guttatthāya parittaṃ pariyāpuṇāti, if she learns verses for the purpose of protection; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The ninth training rule is finished.
