Gabbhinivagga The sub-chapter on pregnant women

66. Dutiyagihigatasikkhāpada 66. The second training rule on married girls

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpenti. At that time the nuns gave the full ordination to married girls who were more than twelve years old, but who had not trained for two years in the six rules. Tā bālā honti abyattā na jānanti kappiyaṃ vā akappiyaṃ vā. They were ignorant and incompetent, and they did not know what was allowable and what was not.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpessantī”ti … pe … “How can nuns give the full ordination to married girls who are more than twelve years old, but who have not trained for two years in the six rules?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that nuns do this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpessanti. How can the nuns give the full ordination to married girls who are more than twelve years old, but who have not trained for two years in the six rules? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …” … vigarahitvā dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— and after criticizing them … he gave a teaching and addressed the monks:

“anujānāmi, bhikkhave, paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ dātuṃ. “Monks, I allow giving approval to a married girl who is more than twelve years old to train in the six rules for two years.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, dātabbā. And it is to be given in this way.

Tāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya saṃghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā bhikkhunīnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— After approaching the Order of nuns, that married girl who is more than twelve years old should put her upper robe over one shoulder and pay respect at the feet of the nuns. She should then squat on her heels, put the palms of her hands together, and say,

‘ahaṃ, ayye, itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassā gihigatā saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācāmī’ti. ‘Venerables, I, so-and-so, a married girl who is more than twelve years old, am training under Venerable so-and-so. I ask the Order for approval to train for two years in the six rules.’

Dutiyampi yācitabbā. And she should ask a second Tatiyampi yācitabbā. and a third time.

Byattāya bhikkhuniyā paṭibalāya saṃgho ñāpetabbo— A competent and capable nun should then inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassā gihigatā saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācati. This married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old and training under Venerable so-and-so, is asking the Order for approval to train in the six rules for two years. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ saṃgho itthannāmāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ dadeyya. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should give approval to this married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old, to train in the six rules for two years. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Ayaṃ itthannāmā itthannāmāya ayyāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassā gihigatā saṃghaṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ yācati. This married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old and training under Venerable so-and-so, is asking the Order for approval to train in the six rules for two years. Saṃgho itthannāmāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiṃ deti. The Order gives approval to this married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old, to train in the six rules for two years. Yassā ayyāya khamati itthannāmāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammutiyā dānaṃ, sā tuṇhassa; Any nun who approves of giving approval to this married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old, to train in the six rules for two years should remain silent. yassā nakkhamati, sā bhāseyya. Any nun who does not approve should speak up.

Dinnā saṃghena itthannāmāya paripuṇṇadvādasavassāya gihigatāya dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhāsammuti. The Order has given approval to this married girl so-and-so, who is more than twelve years old, to train in the six rules for two years. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’

Sā paripuṇṇadvādasavassā gihigatā evaṃ vadehīti vattabbā— That married girl, who is more than twelve years old, should be told to say this: “pāṇātipātā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmi … pe … ‘I undertake to abstain from killing living beings for two years without transgression. … vikālabhojanā veramaṇiṃ dve vassāni avītikkamma samādānaṃ samādiyāmī”ti. I undertake to abstain from eating at the wrong time for two years without transgression.’”

Atha kho bhagavā tā bhikkhuniyo anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya … pe … Then, after criticizing those nuns in many ways for being difficult to support … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— “And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu asikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpeyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun gives the full ordination to a married girl who is more than twelve years old, but who has not trained in the six rules for two years, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Paripuṇṇadvādasavassā nāma Who is more than twelve years old:
pattadvādasavassā. who has reached twelve years of age.
Gihigatā nāma A married girl:
purisantaragatā vuccati. one who has gone to the place of a man is what is meant.
Dve vassānīti Two years:
dve saṃvaccharāni. two years.
Asikkhitasikkhā nāma Who has not trained:
sikkhā vā na dinnā hoti, dinnā vā sikkhā kupitā. the training has not been given to her, or the training has been given to her, but she has failed in it.
Vuṭṭhāpeyyāti Gives the full ordination:
upasampādeyya. gives the full ordination. “Vuṭṭhāpessāmī”ti gaṇaṃ vā ācariniṃ vā pattaṃ vā cīvaraṃ vā pariyesati, sīmaṃ vā sammannati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If, intending to give the full ordination, she searches for a group, a teacher, a bowl, or a robe, or she establishes a boundary, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. After the motion, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Dvīhi kammavācāhi dukkaṭā. After each of the first two announcements, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kammavācāpariyosāne upajjhāyāya āpatti pācittiyassa. When the last announcement is finished, the preceptor commits an offense entailing confession; Gaṇassa ca ācariniyā ca āpatti dukkaṭassa. the group and the teacher commit an offense of wrong conduct.

Permutations

Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as legitimate, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme vematikā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If it is a legitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as illegitimate, and she gives the full ordination, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she perceives it as legitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme vematikā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññā vuṭṭhāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure, and she perceives it as illegitimate, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: paripuṇṇadvādasavassaṃ gihigataṃ dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhaṃ vuṭṭhāpeti, if she gives the full ordination to a married girl who is more than twelve years old, and who has trained in the six rules for two years; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
