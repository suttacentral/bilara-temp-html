Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Naggavagga The sub-chapter on nakedness

25. Cīvarasaṅkamanīyasikkhāpada 25. The training rule on borrowed robes

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī piṇḍāya caritvā allacīvaraṃ pattharitvā vihāraṃ pāvisi. At that time a nun, after walking for almsfood, spread out her wet robe and entered her dwelling. Aññatarā bhikkhunī taṃ cīvaraṃ pārupitvā gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. A second nun put on that robe and entered the village for almsfood. Sā nikkhamitvā bhikkhuniyo pucchi— Soon afterwards the first nun came out and asked the nuns, “apāyye, mayhaṃ cīvaraṃ passeyyāthā”ti? “Venerables, have you seen my robe?” Bhikkhuniyo tassā bhikkhuniyā etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. The nuns told her what had happened, Atha kho sā bhikkhunī ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti— and that nun complained and criticized the second nun, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī mayhaṃ cīvaraṃ anāpucchā pārupissatī”ti. “How can a nun put on my robe without asking for permission?”

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. She then told the nuns what had happened, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ anāpucchā pārupissatī”ti … pe … “How can a nun put on another nun’s robe without asking for permission?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ anāpucchā pārupatī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that a nun did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ anāpucchā pārupissati. How can a nun put on another nun’s robe without asking for permission? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ dhāreyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun wears a borrowed robe, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ nāma A borrowed robe:
upasampannāya pañcannaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ tassā vā adinnaṃ taṃ vā anāpucchā nivāseti vā pārupati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. if she wears any of the five robes belonging to a nun who is fully ordained, and it had not been given to her, nor had she asked for permission to use it, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampannāya upasampannasaññā cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ dhāreti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, and she perceives her as such, and she wears a robe belonging to her, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya vematikā cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ dhāreti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she is unsure if she is, and she wears a robe belonging to her, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya anupasampannasaññā cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ dhāreti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she does not perceive her as such, and she wears a robe belonging to her, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannāya cīvarasaṅkamanīyaṃ dhāreti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she wears a robe belonging to her, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya upasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she perceives her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya anupasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she does not perceive her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sā vā deti, if the robe has been given to her; taṃ vā āpucchā nivāseti vā pārupati vā, if she wears it after asking for permission; acchinnacīvarikāya, if her own robe has been stolen; naṭṭhacīvarikāya, if her own robe has been lost; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fifth training rule is finished.
