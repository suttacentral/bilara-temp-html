Bhikkhunivibhaṅga The nuns’ rules and their analysis

Pācittiyakaṇḍa The chapter on offenses entailing confession

Gabbhinivagga The sub-chapter on pregnant women

70. Dutiyasahajīvinīsikkhāpada 70. The second training rule on students

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena thullanandā bhikkhunī sahajīviniṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā neva vūpakāseti na vūpakāsāpeti. At that time, after giving the full ordination to a student, the nun Thullanandā neither took her away nor had her taken away. Sāmiko aggahesi. And her husband got hold of her.

Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā sahajīviniṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā neva vūpakāsessati na vūpakāsāpessati, sāmiko aggahesi. “How can Venerable Thullanandā give the full ordination to a student and then neither take her away nor have her taken away? Her husband got hold of her. Sacāyaṃ bhikkhunī pakkantā assa, na ca sāmiko gaṇheyyā”ti … pe … If this nun had gone away, her husband would not have got hold of her.” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī sahajīviniṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā neva vūpakāseti na vūpakāsāpeti, sāmiko aggahesī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī sahajīviniṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā neva vūpakāsessati na vūpakāsāpessati, sāmiko aggahesi. How can the nun Thullanandā give the full ordination to a student and then neither take her away nor have her taken away, so that her husband got hold of her? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī sahajīviniṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā neva vūpakāseyya na vūpakāsāpeyya antamaso chappañcayojanānipi, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun gives the full ordination to her student, and then neither takes her away nor has her taken away, even just 65 to 80 kilometers, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Sahajīvinī nāma Student:
saddhivihārinī vuccati. pupil is what is meant.
Vuṭṭhāpetvāti Gives the full ordination:
upasampādetvā. gives the full ordination.
Neva vūpakāseyyāti Neither takes her away:
na sayaṃ vūpakāseyya. she does not herself take her away.
Na vūpakāsāpeyyāti Nor has her taken away:
na aññaṃ āṇāpeyya. she does not ask anyone else to take her away. “Neva vūpakāsessāmi na vūpakāsāpessāmi antamaso chappañcayojanānipī”ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte āpatti pācittiyassa. If she thinks, “I will not take her away nor have her taken away, even just 65 to 80 kilometers,” then by the mere fact of giving up the duty, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sati antarāye, if there is an obstacle; pariyesitvā dutiyikaṃ bhikkhuniṃ na labhati, if she searches for a companion nun for her, but cannot find anyone; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The tenth training rule is finished.

Gabbhinivaggo sattamo. The seventh chapter on pregnant women is finished.
