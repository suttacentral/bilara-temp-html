45. Adhikaraṇasikkhāpada 45. The training rule on legal issues

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī thullanandaṃ bhikkhuniṃ upasaṅkamitvā etadavoca— At that time a certain nun went to the nun Thullanandā and said, “ehāyye, imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehī”ti. “Venerable, please come and settle this legal issue.” Thullanandā bhikkhunī— Thullanandā agreed, “sādhū”ti paṭissuṇitvā, neva vūpasameti na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karoti. but then neither settled it, nor made any effort to do so.

Atha kho sā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. That nun told the nuns what had happened. Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā bhikkhuniyā— “How can Venerable Thullanandā ‘ehāyye, imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehī’ti vuccamānā— ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā, neva vūpasamessati na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karissatī”ti … pe … agree to settle a legal issue, but then neither settle it, nor make any effort to do so?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā— “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?” ‘ehāyye, imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehī’ti vuccamānā— ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā, neva vūpasameti na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karotī”ti?

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā— How can the nun Thullanandā ‘ehāyye, imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehī’ti vuccamānā— ‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā, neva vūpasamessati na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karissati. agree to settle a legal issue, but then neither settle it, nor make any effort to do so? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā—‘ehāyye, imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehī’ti vuccamānā—‘sādhū’ti paṭissuṇitvā sā pacchā anantarāyikinī neva vūpasameyya na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ kareyya, pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun consents when asked by a nun to settle a legal issue, but she then neither settles it nor makes any effort to settle it, then, if there was no obstruction, she commits an offense entailing confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Bhikkhuniyāti By a nun:
aññāya bhikkhuniyā. by another nun.
Adhikaraṇaṃ nāma A legal issue:
cattāri adhikaraṇāni— vivādādhikaraṇaṃ, anuvādādhikaraṇaṃ, āpattādhikaraṇaṃ, kiccādhikaraṇaṃ. there are four kinds of legal issues: legal issues due to disputes, legal issues due to accusations, legal issues due to offenses, legal issues due to proceedings.
Ehāyye imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vūpasamehīti To settle a legal issue:
ehāyye imaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ vinicchehi. to decide a legal issue.
Sā pacchā anantarāyikinīti If there was no obstruction:
asati antarāye. there was no obstacle.
Neva vūpasameyyāti She neither settles it:
na sayaṃ vūpasameyya. she does not settle it herself.
Na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ kareyyāti Nor makes any effort to settle it:
na aññaṃ āṇāpeyya. she does ask anyone else to settle it.

“Neva vūpasamessāmi na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karissāmī”ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte, āpatti pācittiyassa. If, thinking, “I will neither settle it nor make any effort to settle it,” then by the mere fact of giving up her duty, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Permutations

Upasampannāya upasampannasaññā adhikaraṇaṃ neva vūpasameti na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, and she perceives her as fully ordained, and she does not settle the legal issue, nor does she make any effort to settle it, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya vematikā adhikaraṇaṃ neva vūpasameti, na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she is unsure if she is, and she does not settle the legal issue, nor does she make any effort to settle it, she commits an offense entailing confession. Upasampannāya anupasampannasaññā adhikaraṇaṃ neva vūpasameti, na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karoti, āpatti pācittiyassa. If the other person is fully ordained, but she does not perceive her as such, and she does not settle the legal issue, nor does she make any effort to settle it, she commits an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannāya adhikaraṇaṃ neva vūpasameti, na vūpasamāya ussukkaṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she does not settle the legal issue, nor does she make any effort to settle it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya upasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she perceives her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, but she is unsure if she is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anupasampannāya anupasampannasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If the other person is not fully ordained, and she does not perceive her as fully ordained, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sati antarāye, if there is an obstacle; pariyesitvā na labhati, if she searches, but is unable to find anyone to settle it; gilānāya, if she is sick; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The fifth training rule is finished.
