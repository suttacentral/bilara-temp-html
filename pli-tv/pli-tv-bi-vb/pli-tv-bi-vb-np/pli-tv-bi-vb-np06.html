6. Saṃghikacetapanasikkhāpada 6. The training rule on exchanging what belongs to the Order

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena upāsakā bhikkhunisaṃghassa cīvaratthāya chandakaṃ saṅgharitvā aññatarassa pāvārikassa ghare parikkhāraṃ nikkhipitvā bhikkhuniyo upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ— At that time, after collecting voluntary contributions with the aim of supplying robes for the Order of nuns, the lay followers stored the requisites in the house of a cloth merchant. They then went to the nuns and said, “amukassa, ayye, pāvārikassa ghare cīvaratthāya parikkhāro nikkhitto, tato cīvaraṃ āharāpetvā bhājethā”ti. “Venerables, requisites to be used for robes are stored in the house of such-and-such a cloth merchant. Please have someone get cloth from that and then share it out.” Bhikkhuniyo tena parikkhārena bhesajjaṃ cetāpetvā paribhuñjiṃsu. But the nuns got tonics in exchange for those requisites, which they then used. Upāsakā jānitvā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— When the lay followers found out about this, they complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpessantī”ti. “When there are requisites belonging to the Order that are designated for a specific purpose, how can the nuns exchange them for something else?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ upāsakānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those lay disciples, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma bhikkhuniyo aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpessantī”ti … pe … “When there are requisites belonging to the Order that are designated for a specific purpose, how can the nuns exchange them for something else?” … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpentī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nuns did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized them, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpessanti. When there are requisites belonging to the Order that are designated for a specific purpose, how can the nuns exchange them for something else? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘When there are requisites belonging to the Order that are designated for a specific purpose, if a nun exchanges them for something else, she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikenāti That are designated for a specific purpose:
aññassatthāya dinnena. it was given for a specific purpose.
Saṃghikenāti Belonging to the Order:
saṃghassa, na gaṇassa, na ekabhikkhuniyā. belonging to the Order, not to a group, not to an individual nun.
Aññaṃ cetāpeyyāti Exchanges them for something else:
yaṃatthāya dinnaṃ, taṃ ṭhapetvā aññaṃ cetāpeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. if, apart from the purpose for which it was given, she gets something else in exchange, then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When she obtains it, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā ekabhikkhuniyā vā. It is to be relinquished to the Order of nuns, a group, or an individual nun. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it’s to be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in Nuns’ Relinquishment 1, {MS.2.2431-2.2435}, with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, ayye, aññadatthikena parikkhārena aññuddisikena saṃghikena aññaṃ cetāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ, imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti. … ‘Venerables, this thing which I got in exchange for requisites belonging to the Order that was designated for another purpose is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Order.’ … … pe … dadeyyā”ti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyun”ti … pe … you should give … ayyāya dammī”ti. ‘I give this back to you.’”

Permutations

Aññadatthike aññadatthikasaññā aññaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is for a specific purpose and she perceives that it is for a specific purpose, and she gets something else in exchanged for it, she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Aññadatthike vematikā aññaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is for a specific purpose, but she is unsure if it is, and she gets something else in exchanged for it, she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Aññadatthike anaññadatthikasaññā aññaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is for a specific purpose, but she does not perceive that it is for a specific purpose, and she gets something else in exchanged for it, she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Nissaṭṭhaṃ paṭilabhitvā yathādāne upanetabbaṃ. When she receives in return what had been relinquished, it is to be used in accordance with the intention of the donors.

Anaññadatthike aññadatthikasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not for a specific purpose, but she perceives that it is for a specific purpose, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anaññadatthike vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is not for a specific purpose, but she is unsure of it, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anaññadatthike anaññadatthikasaññā, anāpatti. If it is not for a specific purpose, and she does not perceive that it is for a specific purpose, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: sesakaṃ upaneti, if she makes use of the remainder; sāmike apaloketvā upaneti, if she makes use of it after getting permission from the owners; āpadāsu, if there are dangers; if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The sixth training rule is finished.
