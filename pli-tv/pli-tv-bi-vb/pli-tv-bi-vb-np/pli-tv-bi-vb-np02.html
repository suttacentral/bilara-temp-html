2. Akālacīvarabhājanasikkhāpada 2. The training rule on the sharing out of out-of-season robe-cloth

Origin story

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhuniyo gāmakāvāse vassaṃvuṭṭhā sāvatthiṃ agamaṃsu vattasampannā iriyāpathasampannā duccoḷā lūkhacīvarā. At that time, after spending the rains residence in a village monastery, a number of nuns were traveling to Sāvatthī. They were perfect in conduct and deportment, but poorly dressed in shabby robes. Upāsakā tā bhikkhuniyo passitvā— Some lay followers who saw them thought, “imā bhikkhuniyo vattasampannā iriyāpathasampannā duccoḷā lūkhacīvarā, imā bhikkhuniyo acchinnā bhavissantī”ti bhikkhunisaṃghassa akālacīvaraṃ adaṃsu. “These nuns are perfect in conduct and deportment, but poorly dressed in shabby robes; they must’ve been robbed,” and they gave out-of-season robe-cloth to the Order of nuns. Thullanandā bhikkhunī— Because they had done the robe-making ceremony, the nun Thullanandā “amhākaṃ kathinaṃ atthataṃ kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpesi. determined it as in-season robe-cloth, and then shared it out. Upāsakā tā bhikkhuniyo passitvā etadavocuṃ— The lay disciples asked those nuns whether “apayyāhi cīvaraṃ laddhan”ti? they had obtained any robe-cloth, “Na mayaṃ, āvuso, cīvaraṃ labhāma. and they replied that they hadn’t Ayyā thullanandā— and told them what had happened. ‘amhākaṃ kathinaṃ atthataṃ kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpesī”ti. Upāsakā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— Those lay disciples then complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā akālacīvaraṃ ‘kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpessatī”ti. “How could Venerable Thullanandā determine out-of-season robe-cloth as ‘in-season,’ and then share it out?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhuniyo tesaṃ upāsakānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. The nuns heard the complaints of those lay followers, Yā tā bhikkhuniyo appicchā … pe … tā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— and the nuns of few desires complained and criticized her, “kathañhi nāma ayyā thullanandā akālacīvaraṃ ‘kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpessatī”ti. “How could Venerable Thullanandā determine out-of-season robe-cloth as ‘in-season,’ and then share it out?” Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Those nuns then told the monks, Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ … pe … who in turn told the Master. … “saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī akālacīvaraṃ ‘kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpetī”ti? “Is it true, monks, that the nun Thullanandā did this?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … The Buddha criticized her, “… kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, thullanandā bhikkhunī akālacīvaraṃ “kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpessati. How can the nun Thullanandā determine out-of-season robe-cloth as ‘in-season,’ and then share it out? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … evañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyo imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddisantu— And, monks, the nuns should recite this training rule thus:

Final ruling

“Yā pana bhikkhunī akālacīvaraṃ ‘kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyan”ti. ‘If a nun determines out-of-season robe-cloth as “in-season,” and then shares it out, she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession.’”

Definitions

Yā panāti A:
yā yādisā … pe … whoever …
bhikkhunīti Nun:
… pe … ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā bhikkhunīti. … The nun who has been given the full ordination by both complete Orders through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and three announcements that is irreversible and fit to stand—this sort of nun is meant in this case.
Akālacīvaraṃ nāma Out-of-season robe-cloth:
anatthate kathine ekādasamāse uppannaṃ, atthate kathine sattamāse uppannaṃ, kālepi ādissa dinnaṃ, etaṃ akālacīvaraṃ nāma. if the robe-making ceremony has not been done, robe-cloth that is given during the eleven months. If the robe-making ceremony has been done, robe-cloth that is given during the seven months. Also, if it is given in the robe season, but the cloth is designated, it is called “out-of-season robe-cloth.”

Akālacīvaraṃ “kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. If she shares it out, after determining out-of-season robe-cloth as “in-season,” then the effort constitutes wrong conduct. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. When the robe-cloth is obtained, it becomes subject to relinquishment. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṃghassa vā gaṇassa vā ekabhikkhuniyā vā. The robe-cloth is to be relinquished to the Order of nuns, a group, or an individual nun.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. “And, monks, it’s to be relinquished in this way. … pe … To be expanded as in Nuns’ Relinquishment 1, {MS.2.2431-2.2435}, with appropriate substitutions.

“idaṃ me, ayye, akālacīvaraṃ ‘kālacīvaran’ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ, imāhaṃ saṃghassa nissajjāmī”ti … pe … … ‘Venerables, this out-of-season robe-cloth which I shared out after determining it as “in-season” is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Order.’ … dadeyyā”ti … pe … the Order should give … dadeyyun”ti … pe … you should give … ayyāya dammī”ti. ‘I give this robe-cloth back to you.’”

Permutations

Akālacīvare akālacīvarasaññā “kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. If it is out-of-season robe-cloth and she perceives it as such, and she shares it out after determining it as “in-season,” she commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. Akālacīvare vematikā “kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is out-of-season robe-cloth, but she is unsure if it is, and she shares it out after determining it as “in-season,” she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Akālacīvare kālacīvarasaññā “kālacīvaran”ti adhiṭṭhahitvā bhājāpeti, anāpatti. If it is out-of-season robe-cloth, but she perceives it as in-season robe-cloth, and she shares it out after determining it as “in-season,” there is no offense.

Kālacīvare akālacīvarasaññā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is in-season robe-cloth, but she perceives it as out-of-season robe-cloth, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kālacīvare vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If it is in-season robe-cloth, but she is unsure if it is, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Kālacīvare kālacīvarasaññā, anāpatti. If it is in-season robe-cloth and she perceives it as such, there is no offense.

Non-offenses

Anāpatti— There is no offense: akālacīvaraṃ kālacīvarasaññā bhājāpeti, if she shares out out-of-season robe-cloth that she perceives as in-season; kālacīvaraṃ kālacīvarasaññā bhājāpeti, if she shares out in-season robe-cloth that she perceives as in-season; ummattikāya, if she is insane; ādikammikāyāti. if she is the first offender.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The second training rule is finished.
