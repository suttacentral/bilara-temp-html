Cūḷavagga The Short Division

Bhikkhunikkhandhaka The chapter on nuns

1. Mahāpajāpatigotamīvatthu The story of Mahāpajāpati Gotamī

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in Banyan Tree Monastery. Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. At this time Mahāpajāpati Gotamī went to the Master, bowed down to him, Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho mahāpajāpati gotamī bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and said, “sādhu, bhante, labheyya mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. “It would be good, Venerable Sir, if women were allowed to go forth in the Teaching and training proclaimed by the Buddha.”

“Alaṃ, gotami, mā te rucci mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjā”ti. “Let it be, Gotami, don’t pursue this.”

Dutiyampi kho … pe … A second time tatiyampi kho mahāpajāpati gotamī bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and a third time she asked “sādhu, bhante, labheyya mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. the same question “Alaṃ, gotami, mā te rucci mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjā”ti. and got the same reply. Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī— She thought, “na bhagavā anujānāti mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti— “The Master doesn’t allow women to go forth,” dukkhī dummanā assumukhī rudamānā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. and sad and tearful she bowed down, circumambulated him with her right side toward him, and left.

Atha kho bhagavā kapilavatthusmiṃ yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena vesālī tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. When the Master had stayed at Kapilavatthu for as long as he liked, he set out wandering toward Vesālī. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena vesālī tadavasari. When he eventually arrived, Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. he stayed in the hall with the peaked roof in the Great Wood.

Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī kese chedāpetvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā sambahulāhi sākiyānīhi saddhiṃ yena vesālī tena pakkāmi. In the meantime Mahāpajāpati had her hair cut off, put on ocher robes, and set out for Vesālī together with a number of Sakyan women. Anupubbena yena vesālī mahāvanaṃ kūṭāgārasālā tenupasaṅkami. When she eventually arrived, she went to the hall with the peaked roof in the Great Wood. Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī sūnehi pādehi rajokiṇṇena gattena dukkhī dummanā assumukhī rudamānā bahidvārakoṭṭhake aṭṭhāsi. And she stood outside the gateway, sad and tearful, covered in dust and her feet swollen.

Addasā kho āyasmā ānando mahāpajāpatiṃ gotamiṃ sūnehi pādehi rajokiṇṇena gattena dukkhiṃ dummanaṃ assumukhiṃ rudamānaṃ bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhitaṃ. Venerable Ānanda saw her there Disvāna mahāpajāpatiṃ gotamiṃ etadavoca— and said to her, “kissa tvaṃ, gotami, sūnehi pādehi rajokiṇṇena gattena dukkhī dummanā assumukhī rudamānā bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhitā”ti? “Why are you standing outside the gateway like this?”

“Tathā hi pana, bhante ānanda, na bhagavā anujānāti mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. “Because, Venerable Ānanda, the Buddha doesn’t allow women to go forth.”

“Tena hi tvaṃ, gotami, muhuttaṃ idheva tāva hohi, yāvāhaṃ bhagavantaṃ yācāmi mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. “Well then, Gotamī, please wait here for a moment, while I ask the Master to allow women to go forth.”

Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Venerable Ānanda went to the Master, bowed, sat down, Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and said, “esā, bhante, mahāpajāpati gotamī sūnehi pādehi rajokiṇṇena gattena dukkhī dummanā assumukhī rudamānā bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhitā— “Venerable Sir, Mahāpajāpati Gotamī is standing outside the gateway, sad and tearful, covered in dust and her feet swollen. She says ‘na bhagavā anujānāti mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan’ti. you will not allow women to go forth. Sādhu, bhante, labheyya mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. It would be good, Venerable Sir, if women were allowed to go forth.”

“Alaṃ, ānanda, mā te rucci mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjā”ti. “Let it be, Ānanda, don’t pursue this.”

Dutiyampi kho … pe … A second time tatiyampi kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and a third time he asked “sādhu, bhante, labheyya mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. the same question “Alaṃ, ānanda, mā te rucci mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjā”ti. and got the same reply.

Atha kho āyasmā ānando— Ānanda thought, “na bhagavā anujānāti mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjaṃ; “The Master doesn’t allow women to go forth. yannūnāhaṃ aññenapi pariyāyena bhagavantaṃ yāceyyaṃ mātugāmassa tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. Let me ask him in another way.” Atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— He then said, “bhabbo nu kho, bhante, mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitvā sotāpattiphalaṃ vā sakadāgāmiphalaṃ vā anāgāmiphalaṃ vā arahattaphalaṃ vā sacchikātun”ti? “If women were allowed to go forth, would they be capable of realizing the fruit of stream-entry, the fruit of once-returning, the fruit of non-returning, and the fruit of perfection?”

“Bhabbo, ānanda, mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitvā sotāpattiphalampi sakadāgāmiphalampi anāgāmiphalampi arahattaphalampi sacchikātun”ti. “Yes, they would.”

“Sace, bhante, bhabbo mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitvā sotāpattiphalampi sakadāgāmiphalampi anāgāmiphalampi arahattaphalampi sacchikātuṃ; “If that is so, Venerable Sir, bahūpakārā, bhante, mahāpajāpati gotamī bhagavato mātucchā āpādikā, posikā, khīrassa dāyikā, bhagavantaṃ janettiyā kālaṅkatāya thaññaṃ pāyesi; and considering that Mahāpajāpati has been very helpful to you—she is your aunt who nurtured you, brought you up, and breastfed you when your own mother died— sādhu, bhante, labheyya mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjan”ti. it would be good if women were allowed to go forth.”

2. Aṭṭhagarudhamma The eight important rules

“Sace, ānanda, mahāpajāpati gotamī aṭṭha garudhamme paṭiggaṇhāti, sāvassā. “Ānanda, if Mahāpajāpati accepts these eight important rules, Hotu upasampadā. that will be her ordination:

  1. Vassasatūpasampannāya bhikkhuniyā tadahupasampannassa bhikkhuno abhivādanaṃ paccuṭṭhānaṃ añjalikammaṃ sāmīcikammaṃ kātabbaṃ. A nun who has been fully ordained for a hundred years should bow down to a monk who was given the full ordination on that very day, and she should stand up for him, put the palms of her hands together for him, and do acts of respect toward him. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (1) This rule is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  2. Na bhikkhuniyā abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasitabbaṃ. A nun should not spend the rainy season in a monastery without monks. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (2) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  3. Anvaddhamāsaṃ bhikkhuniyā bhikkhusaṃghato dve dhammā paccāsīsitabbā— Every half-month a nun should ask the Order of monks for two things: uposathapucchakañca, ovādūpasaṅkamanañca. about the date of the observance day and about going for the instruction. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (3) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  4. Vassaṃvuṭṭhāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṃghe tīhi ṭhānehi pavāretabbaṃ— A nun who has completed the rainy-season residence should invite correction from both Orders in regard to three things: diṭṭhena vā, sutena vā, parisaṅkāya vā. what has been seen, what has been heard, and what has been suspected. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (4) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  5. Garudhammaṃ ajjhāpannāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṃghe pakkhamānattaṃ caritabbaṃ. A nun who has committed a heavy offense must undertake the trial period for a half-month toward both Orders. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (5) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  6. Dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhāya sikkhamānāya ubhatosaṃghe upasampadā pariyesitabbā. A trainee nun who has trained in the six rules for two years may seek for full ordination in both Orders. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (6) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  7. Na bhikkhuniyā kenaci pariyāyena bhikkhu akkositabbo paribhāsitabbo. A nun may not in any way abuse or revile a monk. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (7) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.
  8. Ajjatagge ovaṭo bhikkhunīnaṃ bhikkhūsu vacanapatho, anovaṭo bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīsu vacanapatho. From today onwards, nuns may not correct monks, but monks may correct nuns. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. (8) This rule too is to be honored and respected all one’s life, and is not to be breached.

Sace, ānanda, mahāpajāpati gotamī ime aṭṭha garudhamme paṭiggaṇhāti, sāvassā hotu upasampadā”ti. Ānanda, if Mahāpajāpati accepts these eight important rules, that will be her ordination.”

Atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato santike aṭṭha garudhamme uggahetvā yena mahāpajāpati gotamī tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā mahāpajāpatiṃ gotamiṃ etadavoca— Ānanda, if Mahāpajāpati accepts these eight important rules, that will be her ordination.” “sace kho tvaṃ, gotami, aṭṭha garudhamme paṭiggaṇheyyāsi, sāva te bhavissati upasampadā. “If you accept eight important rules, Gotami, that will be your ordination.”

Vassasatūpasampannāya bhikkhuniyā tadahupasampannassa bhikkhuno abhivādanaṃ paccuṭṭhānaṃ añjalikammaṃ sāmīcikammaṃ kātabbaṃ. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Na bhikkhuniyā abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasitabbaṃ. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Anvaddhamāsaṃ bhikkhuniyā bhikkhusaṃghato dve dhammā paccāsīsitabbā— uposathapucchakañca, ovādūpasaṅkamanañca. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Vassaṃvuṭṭhāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṃghe tīhi ṭhānehi pavāretabbaṃ— diṭṭhena vā, sutena vā, parisaṅkāya vā. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Garudhammaṃ ajjhāpannāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṃghe pakkhamānattaṃ caritabbaṃ. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhāya sikkhamānāya ubhatosaṃghe upasampadā pariyesitabbā. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Na bhikkhuniyā kenaci pariyāyena bhikkhu akkositabbo paribhāsitabbo. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Ajjatagge ovaṭo bhikkhunīnaṃ bhikkhūsu vacanapatho, anovaṭo bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīsu vacanapatho. Ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo.

Sace kho tvaṃ, gotami, ime aṭṭha garudhamme paṭiggaṇheyyāsi, sāva te bhavissati upasampadā”ti. And he told her the rules.

“Seyyathāpi, bhante ānanda, itthī vā puriso vā daharo, yuvā, maṇḍanakajātiko sīsaṃnahāto uppalamālaṃ vā vassikamālaṃ vā atimuttakamālaṃ vā labhitvā ubhohi hatthehi paṭiggahetvā uttamaṅge sirasmiṃ patiṭṭhāpeyya; She replied, “Just as a young woman or man—someone fond of adornments, who had just washed her hair—would receive a garland of lotuses, a garland of sandan flowers, or a garland of jasmine with both hands and place it on her head, evameva kho ahaṃ, bhante, ānanda ime aṭṭha garudhamme paṭiggaṇhāmi yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīye”ti. so too, do I receive these eight important rules, not to be breached for life.”

Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ānanda then went to the Master, bowed, sat down, Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and said, “paṭiggahitā, bhante, mahāpajāpatiyā gotamiyā aṭṭha garudhammā, upasampannā bhagavato mātucchā”ti. “Venerable Sir, Mahāpajāpati has accepted the eight important rules. Your aunt has been ordained.”

“Sace, ānanda, nālabhissa mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjaṃ, ciraṭṭhitikaṃ, ānanda, brahmacariyaṃ abhavissa, vassasahassaṃ saddhammo tiṭṭheyya. “Ānanda, if women had not been allowed to go forth in the Teaching and training proclaimed by the Buddha, the spiritual life would have lasted a long time—the true Teaching would have lasted a thousand years.

Yato ca kho, ānanda, mātugāmo tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito, na dāni, ānanda, brahmacariyaṃ ciraṭṭhitikaṃ bhavissati. “Ānanda, if women had not been allowed to go forth in the Teaching and training proclaimed by the Buddha, the spiritual life would have lasted a long time—the true Teaching would have lasted a thousand years. Pañceva dāni, ānanda, vassasatāni saddhammo ṭhassati. But since women have been allowed to go forth, the spiritual life will not last long—now the true Teaching will only last five hundred years.

Seyyathāpi, ānanda, yāni kānici kulāni bahutthikāni appapurisakāni, tāni suppadhaṃsiyāni honti corehi kumbhathenakehi; Just as families with many women and few men are easily robbed by thieves, evameva kho, ānanda, yasmiṃ dhammavinaye labhati mātugāmo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjaṃ, na taṃ brahmacariyaṃ ciraṭṭhitikaṃ hoti. so too, the spiritual life does not last long in a teaching and training where women are allowed to go forth.

Seyyathāpi, ānanda, sampanne sālikkhette setaṭṭikā nāma rogajāti nipatati, evaṃ taṃ sālikkhettaṃ na ciraṭṭhitikaṃ hoti; Just as a ripe rice-field affected by whiteheads will not last long, evameva kho, ānanda, yasmiṃ dhammavinaye labhati mātugāmo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjaṃ, na taṃ brahmacariyaṃ ciraṭṭhitikaṃ hoti. so too, the spiritual life does not last long in a teaching and training where women are allowed to go forth.

Seyyathāpi, ānanda, sampanne ucchukkhette mañjiṭṭhikā nāma rogajāti nipatati, evaṃ taṃ ucchukkhettaṃ na ciraṭṭhitikaṃ hoti; Just as a ripe field of sugarcane attacked by red rot will not last long, evameva kho, ānanda, yasmiṃ dhammavinaye labhati mātugāmo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjaṃ, na taṃ brahmacariyaṃ ciraṭṭhitikaṃ hoti. so too, the spiritual life does not last long in a teaching and training where women are allowed to go forth.

Seyyathāpi, ānanda, puriso mahato taḷākassa paṭikacceva āḷiṃ bandheyya yāvadeva udakassa anatikkamanāya; Just as a man might, as a safeguard, surround a large pool with an embankment, so that the water will not overflow, evameva kho, ānanda, mayā paṭikacceva bhikkhunīnaṃ aṭṭha garudhammā paññattā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyā”ti. so too, have I, as a safeguard, laid down the eight important rules, not to be breached for life.”

Bhikkhunīnaṃ aṭṭha garudhammā niṭṭhitā. The eight important rules for nuns are finished.

3. Bhikkhunīupasampadānujānana

Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Mahāpajāpati went to the Master, bowed down, Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho mahāpajāpati gotamī bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and said, “kathāhaṃ, bhante, imāsu sākiyānīsu paṭipajjāmī”ti? “Venerable Sir, what should I do with these Sakyan women?” Atha kho bhagavā mahāpajāpatiṃ gotamiṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. The Master then instructed, inspired, and gladdened her with a teaching, Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. after which she bowed down, circumambulated him with her right side toward him, and left. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— Soon afterwards the Master gave a Teaching and addressed the monks:

“anujānāmi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūhi bhikkhuniyo upasampādetun”ti. “I allow you to give the full ordination to nuns.”

Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo mahāpajāpatiṃ gotamiṃ etadavocuṃ— Soon afterwards those nuns said to Mahāpajāpati, “ayyā anupasampannā, mayañcamhā upasampannā; “We are ordained, but you are not, evañhi bhagavatā paññattaṃ bhikkhūhi bhikkhuniyo upasampādetabbā”ti. for the Master has laid down that the monks should give the full ordination to nuns.” Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Mahāpajāpati then went to Venerable Ānanda, bowed down, Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho mahāpajāpati gotamī āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca— and told him “imā maṃ, bhante ānanda, bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu— what the Sakyan women had said. ‘ayyā anupasampannā, mayañcamhā upasampannā; evañhi bhagavatā paññattaṃ bhikkhūhi bhikkhuniyo upasampādetabbā’”ti.

Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ānanda went to the Master, bowed, sat down, Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca— and told him “mahāpajāpati, bhante, gotamī evamāha— what Mahāpajāpati had said. ‘imā maṃ, bhante ānanda, bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu— ayyā anupasampannā, mayañcamhā upasampannā; evañhi bhagavatā paññattaṃ bhikkhūhi bhikkhuniyo upasampādetabbā’”ti.

“Yadaggena, ānanda, mahāpajāpatiyā gotamiyā aṭṭha garudhammā paṭiggahitā, tadeva sā upasampannā”ti. The Master replied, “Mahāpajāpati was ordained from the time she accepted the eight important rules.”

Atha kho mahāpajāpati gotamī yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. On another occasion Mahāpajāpati went to Venerable Ānanda, bowed down, Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho mahāpajāpati gotamī āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca— and said, “ekāhaṃ, bhante ānanda, bhagavantaṃ varaṃ yācāmi. “Venerable Ānanda, I wish to ask the Master for a favor. Sādhu, bhante, bhagavā anujāneyya bhikkhūnañca bhikkhunīnañca yathāvuḍḍhaṃ abhivādanaṃ paccuṭṭhānaṃ añjalikammaṃ sāmīcikamman”ti. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī garudhammaṃ ajjhāpannā hoti mānattacārinī. On one occasion a nun who had committed a heavy offense was undertaking the trial period. Atha kho tassā bhikkhuniyā etadahosi— She thought, “mayā ca na labbhā ekikāya vatthuṃ, aññāya ca bhikkhuniyā na labbhā saha mayā vatthuṃ, kathaṃ nu kho mayā paṭipajjitabban”ti? “I am not allowed to stay by myself and other nuns are not allowed to stay with me. What should I do?” Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, ekaṃ bhikkhuniṃ sammannitvā tassā bhikkhuniyā dutiyaṃ dātuṃ. “You should appoint one nun as a companion to that nun.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbā. And she should be appointed in this way. Paṭhamaṃ bhikkhunī yācitabbā, yācitvā byattāya bhikkhuniyā paṭibalāya saṃgho ñāpetabbo— First a nun should be asked, and then a capable and competent nun should inform the Order:

‘Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. ‘Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Yadi saṃghassa pattakallaṃ, saṃgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuniṃ sammanneyya itthannāmāya bhikkhuniyā dutiyaṃ. If it seems appropriate to the Order, the Order should appoint nun so-and-so as a companion to nun so-and-so. Esā ñatti. This is the motion.

Suṇātu me, ayye, saṃgho. Please, Venerables, I ask the Order to listen. Saṃgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuniṃ sammannati itthannāmāya bhikkhuniyā dutiyaṃ. The Order appoints nun so-and-so as a companion to nun so-and-so. Yassā ayyāya khamati itthannāmāya bhikkhuniyā sammuti itthannāmāya bhikkhuniyā dutiyāya, sā tuṇhassa; Any nun who approves of appointing nun so-and-so as a companion to nun so-and-so should remain silent. yassā nakkhamati, sā bhāseyya. Any nun who does not approve should speak up.

Sammatā saṃghena itthannāmā bhikkhunī itthannāmāya bhikkhuniyā dutiyā. The Order has appointed nun so-and-so as a companion to nun so-and-so. Khamati saṃghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’”ti. The Order approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’”

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī sikkhaṃ paccakkhāya vibbhami. On one occasion a nun verbally renounced the training and disrobed. Sā puna paccāgantvā bhikkhuniyo upasampadaṃ yāci. Later she returned and asked the nuns for the full ordination. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā sikkhāpaccakkhānaṃ; “A nun cannot verbally renounce the training. yadeva sā vibbhantā tadeva sā abhikkhunī”ti. When she disrobes, she is no longer a nun.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī sakāvāsā titthāyatanaṃ saṅkami. On one occasion a nun joined the ascetics of another sect while still wearing her robes. Sā puna paccāgantvā bhikkhuniyo upasampadaṃ yāci. Later she returned and asked the nuns for the full ordination. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Yā sā, bhikkhave, bhikkhunī sakāvāsā titthāyatanaṃ saṅkantā, sā āgatā na upasampādetabbā”ti. “If a nun goes over to the ascetics of another sect while still wearing her robes, then if she returns, she should not be given the full ordination again.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo purisehi abhivādanaṃ, kesacchedanaṃ, nakhacchedanaṃ, vaṇappaṭikammaṃ, kukkuccāyantā na sādiyanti. At that time the nuns, being afraid of wrongdoing, did not consent to men bowing down to them, or to men shaving their heads, cutting their nails, or treating their sores. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, sāditun”ti. “I allow you to consent.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo pallaṅkena nisīdanti paṇhisamphassaṃ sādiyantī. At that time the nuns were sitting cross-legged, enjoying the touch of their heals. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā pallaṅkena nisīditabbaṃ. “A nun should not sit cross-legged. If she does, Yā nisīdeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā”ti. she commits an offense of wrong conduct.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā bhikkhunī gilānā hoti. On one occasion there was a sick nun Tassā vinā pallaṅkena na phāsu hoti. who was not comfortable without sitting cross-legged. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā aḍḍhapallaṅkan”ti. “I allow a nun to sit semi-cross-legged.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo vaccakuṭiyā vaccaṃ karonti. At that time the nuns were defecating in a restroom. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhuniyo tattheva gabbhaṃ pātenti. And the nuns from the group of six performed abortions there. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā vaccakuṭiyā vacco kātabbo. “A nun should not defecate in a restroom. Yā kareyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, heṭṭhā vivaṭe uparipaṭicchanne vaccaṃ kātun”ti. I allow nuns to defecate in a place that is open underneath but concealed on top.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo cuṇṇena nahāyanti. At that time the nuns were bathing with bath powder. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti … pe … People complained and criticized them, seyyathāpi gihinī kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.” Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā cuṇṇena nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe with bath powder. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, kukkusaṃ mattikan”ti. I allow bran and clay.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo vāsitakāya mattikāya nahāyanti. At that time the nuns were bathing with scented clay. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti … pe … People complained and criticized them, seyyathāpi gihinī kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.” Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā vāsitakāya mattikāya nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe with scented clay. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, pakatimattikan”ti. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo jantāghare nahāyantiyo kolāhalaṃ akaṃsu. On one occasion when the nuns were bathing in a sauna, they made a racket. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā jantāghare nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe in a sauna. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā”ti. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo paṭisote nahāyanti dhārāsamphassaṃ sādiyantī. On one occasion the nuns were bathing against the stream, consenting to the touch of the current. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā paṭisote nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe against the stream. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā”ti. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo atitthe nahāyanti. On one occasion the nuns bathed away from a ford. Dhuttā dūsenti. Scoundrels raped them. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā atitthe nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe away from a ford. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā”ti. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhuniyo purisatitthe nahāyanti. On one occasion the nuns bathed at a ford for men. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti … pe … People complained and criticized them, seyyathāpi gihinī kāmabhoginiyo”ti. “They are just like lay people who indulge in sensual pleasures.” Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Na, bhikkhave, bhikkhuniyā purisatitthe nahāyitabbaṃ. “A nun should not bathe at a ford for men. Yā nahāyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If she does, she commits an offense of wrong conduct. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, mahilātitthe nahāyitun”ti. A nun should bathe at a ford for women.”

Tatiyabhāṇavāro niṭṭhito. The third section for recitation is finished.

Bhikkhunikkhandhako dasamo. The tenth chapter on nuns is finished.
