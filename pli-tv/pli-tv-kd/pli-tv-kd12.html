Cūḷavagga The Short Division

Pārivāsikakkhandhaka The chapter on those on probation

1. Pārivāsikavatta The proper conduct of those on probation

Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Tena kho pana samayena pārivāsikā bhikkhū sādiyanti pakatattānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhivādanaṃ, paccuṭṭhānaṃ, añjalikammaṃ, sāmīcikammaṃ, āsanābhihāraṃ, seyyābhihāraṃ, pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, pādakathalikaṃ, pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, nahāne piṭṭhiparikammaṃ. At that time monks on probation consented to regular monks bowing down to them, standing up for them, putting the palms of their hands together for them, doing acts of respect for them, offering them a seat, offering them a bed, offering them water for washing their feet and a foot-stool, offering them a foot-scraper, receiving their bowl and robe, and massaging their backs when bathing. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti— The monks of few desires complained and criticized them, “kathañhi nāma pārivāsikā bhikkhū sādiyissanti pakatattānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhivādanaṃ, paccuṭṭhānaṃ, añjalikammaṃ, sāmīcikammaṃ, āsanābhihāraṃ, seyyābhihāraṃ, pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, pādakathalikaṃ, pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, nahāne piṭṭhiparikamman”ti. “How can monks on probation consent to these things?” Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. The monks told the Master.

Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṃghaṃ sannipātāpetvā bhikkhū paṭipucchi— Soon afterwards he had the Order of monks assembled and questioned them:

“saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, pārivāsikā bhikkhū sādiyanti pakatattānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhivādanaṃ, paccuṭṭhānaṃ, añjalikammaṃ, sāmīcikammaṃ, āsanābhihāraṃ, seyyābhihāraṃ, pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, pādakathalikaṃ, pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, nahāne piṭṭhiparikamman”ti? “Is it true, monks, that monks on probation consent to these things?”

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

Vigarahi buddho bhagavā— The Master criticized them … “ananucchavikaṃ … pe … “It’s not suitable … kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, pārivāsikā bhikkhū sādiyissanti pakatattānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhivādanaṃ, paccuṭṭhānaṃ, añjalikammaṃ, sāmīcikammaṃ, āsanābhihāraṃ, seyyābhihāraṃ, pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, pādakathalikaṃ, pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, nahāne piṭṭhiparikammaṃ. How can monks on probation consent to these things? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya … pe … This will not give rise to confidence in those without it …” … vigarahitvā … pe … After criticizing them … dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi— he gave a teaching and addressed the monks:

“na, bhikkhave, pārivāsikena bhikkhunā sāditabbaṃ “A monk on probation should not consent to:

  • pakatattānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhivādanaṃ, Regular monks bowing down to him.
  • paccuṭṭhānaṃ, Regular monks standing up for him.
  • añjalikammaṃ, Regular monks putting the palms of their hands together for him.
  • sāmīcikammaṃ, Regular monks doing acts of respect for him.
  • āsanābhihāro, Regular monks offering him a seat.
  • seyyābhihāro, Regular monks offering him a bed.
  • pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, Regular monks offering him water for washing his feet and a foot-stool.
  • pādakathalikaṃ, Regular monks offering him a foot-scraper.
  • pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, Regular monks receiving his bowl and robe.
  • nahāne piṭṭhiparikammaṃ. Regular monks massaging his back when he is bathing.

Yo sādiyeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. If he consents to any of these, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, pārivāsikānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ mithu yathāvuḍḍhaṃ abhivādanaṃ, paccuṭṭhānaṃ, añjalikammaṃ, sāmīcikammaṃ, āsanābhihāraṃ, seyyābhihāraṃ, pādodakaṃ pādapīṭhaṃ, pādakathalikaṃ, pattacīvarappaṭiggahaṇaṃ, nahāne piṭṭhiparikammaṃ. Monks on probation should bow down to each other according to seniority, and likewise stand up, put the palms of their hands together, do acts of respect, offer a seat, offer a bed, offer water for washing the feet and a foot-stool, offer a foot-scraper, receive bowl and robe, and massage each other’s backs according to seniority when bathing.

Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, pārivāsikānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pañca yathāvuḍḍhaṃ— Monks on probation should do five things with regular monks according to seniority: uposathaṃ, pavāraṇaṃ, vassikasāṭikaṃ, oṇojanaṃ, bhattaṃ. the observance-day ceremony, the invitation ceremony, the sharing out of rainy-season cloth, meal invitations, and meals. Tena hi, bhikkhave, pārivāsikānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vattaṃ paññapessāmi yathā pārivāsikehi bhikkhūhi vattitabbaṃ.

Pārivāsikena, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā sammā vattitabbaṃ. “I will lay down the proper conduct for a monk on probation. Tatrāyaṃ sammāvattanā— This is the proper conduct:

  1. Na upasampādetabbaṃ, He should not give the full ordination.
  2. na nissayo dātabbo, He should not give support.
  3. na sāmaṇero upaṭṭhāpetabbo, He should not have a novice monk attend on him.
  4. na bhikkhunovādakasammuti sāditabbā, He should not accept being appointed as an instructor of the nuns.
  5. sammatenapi bhikkhuniyo na ovaditabbā. Even if appointed, he should not instruct the nuns.
  6. Yāya āpattiyā saṃghena parivāso dinno hoti sā āpatti na āpajjitabbā, aññā vā tādisikā, tato vā pāpiṭṭhatarā; He should not commit the same offense as the offense for which the Order gave him probation, nor one that is similar or worse.
  7. kammaṃ na garahitabbaṃ, He should not criticize the legal procedure.
  8. kammikā na garahitabbā. He should not criticize those who did the procedure.
  9. Na pakatattassa bhikkhuno uposatho ṭhapetabbo, He should not cancel the observance-day ceremony of a regular monk.
  10. na pavāraṇā ṭhapetabbā, He should not cancel the invitation of a regular monk.
  11. na savacanīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, He should not speak to a regular monk to have him corrected or penalized.
  12. na anuvādo paṭṭhapetabbo, He should not criticize a regular monk.
  13. na okāso kāretabbo, He should not ask a regular monk for permission to accuse him of an offense.
  14. na codetabbo,. He should not accuse a regular monk of an offense.
  15. na sāretabbo, He should not remind a regular monk of an offense.
  16. na bhikkhūhi sampayojetabbaṃ. He should not associate inappropriately with other monks.
  17. Na, bhikkhave, pārivāsikena bhikkhunā pakatattassa bhikkhuno purato gantabbaṃ, He should not walk in front of a regular monk.
  18. na purato nisīditabbaṃ. He should not sit in front of a regular monk.
  19. Yo hoti saṃghassa āsanapariyanto seyyāpariyanto vihārapariyanto so tassa padātabbo. Tena ca so sāditabbo. He should be given the last seat, the last bed, and the last dwelling of the Order and should consent to that.
  20. Na, bhikkhave, pārivāsikena bhikkhunā pakatattena bhikkhunā puresamaṇena vā He should not attend on a regular monk when approaching families.
  21. pacchāsamaṇena vā kulāni upasaṅkamitabbāni, He should not be attended on by a regular monk when approaching families.
  22. na āraññikaṅgaṃ samādātabbaṃ, He should not undertake the practice of staying in the wilderness.
  23. na piṇḍapātikaṅgaṃ samādātabbaṃ, He should not undertake the practice of eating only almsfood.
  24. na ca tappaccayā piṇḍapāto nīharāpetabbo—mā maṃ jāniṃsūti. He should not have someone bring back almsfood for him because he does not want others to know about his status.

He should inform about his status on the invitation day. Sace gilāno hoti, dūtenapi ārocetabbaṃ. If he is sick, he should inform about his status by messenger.

“Tayo kho, upāli, pārivāsikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā. “There are three such things, Upāli: Sahavāso, vippavāso, anārocanā— he stays in the same room as a regular monk; he stays apart from other monks; he does not inform other monks of his status.” ime kho, upāli, tayo pārivāsikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā”ti.

Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ mahābhikkhusaṃgho sannipatito hoti. On one occasion a large Order of monks had assembled at Sāvatthī. Na sakkonti pārivāsikā bhikkhū parivāsaṃ sodhetuṃ. The monks on probation were not able to fulfill their probationary duties. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, parivāsaṃ nikkhipituṃ. “I allow you to set aside the probation.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nikkhipitabbo. And it should be done in this way. Tena pārivāsikena bhikkhunā ekaṃ bhikkhuṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— The monk on probation should approach a monk, put his robe over one shoulder, squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say, ‘parivāsaṃ nikkhipāmī’ti. ‘I set aside the probation,’ Nikkhitto hoti parivāso. ‘Vattaṃ nikkhipāmī’ti. or ‘I set aside the proper conduct.’” Nikkhitto hoti parivāso”.

Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyā bhikkhū tahaṃ tahaṃ pakkamiṃsu. Soon afterwards the monks at Sāvatthī left in various directions. Sakkonti pārivāsikā bhikkhū parivāsaṃ sodhetuṃ. The monks on probation were again able to fulfill their probationary duties. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. They told the Master and he said:

“Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, parivāsaṃ samādiyituṃ. “I allow you to take up the probation.

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samādiyitabbo. And it should be done in this way. Tena pārivāsikena bhikkhunā ekaṃ bhikkhuṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo— The monk on probation should approach a monk, put his robe over one shoulder, squat on his heels, put the palms of his hands together, and say, ‘parivāsaṃ samādiyāmī’ti. ‘I take up the probation,’ Samādinno hoti parivāso. ‘Vattaṃ samādiyāmī’ti. or ‘I take up the proper conduct.’” Samādinno hoti parivāso”.

Pārivāsikavattaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ. The proper conduct of those on probation is finished.

“Saccaṃ, bhagavā”ti. “It’s true, Master.”

  • seyyābhihāro, Regular monks offering him a bed.
